# Tax Elections Library

**Resource file for the Vibe CPA Skills Pack — `tax-elections-library`**

This resource contains 24 commonly-needed tax elections organized by
category. Each entry provides:

- **Authority** — IRC section, Treasury regulation, revenue procedure.
- **Purpose** — what the election does and why a taxpayer makes it.
- **When to file** — statutory or regulatory deadline.
- **Where to file** — filing destination (with return; separate
  service center; both).
- **Eligibility / Mechanics** — who qualifies and how the election
  operates.
- **Sample election language** — fill-in template suitable for
  attachment to a return or separate filing.
- **Notes / Pitfalls** — practitioner observations.

Practitioners should always verify current authority before filing.
Election deadlines are typically strict and usually not subject to
§9100 relief except where specifically provided.

# Part A. Initial-Year and Formation Elections

## A1. §351 Corporate Formation Filing Attachment

**Authority:** IRC §351; Treas. Reg. §1.351-3.

**Purpose.** §351 permits tax-free transfer of property to a
corporation in exchange for stock if the transferor(s) are in
"control" (defined as 80% by vote and value) immediately after the
exchange. Treas. Reg. §1.351-3 requires both transferor and
transferee to attach a statement to their respective tax returns
disclosing details of the transaction.

**When to file.** Attach to the timely-filed (with extensions)
return for the tax year of the transaction. One copy attached to
the transferor's return; one copy attached to the transferee
corporation's return. One statement per transferor.

**Where to file.** With the federal income tax return of each
party.

**Sample election language:**

```
SECTION 351 FILING ATTACHMENT
(Attach 1 copy per transferor to both transferor and
transferee returns)

Transferor:                          Transferee:
Name:    _______________________     Name:    _______________________
Address: _______________________     Address: _______________________
Address: _______________________     Address: _______________________
City:    _______________________     City:    _______________________
State:   _______________________     State:   _______________________
Zip:     _______________________     Zip:     _______________________
FEIN:    _______________________     FEIN:    _______________________

Transfer Date: __________________

TRANSFEROR DISCLOSURES — Property Transferred:
  Description                                     Basis
  _______________________________________     ________
  _______________________________________     ________
  _______________________________________     ________
  _______________________________________     ________
  Total                                        ________

Stock Transferred:
  Number of Shares           ________
  Common or Preferred        ________
  FMV Per Share              ________
  Par Value                  ________

Debt Securities Transferred:
  Principal                  ________
  Terms                      ________

Money Transferred:
  Amount                     ________

Other Property Transferred:
  Description                ________
  FMV                        ________

  Description                ________
  Amount                     ________
  Terms                      ________

TRANSFEREE DISCLOSURES — Property Received:
  Description                                     Basis
  _______________________________________     ________
  _______________________________________     ________
  _______________________________________     ________
  Total                                        ________

Capital Stock:
  Common stock issued prior to exchange:    ________
  Common stock issued after exchange:       ________
  Common stock outstanding prior:           ________
  Common stock outstanding after:           ________
  Classes of stock:                         ________

Transferor Information:
  Shares held prior to exchange:            ________
  Shares issued in exchange:                ________
  Shares held after exchange:               ________
  FMV of shares issued:                     ________
```

**Notes / Pitfalls.**

- "Control" is tested *immediately after* the exchange. Pre-arranged
  share dispositions may break control.
- Boot received (cash + non-stock property + assumed liabilities in
  excess of basis) triggers gain recognition under §351(b).
- Liabilities assumed in excess of aggregate basis of property
  transferred = gain under §357(c).
- Service contributions are NOT property and are taxable as
  compensation under §351(d).
- Disclosure failure does not invalidate the §351 treatment but is
  required by regulation; accuracy-related penalties may apply for
  inadequate disclosure.

## A2. LLC Late C Corp Election Relief (Rev. Proc. 2009-41)

**Authority:** Rev. Proc. 2009-41; Form 8832 (Entity Classification
Election).

**Purpose.** Provides streamlined relief for an eligible entity (LLC)
that intended to be classified as a corporation but failed to file
Form 8832 timely. Avoids the need for a private letter ruling under
§9100 relief.

**Eligibility.** All four conditions must be satisfied:

1. **Failure type.** The entity failed to obtain its desired
   classification because either:
   - An initial classification of a newly-formed entity failed
     because Form 8832 was not timely filed; OR
   - A requested change in classification failed solely because
     Form 8832 was not timely filed.

2. **Tax return filings.** Either:
   - The entity has not filed a federal tax or information return
     for the first year in which the election was intended because
     the due date has not passed; OR
   - The entity timely filed all required federal tax and information
     returns consistent with its requested classification for all
     intended-effective years, and no inconsistent returns have been
     filed during any of the taxable years.

3. **Reasonable cause.** The entity has reasonable cause for its
   failure to timely make the check-the-box election.

4. **Time limit.** Three years and 75 days from the requested
   effective date have not passed.

**When to file.** Within 3 years and 75 days of the requested
effective date.

**Where to file.** Form 8832 with the IRS service center, marked at
the top "FILED PURSUANT TO REV. PROC. 2009-41." Reasonable cause
statement attached.

**Mechanics.**

1. Complete Form 8832 with the original requested effective date.
2. At the top of the form, write "FILED PURSUANT TO REV. PROC.
   2009-41."
3. Attach a statement signed by an officer/owner explaining
   reasonable cause for the late filing.
4. Send to the IRS service center where the entity files (or would
   have filed) its return.
5. Attach a copy of Form 8832 with the entity's first return for
   the year the election is to be effective (or the most recent
   return if returns have already been filed).

**Notes / Pitfalls.**

- Rev. Proc. 2009-41 is the streamlined alternative to §9100 relief
  via PLR. PLR fees can run $30,000+; Rev. Proc. 2009-41 is free.
- Track the 3-years-and-75-days clock carefully. Once expired, only
  §9100 PLR relief remains.
- Distinct from Rev. Proc. 2013-30 (S corp late election relief) —
  see A3.

## A3. S Corp Late Election Relief (Rev. Proc. 2013-30)

**Authority:** Rev. Proc. 2013-30; IRC §1362(b)(5); Form 2553.

**Purpose.** Provides streamlined relief for late S corp elections
without need for private letter ruling. Consolidates earlier relief
procedures (Rev. Procs. 97-48, 2003-43, 2004-48, 2007-62) into a
single procedure.

**Required documentation.** A late S election relief filing under
Rev. Proc. 2013-30 must include:

1. **A newly completed Form 2553** with the original requested
   effective date entered on Line E.
2. **Note at the top of the new Form 2553:** "FILED PURSUANT TO
   REV. PROC. 2013-30."
3. **Shareholder signatures.** All persons who were shareholders
   from the original requested effective date until the late filing
   date must sign the new Form 2553.
4. **Individual shareholder statements.** Each shareholder must
   complete and sign, individually, the Part I Box K statement (or
   on the separate statement provided in Form 2553 instructions),
   covering the period from the requested effective date.
5. **Reasonable cause statement.** An officer of the corporation
   prepares and signs a "reasonable cause" statement in Box H of
   page 1 of Form 2553.
6. **For LLCs.** Also file Form 8832 (Entity Classification Election)
   contemporaneously, requesting the same effective date.

**When to file.** Within 3 years and 75 days of the requested
effective date.

**Where to file.**

- Send 1 copy of Form 2553 (and Form 8832 if required) directly to
  the IRS service center.
- Send a 2nd copy attached to the corporation's return for the year
  the S election is to be effective (or the most recent return if
  prior years have been filed).

**Notes / Pitfalls.**

- All shareholders during the entire intended-S-election period must
  consent. Missing consents = relief denied.
- Reasonable cause is fact-intensive. Examples that work: reliance
  on competent tax professional who failed to file; entity-level
  illness/death; loss of records due to disaster. Examples that fail:
  taxpayer was unaware of S election requirement.
- Entity must have filed all returns consistent with S corp
  treatment from the intended effective date forward.
- 3-year-and-75-day window is strict — past that, only PLR §9100
  relief remains.

# Part B. Individual Elections

## B1. §83(b) Election (Restricted Stock / Property for Services)

**Authority:** IRC §83(b); Treas. Reg. §1.83-2.

**Purpose.** Allows a recipient of restricted property (typically
restricted stock) for services to elect to recognize ordinary income
in the year of transfer based on FMV at transfer (less amount paid),
rather than waiting until the property vests. After §83(b), all
post-transfer appreciation is capital gain; without §83(b),
appreciation through vesting is ordinary income.

**When to file.** **Within 30 days of the date of property transfer.**
The 30-day deadline is jurisdictional and cannot be extended. Late
filing voids the election.

**Where to file.**

- Mail the original election to the IRS service center where the
  taxpayer files Form 1040.
- Provide a copy to the person for whom the services were performed
  (typically the employer).
- Attach a copy to the Form 1040 for the year of transfer.

**Sample election language:**

```
SECTION 83(b) ELECTION

The undersigned taxpayer hereby elects, pursuant to § 83(b) of the
Internal Revenue Code of 1986, as amended, to include in gross
income as compensation for services the excess (if any) of the fair
market value of the shares described below over the amount paid for
those shares.

1. The name, taxpayer identification number, address of the
   undersigned, and the taxable year for which this election is
   being made are:

   TAXPAYER'S NAME:  ___________________________________________
   TAXPAYER'S TIN:   ___________________________________________
   ADDRESS:          ___________________________________________
   TAXABLE YEAR:     Calendar Year 20__

2. The property which is the subject of this election is _________
   shares of common stock of _______________________.

3. The property was transferred to the undersigned on [DATE].

4. The property is subject to the following restrictions:
   [Describe applicable restrictions here.]

5. The fair market value of the property at the time of transfer
   (determined without regard to any restriction other than a
   non-lapse restriction as defined in § 1.83-3(h) of the Income
   Tax Regulations) is:
   $______ per share × ________ shares = $___________.

6. For the property transferred, the undersigned paid:
   $______ per share × ________ shares = $______________.

7. The amount to include in gross income is $______________.
   [Item 5 minus Item 6.]

The undersigned taxpayer will file this election with the Internal
Revenue Service office with which taxpayer files his or her annual
income tax return not later than 30 days after the date of transfer
of the property. A copy of the election also will be furnished to
the person for whom the services were performed. Additionally, the
undersigned will include a copy of the election with his or her
income tax return for the taxable year in which the property is
transferred. The undersigned is the person performing the services
in connection with which the property was transferred.

Dated: _________________

Taxpayer: _____________________________________
```

**Notes / Pitfalls.**

- **30-day deadline is absolute.** No §9100 relief.
- File via certified mail with return receipt for proof of timely
  filing.
- Election is **irrevocable** without IRS consent.
- If property is later forfeited (e.g., employee leaves before
  vesting), no deduction is allowed for the §83(b) tax paid on the
  forfeited shares — taxpayer loses the tax.
- Best for situations where: (a) FMV at transfer is low, (b) taxpayer
  is confident of vesting, and (c) significant appreciation expected.
- For ISOs, §83(b) is not necessary (statutory option treatment
  applies); for NSOs and restricted stock, §83(b) is the principal
  planning lever.

## B2. 10-T Election (Home Equity Debt Treated as Trade or Business)

**Authority:** Notice 89-35; "10-T" is the practitioner's shorthand
for the trace-from-home-equity-debt election under interest tracing
rules in Temp. Reg. §1.163-8T.

**Purpose.** Allows a taxpayer to treat interest on home equity debt
as trade or business interest (instead of investment, personal, or
home mortgage interest) when the debt proceeds are used in a trade
or business. This reclassification permits the interest to be
deducted against business income (typically on Schedule C, E, or F)
rather than being subject to the home mortgage interest limits or
the §163(d) investment interest limit.

**When to file.** Attach to the **initial-election-year return only**.
Once the election is made, no further annual attachment is required
unless the use of debt proceeds changes.

**Where to file.** With the Form 1040 for the year of election.

**Sample election language:**

```
10-T ELECTION
(Attach to Initial Election Year Return Only)

Taxpayer: ________________________________________________________
Form: 1040
FEIN/SSN: ______________________________
Tax Year: ______________________________

The above taxpayer hereby elects to treat $____________ of home
equity debt as trade or business debt. The proceeds of this home
equity debt were utilized in the trade or business of the above
taxpayer. During the above tax year, the taxpayer incurred and paid
$___________ of interest which was deducted on Schedule ___,
Line ___.
```

**Notes / Pitfalls.**

- **TCJA limitation.** Beginning 2018, §163(h)(3)(F) generally
  suspended deduction of home equity debt interest as an itemized
  deduction except where proceeds are used to buy/build/substantially
  improve the residence securing the loan. The 10-T election preserves
  deduction for trade-or-business uses.
- Document the actual use of proceeds (deposit-to-disbursement
  trace) per Temp. Reg. §1.163-8T.
- Keep records of the trace for as long as the debt remains
  outstanding — IRS may challenge the characterization in any open
  year.
- The 30-day deposit rule under Reg. §1.163-8T(c)(4): an expenditure
  from any account, within 30 days of depositing debt proceeds into
  that account, is treated as a disbursement of those proceeds.

# Part C. Property and Capitalization Elections

## C1. §266 Property Tax / Carrying-Cost Capitalization

**Authority:** IRC §266; Treas. Reg. §1.266-1.

**Purpose.** Permits taxpayer to elect to capitalize (rather than
deduct) property taxes, mortgage interest, and other carrying costs
on unimproved/unproductive real property, real property under
construction, or personal property in installation. The election is
typically used when the deduction would otherwise be lost (e.g.,
taxpayer in low/no income year, or property not yet income-producing
so deduction has limited current benefit). Capitalized costs increase
basis.

**When to file.** Annual election; attach statement to the timely-filed
return (with extensions) for the year of the capitalization. Election
is **made annually** — capitalization in one year does not bind future
years.

**Where to file.** With Form 1040 / 1120 / 1065 / 1120-S.

**Sample election language:**

```
IRC SECTION 266 ELECTION TO CAPITALIZE CARRYING COSTS

Taxpayer: ____________________________
FEIN/SSN: ____________________________
Tax Year: ______

For tax year ______ the taxpayer hereby elects under Internal
Revenue Code Section 266 and related Regulations at 1.266-1 to
capitalize rather than deduct property tax paid, interest, and
other carrying costs during the year on the following property:

Property Description                                    Amount
________________________________________            __________
________________________________________            __________
________________________________________            __________
________________________________________            __________
```

**Notes / Pitfalls.**

- Three categories of costs may be capitalized: **(1) taxes**,
  **(2) interest** (must satisfy §163(d) or §163(h) deduction tests
  separately), and **(3) other carrying charges** (insurance,
  maintenance, etc.).
- Election can be made for **any one, two, or all three** categories
  per year. Categories selected are independent.
- Must be elected for **each year separately**. No automatic
  carryforward.
- Property must be one of the qualifying types: unimproved /
  unproductive real property; real property under construction;
  personal property in installation phase.
- Once a property becomes income-producing, the election is no
  longer available for that property.
- Useful interaction with §263A UNICAP for builders/developers, but
  do not duplicate capitalization.

## C2. De Minimis Safe Harbor (Reg. §1.263(a)-1(f))

**Authority:** Treas. Reg. §1.263(a)-1(f); the tangible property
regulations finalized in T.D. 9636 (2013).

**Purpose.** Provides a safe harbor under which a taxpayer can deduct
amounts paid for tangible property (rather than capitalize) if the
amount per item or per invoice does not exceed:

- **$5,000** for taxpayers with an Applicable Financial Statement
  (AFS), defined in Reg. §1.263(a)-1(f)(4).
- **$2,500** for taxpayers without an AFS.

**Effect.** Removes the capitalize-vs-expense judgment for items
under threshold. Eliminates §263A UNICAP issues for those items.
Eliminates depreciation tracking and recapture.

**When to file.** Annual election; attach statement to the timely-filed
return (with extensions) for each year the election is to apply.
Must be made annually; not automatic.

**Where to file.** With Form 1040 / 1120 / 1065 / 1120-S.

**Required policy.** The taxpayer must have a written accounting
procedure in place at the **beginning** of the tax year that:

1. Treats amounts paid for property costing less than the applicable
   threshold (or having an economic useful life of 12 months or less)
   as expenses for non-tax (i.e., book) purposes.
2. Is followed consistently for both book and tax purposes.

**Sample written accounting policy (must be adopted before year start):**

```
[ENTITY NAME], address, FEIN, hereby adopts for book and Federal
income tax purposes the following policy regarding capitalization
expenses for the year beginning January 1, 20__.

In accordance with Internal Revenue Code Sections 167 and 168 and
related Regulations at 1.263(a)-1(f), [ENTITY NAME] has determined
that amounts whose individual cost (including tax, installation,
and delivery costs) does not exceed $2,500 [or $5,000 for AFS
taxpayers] will be deducted as incurred as an operating expense.
Amounts exceeding this dollar limit will be examined individually
to determine if their use or purpose requires capitalization under
the betterment, adaptation, or restoration rules used by the
Internal Revenue Service and will be capitalized or expensed as
incurred as a result of the application of those rules.

Date adopted: _______________
Signed: _____________________
Title:  _____________________
```

**Sample election attachment to return:**

```
DE MINIMIS SAFE HARBOR ELECTION
Treas. Reg. § 1.263(a)-1(f)

Taxpayer:       __________________________________________
FEIN/SSN:       __________________________________________
Tax Year:       __________________________________________

The taxpayer hereby elects under Treas. Reg. § 1.263(a)-1(f) to
apply the de minimis safe harbor for the above tax year.

The taxpayer's threshold under the safe harbor is:
   ☐ $5,000 per item (AFS taxpayer)
   ☐ $2,500 per item (non-AFS taxpayer)

The taxpayer has had a written accounting procedure in place since
the beginning of the tax year that treats amounts paid for property
under the threshold as expenses for non-tax purposes. The taxpayer
treats such amounts consistently as expenses for both book and tax
purposes.
```

**Notes / Pitfalls.**

- Threshold applies **per item** (or per invoice if invoice itemizes
  multiple items, then per item on invoice). Cannot aggregate
  multiple items to stay under threshold.
- Must have written policy **before** year begins. Mid-year adoption
  of policy does not qualify the prior portion of the year.
- AFS = audited financial statement, SEC-filed financial statement,
  or a financial statement required by federal/state government
  agency other than the IRS. Reviewed financial statement does NOT
  qualify as AFS.
- Election is **annual**; must be made each year on a timely-filed
  return (with extensions).
- Items deducted under safe harbor are not subject to §263A UNICAP
  (Reg. §1.263A-1(b)(13)).
- Deducted items still must be ordinary and necessary §162 business
  expenses.
- Different from §179 expensing — de minimis is automatic if elected;
  §179 has annual limits and income limitations.

## C3. §179 Qualifying Property Reference (2025)

**Authority:** IRC §179; Treas. Reg. §1.179-1 through §1.179-6.

**2025 limits.**

- **§179 expense limit:** $2,500,000 (subject to verification under
  current law / OBBBA).
- **Phase-out threshold:** $4,000,000.
- **SUV limit (§179(b)(5)):** Verify current-year SUV cap.

**Qualifying property — tangible personal property in 3-10 year asset
class lives**, with the following exceptions and additions:

**Excluded from §179:**

- Air conditioning and heating systems (when not classified as
  qualifying improvement property — see below).
- Property purchased from related parties (per §179(d)(2)).
- Property used predominantly outside the United States.
- Orchards, groves, and citrus trees.
- Residential rental property and its personal property.

**Specifically included in §179:**

- **Single-purpose agricultural structures**, drainage tile, and
  agricultural fences.
- **Property contained in or attached to a building** (other than
  structural components), such as refrigerators, grocery store
  counters, office equipment, printing presses, testing equipment,
  and signs.
- **Gasoline storage tanks and pumps** at retail service stations.
- **Livestock**, including horses, cattle, hogs, sheep, goats, mink,
  and other furbearing animals.
- **Property used predominantly to furnish lodging** (lodging
  property exclusion eliminated by 2017 Tax Cuts Act). IRC
  §50(b)(2) includes any facility where sleeping accommodations are
  rented.

**Improvements to nonresidential real property** placed in service
**after** the date the building was originally placed in service
(2017 Tax Cuts Act addition):

- **Qualified improvement property** (interior improvements to
  nonresidential real property; excludes enlargements, internal
  structural framework, elevators/escalators).
- **Commercial roofs.**
- **Heating, ventilation, and air-conditioning (HVAC) property.**
- **Fire protection and alarm systems.**
- **Security systems.**

**Notes / Pitfalls.**

- §179 cannot create or increase a Schedule C / Schedule F / K-1
  active business loss — limited to taxable income from active
  trades or businesses (§179(b)(3)). Excess carries forward.
- §179 reduces basis for §168(k) bonus calculation; bonus applies to
  remaining basis.
- §179 election is per-asset on Form 4562; can elect for some assets
  and not others.
- State decoupling is widespread — track separately.
- For light passenger autos, §280F luxury auto cap interacts with
  §179.
- For heavy SUVs (>6,000 lbs GVWR), §179(b)(5) imposes a separate
  SUV-specific cap (verify current-year amount).
- OBBBA (2025) — verify final §179 limits under Pub L 119-21.

# Part D. Passive Activity (§469) Elections

## D1. §469 Activity Grouping Election (Reg. §1.469-4)

**Authority:** Treas. Reg. §1.469-4; Rev. Proc. 2010-13.

**Purpose.** Permits a taxpayer to group two or more trade-or-business
activities or rental activities together as a single activity for
§469 passive activity purposes if they constitute an "appropriate
economic unit." Grouping affects:

1. **Material participation.** A taxpayer can satisfy material
   participation across grouped activities collectively rather than
   for each separately.
2. **Loss release on disposition.** Suspended passive losses
   released only on full disposition of grouped activity.
3. **Self-rental rule (Reg. §1.469-2(f)(6)).** Grouping interaction
   with self-rental.

**When to file.** New or modified groupings made on or after
**1/25/2010** must be disclosed on a written statement attached to
the return for the year of formation, addition, regrouping, or
disposition. Failure to disclose creates each activity as separate
unless reasonable cause is shown.

**Where to file.** With Form 1040 / 1120-S / 1065.

**Annual disclosure required for:**

1. **New grouping** — first year a grouping is established.
2. **Addition to grouping** — when an activity is added.
3. **Regrouping** — when a grouping is modified.
4. **Disposition** — when an activity is disposed.

**No annual disclosure required if** the existing grouping is
unchanged from the prior year.

**Sample election language:**

```
GROUPING ELECTION FOR NEW OR MODIFIED ACTIVITIES

Taxpayer:           ___________________________________________
FEIN/SSN:           ___________________________________________
Tax Year:           ___________________________________________

In accordance with Treas. Reg. §1.469-4, the taxpayer hereby
states that the grouped activities listed below constitute an
appropriate economic unit for the measurement of gain or loss for
purposes of section 469.

Activity 1:
  Name / Address / EIN: _______________________________________
Activity 2:
  Name / Address / EIN: _______________________________________
Activity 3:
  Name / Address / EIN: _______________________________________
Activity 4:
  Name / Address / EIN: _______________________________________

Type of statement:
   ☐ New grouping
   ☐ Addition to existing grouping
   ☐ Regrouping
   ☐ Disposition

Description of the appropriate economic unit relationship:
________________________________________________________________
________________________________________________________________
________________________________________________________________
```

**Notes / Pitfalls.**

- "Appropriate economic unit" is determined under five facts and
  circumstances factors per Reg. §1.469-4(c): similarities/differences
  in types of trade or business; extent of common control; extent of
  common ownership; geographical location; interdependencies (e.g.,
  buying/selling between activities; single set of books).
- Rental activities **cannot** generally be grouped with non-rental
  activities (Reg. §1.469-4(d)) except where one is "insubstantial"
  in relation to the other or 50% / 50% ownership.
- Grouping is generally **binding in subsequent years** unless the
  facts and circumstances change materially or the taxpayer
  regroups (which itself requires disclosure).
- IRS may regroup activities if the taxpayer's grouping does not
  reflect an appropriate economic unit and a principal purpose is
  tax avoidance.
- Real estate professional aggregation election (D2) is a
  **separate** election under §1.469-9(g) that aggregates rental
  activities specifically. The two operate independently but both
  may apply to the same taxpayer.

## D2. Real Estate Professional Rental Aggregation (Reg. §1.469-9(g)(3))

**Authority:** IRC §469(c)(7); Treas. Reg. §1.469-9; Rev. Proc.
2011-34.

**Purpose.** A qualifying real estate professional (REPS) under
§469(c)(7) treats rental real estate activities as nonpassive only
if the taxpayer **materially participates** in the activity. By
default, each rental property is a separate activity, requiring the
taxpayer to materially participate in each. The §469(c)(7)(A)
election aggregates **all** rental real estate interests into a
single activity, making it possible to satisfy material participation
across the portfolio collectively.

**Eligibility.**

1. **Real estate professional status (§469(c)(7)(B)):**
   - More than 50% of personal services performed in trades or
     businesses are performed in real property trades or businesses
     in which the taxpayer materially participates; AND
   - More than 750 hours of services during the tax year are
     performed in real property trades or businesses in which the
     taxpayer materially participates.

2. **Material participation in the aggregated rental activity** —
   typically the 500-hour test under §469(h).

**When to file.** Attach to the timely-filed return for the
**first year** in which the election is made. Election is binding
for all subsequent years until the taxpayer **revokes** it under
Rev. Proc. 2011-34 (which requires showing a material change in
facts and circumstances).

**Where to file.** With Form 1040.

**Sample election language:**

```
AGGREGATION ELECTION FOR REAL ESTATE PROFESSIONAL

Taxpayer:           ___________________________________________
FEIN/SSN:           ___________________________________________
Tax Year:           ___________________________________________

In accordance with Treas. Reg. § 1.469-9(g)(3), the taxpayer
hereby states that he (or she) is a qualifying real estate
professional under IRC § 469(c)(7), and elects under
IRC § 469(c)(7)(A) to treat all interests in the real estate
listed below as a single rental real estate activity.

Property 1:
  Description / Address: _______________________________________
Property 2:
  Description / Address: _______________________________________
Property 3:
  Description / Address: _______________________________________
Property 4:
  Description / Address: _______________________________________
```

**Notes / Pitfalls.**

- **Time logs are critical.** Maintain contemporaneous logs of
  hours by activity to substantiate the 750-hour test and the
  material participation tests. *Hailstock*, *Hassanipour*, and
  *Frank Aragona Trust* establish the documentation standard.
- The 50%-of-services test counts ALL personal services, not just
  rental. A full-time W-2 employee in a non-real-estate field
  generally cannot satisfy 50%.
- Spouses may aggregate hours for the 750-hour test and material
  participation, but **only one spouse** must satisfy the 50% test
  (Reg. §1.469-9(c)(4)).
- Once made, the election is binding. Revocation under Rev. Proc.
  2011-34 requires demonstrating a "material change in facts and
  circumstances." Mere change in tax preference is not sufficient.
- The aggregation election is independent of the §1.469-4 grouping
  election (D1). REPS may make both.
- Suspended passive losses from years prior to REPS qualification
  remain suspended until the property generating them is fully
  disposed of in a fully-taxable transaction.
- Election permits material participation to be satisfied on the
  aggregate. Once satisfied, ALL rentals in the aggregate are
  nonpassive — losses from any of them offset W-2 / portfolio
  income.
- Self-rental rule (Reg. §1.469-2(f)(6)) still applies even after
  aggregation: rental income from a property leased to a
  taxpayer-controlled business is recharacterized as nonpassive
  income (one-way recharacterization).

# Part E. Partnership Elections

## E1. §754 Step-Up Election

**Authority:** IRC §754; Treas. Reg. §1.754-1; IRC §734(b); §743(b);
§755.

**Purpose.** Permits a partnership to elect to adjust the basis of
partnership property in two situations:

1. **§743(b) — Transfer of partnership interest.** Upon sale,
   exchange, or transfer-on-death of a partnership interest, the
   basis of partnership property is adjusted with respect to the
   transferee partner only, generally to step up (or step down) the
   transferee's share of inside basis to match outside basis.
2. **§734(b) — Distribution of partnership property.** Upon
   distribution of partnership property to a partner, the basis of
   remaining partnership property is adjusted.

**§755** allocates the §734/§743 adjustments among the partnership's
specific assets.

**When to file.** Attach the election statement to a timely-filed
(with extensions) Form 1065 for the year in which the triggering
event occurs (sale, exchange, transfer, or distribution).

**Where to file.** With Form 1065.

**Effect.** Once made, the §754 election applies to all subsequent
transfers and distributions until revoked. **Revocation requires IRS
consent.** The election is therefore essentially permanent in
practice.

**Mandatory adjustment scenarios (without election).** Even without a
§754 election, mandatory adjustments may occur under:

- **§743(b) substantial built-in loss (>$250,000)** — mandatory step-
  down on transfer of partnership interest.
- **§734(b) substantial basis reduction (>$250,000)** — mandatory
  step-down on certain distributions.

**Sample election language:**

```
SECTION 754 ELECTION STATEMENT
(Pursuant to Treas. Reg. § 1.754-1(b)(1))

Partnership/LLC Name: ____________________________________________
Federal ID #:         ____________________________________________
Tax Year Ending:      ____________________________________________

The partnership hereby elects, pursuant to IRC Section 754, to
adjust the basis of partnership property as a result of a
distribution of property or a sale or exchange of a partnership
interest as provided in IRC Sections 734(b) and 743(b).

____________________________     ____________________________
General Partner Signature        Print General Partner's Name

Date: ______________________
```

**Notes / Pitfalls.**

- Effect is **forward-looking and permanent** without IRS consent
  to revoke. Many practitioners advise carefully because a §754
  election creates ongoing compliance burdens (annual basis
  adjustment tracking; allocation among assets; potential for
  step-downs in future transfers).
- §734(b) and §743(b) adjustments are **partner-specific** for §743
  (only the transferee partner is affected) and **partnership-wide**
  for §734 (basis of remaining assets is adjusted).
- §755 allocations among partnership assets follow detailed rules;
  often requires appraisal.
- For transferee partner under §743(b), Form 8308 (Report of a Sale
  or Exchange of Certain Partnership Interests) reporting may also
  be required.
- §754 election attaches at the **partnership** level; affects all
  partners.
- A §754 election cannot be made retroactively — only for the year
  of the triggering event.

## F2. QSST Election (§1361(d)(2))

**Authority:** IRC §1361(d)(2); Treas. Reg. §1.1361-1(j).

**Purpose.** Allows a Qualified Subchapter S Trust (QSST) to hold S
corp stock without disqualifying the S election. The current income
beneficiary is treated as the deemed owner of the S corp stock under
§1361(d)(1)(B), with all income, deductions, and credits flowing
through to the beneficiary.

**Eligibility (Reg. §1.1361-1(j)):**

A QSST must satisfy ALL of the following requirements:

1. **One income beneficiary.** During the life of the current income
   beneficiary, there will be only one income beneficiary of the
   trust. If husband and wife are co-beneficiaries, both must be
   U.S. residents or citizens AND must file joint returns.
2. **Corpus distribution restriction.** Any corpus distributed during
   the life of the current income beneficiary may be distributed
   only to that beneficiary.
3. **Termination event.** The current beneficiary's income interest
   in the trust terminates on the earlier of the beneficiary's death
   or upon termination of the trust.
4. **Asset distribution on termination.** Upon termination of the
   trust during the life of such income beneficiary, the trust
   distributes all its assets to such beneficiary.
5. **All income distributed currently.** The trust is required by
   its terms to distribute all of its income currently, OR the
   trustee distributes all of its income currently if not so
   required.
6. **No support obligation use.** No distribution of income or corpus
   by the trust will be in satisfaction of the grantor's legal
   obligation to support or maintain the income beneficiary.

**Who makes the election.** The **current income beneficiary** makes
the election (not the trustee).

**When to file.** No later than 2 months and 15 days after the date
the stock was transferred to the trust.

**Where to file.** Mail to the IRS Service Center where the
**corporation** files its return.

**Sample election language:**

```
QUALIFIED SUBCHAPTER S TRUST ELECTION
Made under IRC Section 1361(d)(2)

Current Income Beneficiary:        Trust:                      Corporation:
Name:    ____________________      Name:    _______________    Name:    _______________
Address: ____________________      Address: _______________    Address: _______________
TIN:     ____________________      TIN:     _______________    TIN:     _______________

The current income beneficiary, _________________ TIN __________
is making the qualified QSST election herein in accordance with
Internal Revenue Code Section 1361(d)(2) effective ______________
to treat the trust as a qualified Subchapter S trust pursuant to
IRC § 1361(c)(2)(A)(i). _____ shares of stock were transferred to
the _________ Trust TIN ________________ on _________________.

The _________ Trust (TIN _____________) includes all provisions of
Internal Revenue Regulation 1.1361-1(j)(6)(ii)(E) under the terms
of the trust and applicable local law:

(i)   During the life of the current income beneficiary, there
      will be only one income beneficiary of the trust (if husband
      and wife are beneficiaries, that they will file joint
      returns and that both are U.S. residents or citizens);

(ii)  Any corpus distributed during the life of the current income
      beneficiary may be distributed only to that beneficiary;

(iii) The current beneficiary's income interest in the trust will
      terminate on the earlier of the beneficiary's death or upon
      termination of the trust; and

(iv)  Upon the termination of the trust during the life of such
      income beneficiary, the trust will distribute all its assets
      to such beneficiary.

(2)   The trust is required to distribute all of its income
      currently, or that the trustee will distribute all of its
      income currently if not so required by the terms of the
      trust.

(3)   No distribution of income or corpus by the trust will be in
      satisfaction of the grantor's legal obligation to support or
      maintain the income beneficiary.

Signed by the current income beneficiary, _________________,
this ___ day of __________, 20__.

___________________________________
Current Income Beneficiary
```

**Notes / Pitfalls.**

- **Beneficiary makes the election**, not the trustee. Trustee
  cannot consent on the beneficiary's behalf.
- 2-month-and-15-day deadline runs from **date of stock transfer**,
  not from formation of the trust.
- The trust **must distribute all income currently**. If the trust
  document does not require this, the trustee must do it as a matter
  of practice — but the better practice is to amend the trust
  document.
- Once made, election is **binding** until trust terminates or
  beneficiary changes via permitted means.
- Each successive income beneficiary must file a new QSST election
  within 2 months and 15 days after becoming the income beneficiary.
- ESBT election (F3) is the principal alternative; ESBTs are taxed at
  trust-level top rates but offer more flexibility.

## F3. ESBT Election (§1361(e)(3))

**Authority:** IRC §1361(e); Treas. Reg. §1.1361-1(m).

**Purpose.** Allows an Electing Small Business Trust (ESBT) to hold
S corp stock without disqualifying the S election. Unlike QSSTs,
ESBTs may have multiple beneficiaries and accumulate income.
However, the S corp portion of the ESBT's income is taxed at the
**highest individual rate** (currently 37%) rather than flowing
through to beneficiaries.

**Eligibility (§1361(e)(1)):**

1. The trust does not have any beneficiary other than:
   (i) An individual,
   (ii) An estate, or
   (iii) A §170(c)(2)-(5) charitable organization that holds a
        contingent remainder interest.
2. No interest in the trust was acquired by purchase.
3. The trust files an ESBT election.

**Who makes the election.** The **trustee** files the ESBT election
(unlike QSST, where the beneficiary files).

**When to file.** No later than 2 months and 15 days after the date
the stock was transferred to the trust (or the date the trust first
becomes eligible as an ESBT).

**Where to file.** Mail to the IRS Service Center where the
**corporation** files its return.

**Sample election language:**

```
QUALIFIED ELECTING SMALL BUSINESS TRUST ELECTION
Made under IRC Section 1361(e)(3)

Potential Current Beneficiary:     Trust:                      Corporation:
Name:    ____________________      Name:    _______________    Name:    _______________
Address: ____________________      Address: _______________    Address: _______________
TIN:     ____________________      TIN:     _______________    TIN:     _______________

The potential current beneficiary, ________________ TIN ________
is making the qualified ESBT election herein in accordance with
Internal Revenue Code Section 1361(e)(3) effective ______________
pursuant to Internal Revenue Code Section 1361(c)(2)(A)(v).
_____ shares of stock were transferred to the _________ Trust
TIN _______________ on _________________.

The _____________ Trust (TIN _________________) includes all
provisions of Internal Revenue Regulation 1.1361-1(m)(2):

1. This election is made under Internal Revenue Code §1361(e)(3).

2. The stock was transferred to the trust ________________.

3. The election is to be effective _______________________.

4. The trustee represents:
   (1) The trust meets the definitional requirements of
       §1361(e)(1); and
   (2) All potential current beneficiaries of the trust meet the
       shareholder requirements of §1361(b)(1).

Signed by the trustee, _____________________,
this ___ day of __________, 20__.

___________________________________
Trustee
```

**Notes / Pitfalls.**

- **S corp portion taxed at top individual rate** (37% as of 2025).
  This is generally less favorable than QSST flow-through (where
  beneficiary's marginal rate applies).
- However, ESBT permits **multiple beneficiaries**, **discretionary
  distributions**, and **income accumulation** — features QSST
  cannot accommodate.
- ESBT is preferred when the trust has multiple beneficiaries with
  varying income needs, when the grantor wants discretionary
  distributions, or when minor beneficiaries are involved.
- A "potential current beneficiary" includes any person who, at the
  time, is entitled to or is in the discretion of any person to
  receive a distribution from the principal or income of the trust.
  All potential current beneficiaries are counted as S corp
  shareholders for the §1361(b)(1) 100-shareholder test.
- TCJA permitted nonresident alien (NRA) beneficiaries of an ESBT
  (post-2017) — formerly disqualifying.
- Conversion between QSST and ESBT is permitted under specific
  procedures (Reg. §1.1361-1(j)(12) and (m)(7)).

## F4. §1377(a)(2) Closing-of-Books Election

**Authority:** IRC §1377(a)(2); Treas. Reg. §1.1377-1(b).

**Purpose.** When a shareholder's interest is terminated during an S
corp's tax year (e.g., by sale, gift, or redemption), §1377(a)(1)
generally requires pro-rata daily allocation of S corp items between
the terminating shareholder and the continuing shareholders. The
§1377(a)(2) election permits the corporation to treat the year as if
it consisted of two short tax years — the first ending on the date
of termination, the second running from the day after termination
through year-end.

This more-precisely allocates income, gain, loss, deduction, and
credit between pre- and post-termination periods.

**Eligibility.**

- A shareholder's interest must be **completely terminated** during
  the year (not merely reduced).
- The corporation and **all affected shareholders** must consent.
- "Affected shareholders" = the terminating shareholder and any
  shareholder to whom the terminating shareholder transferred shares.

**When to file.** Attach to the timely-filed (with extensions) Form
1120-S for the year of termination.

**Where to file.** With Form 1120-S.

**Sample election language:**

```
ELECTION TO TREAT TAX YEAR AS IF IT CONSISTED OF
2 TAX YEARS UPON DISPOSITION OF SHAREHOLDER'S STOCK
(Attach to timely filed 1120-S for year of transfer)

Company Name:                 _____________________________________
Address:                      _____________________________________
FEIN:                         _____________________________________
IRS Service Center:           _____________________________________
Corporate Year End:           _____________________________________

Name of Terminating Shareholder(s):
  _____________________________________________________________

Cause of Termination:
  _____________________________________________________________

Name of Acquiring Shareholder(s):
  _____________________________________________________________

Date of Event:
  _____________________________________________________________

The above corporation hereby elects under §1377(a)(2) and
Reg. §1.1377-1(b) to have the rules provided in §1377(a)(1)
applied as if the taxable year consisted of two taxable years.
All affected shareholders consent to this election.

Terminating Shareholder Consent:        Acquiring Shareholder Consent:
___________________________             ___________________________
Name: _____________________             Name: _____________________
Address: __________________             Address: __________________
SSN: ______________________             SSN: ______________________
Date: _____________________             Date: _____________________
```

*Note: Treas. Reg. §1.1377-1 eliminated the formal signature
requirement, although signed consents remain best practice.*

**Notes / Pitfalls.**

- Election applies **only to the affected shareholders' allocations**;
  remaining shareholders' allocations are still pro-rata daily for
  the full year unless §1362(e)(3) (S termination year) is also in
  play.
- If the corporation experiences a §1362(e) termination event during
  the year (e.g., loss of S status), §1362(e)(3) closing-of-books is
  separately available.
- Useful when income is concentrated in one period (e.g., a large
  sale of inventory or recognition of capital gain near year-end)
  and pro-rata daily allocation would distort the terminating
  shareholder's K-1.
- All affected shareholders' consent is required — single
  shareholder dissent defeats the election.
- Once made, election is binding for the year.

## F5. Reg. §1.1367-1 Change in Basis-Reduction Ordering Rules

**Authority:** Treas. Reg. §1.1367-1(g).

**Purpose.** S corp shareholder basis is reduced by losses,
deductions, distributions, and certain nondeductible expenses /
oil-and-gas depletion. The default ordering rule reduces basis FIRST
by **distributions**, THEN by **nondeductible expenses and oil-and-
gas depletion**, and FINALLY by **deductible losses and deductions**.

The Reg. §1.1367-1(g) election reverses the second and third
priorities, reducing basis FIRST by **deductible losses and
deductions** before nondeductible expenses and oil-and-gas
depletion. This permits the shareholder to use deductible losses
in the current year (within basis) at the cost of deferring
nondeductible expenses / oil-and-gas depletion to future years
(carrying them over until basis exists).

**Effect.** Maximizes current deductible loss utilization at the
expense of nondeductible expense / depletion deferral.

**Who makes the election.** Each individual shareholder, not the
corporation. Each shareholder's election is independent.

**When to file.** Attach to the timely-filed (with extensions)
shareholder Form 1040 for the first year the election applies.
Once made, it is binding for that year and all future years until
revoked (revocation requires IRS consent).

**Where to file.** With Form 1040.

**Sample election language:**

```
S CORPORATION SHAREHOLDER ELECTION
TO CHANGE ORDERING RULES

Name of Individual:    _________________________________________
SSN:                   _________________________________________
Tax Year Ending:       _________________________________________

Election to Reduce Basis by Items of Loss or Deduction
Before Reducing Basis by Nondeductible Expenses
And Certain Oil and Gas Depletion

The taxpayer hereby elects, pursuant to Treas. Reg. §1.1367-1, to
reduce basis by items of loss or deduction before nondeductible
expenses and certain oil and gas depletion. The taxpayer agrees
to carry over to the succeeding year the nondeductible,
non-capital expenses and certain oil and gas depletion deductions
that exceed stock and debt basis.
```

**Notes / Pitfalls.**

- Election is **per shareholder**, not per corporation. Different
  shareholders of the same S corp may make different elections.
- Election is **binding in subsequent years** unless revoked.
- Useful when the shareholder has substantial losses that would
  otherwise be deferred under at-risk / passive activity rules but
  could be used currently if basis allowed.
- Carryover of nondeductible expenses / oil-and-gas depletion is
  preserved in subsequent years when basis becomes available.
- Coordinate with §469 passive activity, §465 at-risk, and §461(l)
  excess business loss limitations — basis is the **first**
  limitation, not the only one.

## F6. Reg. §1.1368-1(f)(2) Distribute E&P Before AAA

**Authority:** Treas. Reg. §1.1368-1(f)(2); IRC §1368(e)(3).

**Purpose.** A former C corporation that has elected S status may
have **accumulated earnings and profits (AE&P)** from C corp years.
By default under §1368, S corp distributions reduce the
**Accumulated Adjustments Account (AAA)** first (tax-free to the
extent of basis), then E&P (taxable as dividend), then return of
remaining basis.

The §1.1368-1(f)(2) election reverses this ordering, allowing the
corporation to distribute **E&P FIRST** before AAA. This is
generally counterintuitive (it accelerates dividend recognition
to shareholders) but is useful when:

1. The corporation wants to **eliminate C-era E&P** to avoid future
   §1375 passive investment income tax issues. §1375 imposes a
   corporate-level tax on excess passive investment income for S
   corps with AE&P.
2. The corporation faces potential §1362(d)(3) **S election
   termination** for excessive passive investment income with AE&P
   (3 consecutive years > 25% passive investment income).
3. Shareholders are in low brackets in the current year and want to
   accelerate dividend recognition.

**Who makes the election.** The corporation, with **consent of all
affected shareholders** who received distributions during the year.

**When to file.** Attach to the timely-filed (with extensions) Form
1120-S for the year of distribution.

**Where to file.** With Form 1120-S.

**Sample election language:**

```
ELECTION & SHAREHOLDER CONSENT TO DISTRIBUTE E&P BEFORE AAA

Corporation:   ____________________________________________________
Address:       ____________________________________________________
               ____________________________________________________
FEIN:          ____________________________________________________

The above corporation hereby elects under Treas. Reg.
§1.1368-1(f)(2) to treat distributions to shareholders during
the taxable year ended December 31, ____ as first made from the
corporation's accumulated earnings & profits.

All affected shareholders who received corporate distributions
during the stated year hereby consent to this election below.

Shareholder Consents:

Stockholder: _______________________   Date: ______________
  Signed: _________________________
  Address: _______________________________________________________
  Address: _______________________________________________________

Stockholder: _______________________   Date: ______________
  Signed: _________________________
  Address: _______________________________________________________
  Address: _______________________________________________________
```

**Notes / Pitfalls.**

- All shareholders who received distributions during the year must
  consent. Failing to obtain a consent voids the election.
- Distributions treated as dividends are subject to qualified dividend
  rates (0%, 15%, or 20%) at the shareholder level, plus NIIT if
  applicable.
- Once C-era E&P is eliminated, subsequent S corp distributions are
  governed entirely by AAA / OAA / basis rules under §1368.
- Useful interaction with §1375 passive investment income tax
  planning and §1362(d)(3) S election termination avoidance.
- Combine with deemed dividend election (F7) when no actual cash
  distribution is desired but E&P elimination is.

## F7. Reg. §1.1368-1(f)(3) Deemed Dividend Election

**Authority:** Treas. Reg. §1.1368-1(f)(3).

**Purpose.** Permits an S corp with C-era AE&P to treat as a
**deemed dividend** an amount of accumulated E&P, even though no
actual cash distribution is made. Mechanically, the election is
treated as if the corporation distributed AE&P to shareholders pro
rata, and the shareholders contributed it back. Shareholders have
dividend income; corporation eliminates the deemed amount of E&P;
shareholder basis is increased by the deemed contribution.

**Use cases.**

1. **Eliminate C-era E&P** without actual distribution (e.g., when
   cash is needed for operations).
2. **Avoid §1375 passive investment income tax** by eliminating
   AE&P trigger.
3. **Avoid §1362(d)(3) S election termination** for >25% passive
   investment income with AE&P over 3 consecutive years.
4. **Step-up shareholder basis** for future loss utilization.

**Who makes the election.** The corporation, with consent of all
shareholders owning shares on the **last day of the tax year**
(typically December 31).

**When to file.** Attach to the timely-filed (with extensions) Form
1120-S for the year of the deemed dividend.

**Where to file.** With Form 1120-S.

**Sample election language:**

```
ELECTION & SHAREHOLDER CONSENT TO DEEMED DIVIDEND

Corporation:   ____________________________________________________
Address:       ____________________________________________________
               ____________________________________________________
FEIN:          ____________________________________________________

The above corporation hereby elects under Treas. Reg.
§1.1368-1(f)(3) to distribute all or part of its accumulated
earnings and profits as a deemed dividend to all persons owning
corporate shares on the last day of December, ______.

All affected shareholders, who constitute all shareholders of the
corporation, who received corporate distributions during the
stated year hereby consent to this election to treat the deemed
dividend in accordance with the rules of §1368 and the related
regulations.

Shareholder Consents:

Stockholder:        _____________________   Date: ______________
  Signed:           _____________________
  Address:          _____________________________________________
  Address:          _____________________________________________
  Deemed Amt. Rcvd: _____________________
  SSN:              _____________________

Stockholder:        _____________________   Date: ______________
  Signed:           _____________________
  Address:          _____________________________________________
  Address:          _____________________________________________
  Deemed Amt. Rcvd: _____________________
  SSN:              _____________________
```

**Notes / Pitfalls.**

- ALL shareholders must consent — not just those who received actual
  distributions.
- Allocation of deemed dividend is **pro rata** to share ownership
  on the last day of the year.
- Shareholders recognize qualified dividend income (0/15/20%) plus
  NIIT if applicable, even without cash receipt. They must have
  funds to pay the tax (deemed dividend creates a phantom income
  problem if shareholders don't have other liquid resources).
- Shareholder basis is increased by the deemed contribution back
  amount, dollar-for-dollar.
- AE&P is eliminated by the deemed amount. Useful for §1375 / §1362(d)(3)
  cleanup.
- Distinct from F6 (actual distribution from E&P first); deemed
  dividend (F7) is a phantom dividend with no cash movement; F6 is
  ordering for actual distributions.
- Once made, election is binding for the year.

## F8. Corporate Revocation of S Election (§1362(d)(1))

**Authority:** IRC §1362(d)(1); Treas. Reg. §1.1362-6.

**Purpose.** Revokes the S corporation election, returning the
corporation to C corporation status. Effective on the date specified
(prospectively) or on the first day of the year of revocation if
filed by the 15th day of the 3rd month of that year.

**Required consent.** Shareholders owning **more than 50% of the
corporation's stock** (voting + non-voting combined) must consent to
the revocation. Non-consenting shareholders cannot block revocation
if the >50% threshold is met.

**When to file.**

- **Effective for current tax year:** File on or before the **15th
  day of the 3rd month** of the current tax year.
- **Effective for future tax year:** File at any time, specifying
  the effective date.

**Where to file.** IRS Service Center where the S election (Form
2553) was originally filed. (Generally the corporation's regular
filing center.)

**Filing components.**

1. **Corporate statement of revocation** signed by an authorized
   officer.
2. **Shareholder consent statement** signed by shareholders owning
   >50% of stock — see F9.

**Sample corporate statement of revocation:**

```
CORPORATE STATEMENT OF REVOCATION OF S ELECTION
Under IRC § 1362(a)

[TODAY'S DATE]

Internal Revenue Service Center
[Service Center Address]

Re:  [CORPORATION NAME]
     [CORPORATION ADDRESS]
     [FEIN]

Subject: Revocation of S Corporation election

Please be advised that [CORPORATION NAME], a corporation with its
principal office located at [CORPORATION ADDRESS], hereby revokes
the Subchapter S election it previously made under §1362(a) of
the Internal Revenue Code.

The first year for which this revocation is intended to be
effective is the corporation's tax year beginning _________.

The corporation has _______ shares of stock issued and
outstanding at the effective date including all voting and
non-voting stock.

A statement signed by shareholders owning more than 50% of the
corporation's stock and consenting to the revocation is attached.

Sincerely,

[CORPORATE OFFICER NAME]
Title: _____________________
```

**Notes / Pitfalls.**

- Revocation is **prospective only** unless filed by the 15th day of
  the 3rd month of the current year.
- Once revoked, the corporation **cannot re-elect S status for 5
  years** under §1362(g) without IRS consent.
- A revocation triggers a "**S termination year**" under §1362(e)
  if it occurs mid-year, requiring allocation of income between
  S short period and C short period (closing-of-books or pro-rata
  daily, election available under §1362(e)(3)).
- Built-in gains tax under §1374 may apply to the C corp upon sale
  of pre-conversion built-in-gain assets (10-year recognition
  period).
- All shareholders, voting and non-voting, count toward the >50%
  threshold; ownership is by share, not headcount.

# Part G. Decedent and Estate Elections

## G1. §213(c) Election to Deduct Decedent's Medical Expenses on Form 1040

**Authority:** IRC §213(c); Treas. Reg. §1.213-1(d)(2).

**Purpose.** When a taxpayer dies, medical expenses paid by the
estate within **one year after the date of death** for medical care
of the decedent provided **before death** are generally deductible on
Form 706 (estate tax) under §2053 OR on Form 1040 (final or amended
final return) under §213. The §213(c) election permits the executor
to claim the deduction on Form 1040 (the decedent's final or amended
final return) instead of Form 706, with a corresponding waiver of
the §2053 estate-tax deduction.

**Why elect Form 1040 deduction instead of Form 706.** Often the
income tax benefit on Form 1040 (effective rate up to 37%) exceeds
the estate tax benefit on Form 706 (top rate 40%, but only if
estate exceeds the exclusion). For estates below the federal
exclusion ($13.61 million in 2024 / $13.99 million in 2025), no
estate tax is owed regardless of the Form 706 medical expense
deduction, so the Form 1040 election is the only path to extract
tax benefit.

**Eligibility (§213(c)):**

1. Medical expenses must be **paid out of the decedent's estate**
   (or by other parties) within **one year after the date of death**.
2. Expenses must be for **medical care of the decedent** provided
   before death.
3. **Waiver** of §2053 estate-tax deduction must be filed.

**Who makes the election.** Generally the executor of the estate,
acting on behalf of the decedent, files the amended or final Form
1040 with the §213(c) election attached.

**When to file.** With the decedent's final Form 1040 for the year
of death, OR amended final Form 1040 if expenses are paid post-filing
but within the one-year-after-death window.

**Sample election language:**

```
ELECTION TO EXPENSE DECEDENT'S MEDICAL EXPENSES

Decedent:                ___________________________________________
SSN:                     ___________________________________________
Date of Death:           ___________________________________________

Election under §213(c) and Treas. Reg. §1.213-1(d)(2):

The taxpayer hereby elects to deduct the following expenses, paid
within one year of the decedent's death, on Form 1040 for the year
ended December 31, [YEAR].

These expenses have not been claimed under §2053 on Form 706; and
further, the taxpayer hereby waives the right to deduct the
expenses at any time for estate tax purposes under §2053.

Expense category:                                         Amount:
   Doctors and hospitals                                 $ ______
   Prescriptions                                         $ ______
   Medical equipment                                     $ ______
   Long-term care                                        $ ______
   Other medical (specify): ______________________       $ ______
   __________________________________________________
   Total                                                 $ ______

Executor:                ___________________________________________
Date:                    ___________________________________________
Signature:               ___________________________________________
```

**Notes / Pitfalls.**

- The waiver of §2053 is **irrevocable** for the items elected.
  Cannot claim the same expense on both Form 1040 and Form 706.
- Expenses paid by the surviving spouse from joint funds are
  generally still eligible; payment source matters less than payment
  timing (within one year of death).
- §213(a) 7.5%-of-AGI threshold still applies on Form 1040.
- For estates below the federal exclusion, the §213(c) election is
  nearly always preferable because the §2053 deduction provides no
  benefit (no federal estate tax liability to reduce).
- For estates above the federal exclusion, run a comparison: Form
  706 marginal rate (40% above the exclusion) vs. Form 1040
  marginal rate. Sometimes the Form 706 deduction provides greater
  benefit.
- State estate tax may also factor in for estates in states with
  decoupled estate taxes (CT, NY, MA, IL, etc.) — coordinate state
  treatment.

## G2. Portability Election Waiver (§2010(c)(5)(A))

**Authority:** IRC §2010(c)(5)(A) (Deceased Spouse Unused Exclusion
— DSUE / "portability"); Form 706.

**Purpose.** A portability election allows the surviving spouse to
use the decedent spouse's unused federal estate tax exclusion
(DSUE) by adding it to the surviving spouse's own exclusion.
Portability is elected by **timely filing a complete Form 706** for
the decedent's estate, even if no estate tax is otherwise owed.

The "portability election waiver" is the **executor's affirmative
decision NOT to file Form 706** when filing would otherwise be
optional, thereby **waiving** the portability benefit. This
document records the executor's informed decision and shifts
liability for that decision from the tax preparer to the executor.

**Why a waiver document is needed.** When an estate is below the
federal exclusion ($13.61M for 2024 / $13.99M for 2025), Form 706
is not statutorily required. However, electing portability requires
filing Form 706 — usually at significant cost ($5,000 - $25,000+
in preparation fees plus appraisals). The executor must weigh:

- **Cost of Form 706** (preparer fees, appraisals, attorney review).
- **Probability that surviving spouse's combined estate** will
  exceed the surviving spouse's own exclusion at her/his death.
- **Risk that exclusion amounts decline** in future years (e.g.,
  TCJA exclusion sunset, scheduled to occur after 2025 absent
  legislation; OBBBA may have addressed).
- **Investment growth** of marital assets potentially pushing
  the survivor over her/his exclusion.

For modest combined estates, the cost of Form 706 may exceed the
reasonably-likely portability benefit. The waiver documents the
executor's decision and protects the tax preparer.

**Sample waiver language:**

```
PORTABILITY EXECUTOR STATEMENT

As executor of the estate, it is your responsibility to determine
if the cost of preparing the Federal Form 706 estate tax return
exceeds the potential benefit of the portability amount to be
derived from filing the Federal Estate Tax Form 706. Please
indicate below your decision whether to incur the cost of filing
Form 706 for the estate in order to protect the portability
amount or to not file Form 706 and forego any potential benefit
to be derived from filing a Federal Form 706 for the estate.

Estate of:        _______________________________________________
Date of Death:    _______________________________________________
FEIN:             _______________________________________________
Executor:         _______________________________________________

☐  I wish to file Form 706 for the above estate to elect
   portability of the deceased spouse's unused exclusion (DSUE).

   _______________________________________   _______________
   (Executor Signature)                       (Date)


-- OR --

ELECTION TO NOT FILE FORM 706
COMBINED WITH EXECUTOR ASSUMPTION OF LIABILITY

☐  I do not wish to file Form 706 for the above estate and
   understand that by not filing Form 706 in a timely and
   complete manner I am foregoing the ability to transfer the
   unused estate tax equivalent amount to the surviving spouse.
   I further understand that this action relieves the tax
   professional from any and all liability from the failure to
   make a portability election and transfers this liability to
   the executor.

   _______________________________________   _______________
   (Executor Signature)                       (Date)
```

**Notes / Pitfalls.**

- **Portability filing deadline:** Generally **9 months after date
  of death**, with 6-month extension available via Form 4768 (total
  15 months). **Rev. Proc. 2022-32** provides simplified late-filing
  relief for portability-only Form 706 filings up to **5 years after
  the date of death** without the need for a private letter ruling.
- The 5-year window under Rev. Proc. 2022-32 **only applies** if
  the estate is otherwise not required to file Form 706 (i.e.,
  below the exclusion threshold).
- Portability is **not available** if the surviving spouse has
  remarried at the time of subsequent death — only the most recent
  deceased spouse's DSUE is transferable.
- Generation-skipping transfer (GST) tax exemption is **NOT
  portable** — only the estate / gift exclusion. The executor's
  decision to forgo portability does not affect GST.
- Several state estate taxes do NOT have portability (e.g., NY).
  State-only Form 706-equivalent may still be required.
- TCJA doubled the exclusion; the doubled portion sunsets after
  2025 absent legislation. OBBBA — `[CITATION NEEDED — search:
  OBBBA estate tax exclusion Pub L 119-21]`. If the exclusion
  reverts to ~$7M (TCJA pre-doubling level adjusted for inflation),
  more estates will benefit from portability — making the waiver
  decision more consequential.
- Document the executor's decision in writing **regardless of
  outcome** (file or waive). This is core practitioner risk
  management.

# Part H. Farm Elections

## H1. §451(d) Defer Crop Insurance Proceeds

**Authority:** IRC §451(d); Treas. Reg. §1.451-6.

**Purpose.** A cash-method farmer may elect to defer reporting of
crop insurance and disaster payments received in the current year
to the **following year**, when the insurance proceeds compensate
for crop damage or destruction that would normally have produced
income in the following year under the farmer's normal business
practice.

**Eligibility (§451(d)):**

1. Taxpayer is in the **trade or business of farming**.
2. Taxpayer reports income on the **cash receipts and disbursements
   method** of accounting.
3. Insurance / disaster payment is received because of **destruction
   or damage** to crops.
4. Under the farmer's **normal business practice**, the income from
   the damaged or destroyed crops would have been included in gross
   income for a tax year **following** the year of destruction or
   damage.

**When to file.** Attach to the timely-filed (with extensions)
return for the year the crop insurance proceeds are received.

**Where to file.** With Form 1040 / Schedule F.

**Sample election language:**

```
ELECTION TO DEFER CROP INSURANCE PROCEEDS
IRC § 451(d)
(Attachment to Return)

Taxpayer:        ___________________________________________
FEIN/SSN:        ___________________________________________
Tax Year:        ___________________________________________

Code to defer insurance proceeds received as a result of
destruction or damage to crops. The taxpayer declares that they
are in the trade or business of farming reporting taxable income
using the cash receipts and disbursements method of accounting
and that under the taxpayer's normal business practice the income
derived from the crops which were damaged or destroyed would have
been included in gross income for a taxable year following the
taxable year of such destruction or damage.

The following information is provided in accordance with
Treas. Reg. §1.451-6(b)(1):

(a) Specific crop or crops damaged or destroyed:
    _______________________________________________________________
    _______________________________________________________________
    _______________________________________________________________

    _______________________________________________________________
    _______________________________________________________________

    ____________   ____________   ____________

    Payment Amount   |  Crop Description   |  Date Received
    _______________  |  _________________  |  ______________
    _______________  |  _________________  |  ______________
    _______________  |  _________________  |  ______________
    _______________  |  _________________  |  ______________
    _______________  |  _________________  |  ______________
    _______________  |  _________________  |  ______________

(e) Name and address of insurers in part (d):
    1. ___________________________________________________________
    2. ___________________________________________________________
    3. ___________________________________________________________
    4. ___________________________________________________________
    5. ___________________________________________________________
    6. ___________________________________________________________
```

**Notes / Pitfalls.**

- "Normal business practice" must support that income from the
  damaged crops would have been recognized in the **following**
  year. Documentation of the farmer's typical sale timing
  (storage, contract dates, etc.) is critical.
- One-year deferral only — cannot defer 2+ years.
- Election applies to **all** qualifying crop insurance / disaster
  payments received in the year, not selectively per payment.
- Disaster-related payments under federal disaster declarations may
  qualify even if not technically "crop insurance" — see Treas.
  Reg. §1.451-6.
- Cannot be combined with §1033 involuntary conversion deferral
  for the same proceeds.
- Election is annual; must be made each year crop insurance is
  received and deferral is desired.

## H2. §451(e) Defer Forced Livestock Sale Proceeds

**Authority:** IRC §451(e); Treas. Reg. §1.451-7.

**Purpose.** A cash-method farmer may elect to **defer one year**
the reporting of income from livestock sales that exceeded normal
sales because of **weather-related conditions** that forced the
early sale or exchange. Distinct from §1033(e), which permits
**replacement** of the livestock to defer gain entirely (4-year
replacement window).

**Eligibility (§451(e)):**

1. Taxpayer is in the **trade or business of farming**.
2. Taxpayer reports income on the **cash receipts and disbursements
   method** of accounting.
3. **Weather-related conditions** forced the early sale or exchange
   of livestock.
4. Excess sales — the number of livestock sold exceeded what would
   have been sold under the taxpayer's **normal business practice**
   in the absence of the weather conditions.

**Note on §1033(e) coordination.** §1033(e) provides a **4-year
replacement period** to defer gain on forced livestock sales by
acquiring replacement livestock — this is a separate, more powerful
deferral when replacement is feasible. §451(e) is a one-year
deferral that does not require replacement. Many farmers use both:
§451(e) deferral provides cash flow to fund §1033(e) replacement.

**When to file.** Attach to the timely-filed (with extensions)
return for the year of the forced sales.

**Where to file.** With Form 1040 / Schedule F.

**Sample election language:**

```
ELECTION TO DEFER LIVESTOCK SALE PROCEEDS
IRC § 451(e)
(Attachment to Return)

Taxpayer:        ___________________________________________
FEIN/SSN:        ___________________________________________
Tax Year:        ___________________________________________
Address:         ___________________________________________

The above taxpayer elects under §451(e) of the Internal Revenue
Code to defer sales proceeds from the forced sale of livestock
because of weather-related conditions.

The taxpayer declares that they are in the trade or business of
farming reporting taxable income using the cash receipts and
disbursements method of accounting.

The following information is provided in accordance with
Treas. Reg. §1.451-7(g):

(a) Evidence of the weather-related conditions which forced the
    early sale or exchange of the livestock and the date, if
    known, on which the area was designated as eligible for
    assistance by the Federal Government:
    _______________________________________________________________
    _______________________________________________________________
    _______________________________________________________________

(b) The relationship between the affected area and the
    taxpayer's early sale or exchange of the livestock:
    _______________________________________________________________
    _______________________________________________________________

(c) Total number of livestock sold in each of the 3 preceding
    years:
       Last year:                _________
       Two prior years ago:      _________
       Three prior years ago:    _________

(d) Number of livestock that would have been sold in the taxable
    year had the taxpayer followed its normal business practice
    in the absence of such weather conditions:    _________

(e) Total number of animals sold, and the number sold due to
    weather-related conditions during the taxable year:

    Livestock          Total      Number Normally    Excess Sold
    Classification     Number     Sold               Due to Weather
    ______________     _______    _____________      _____________
    ______________     _______    _____________      _____________
    ______________     _______    _____________      _____________

    classification, pursuant to Treas. Reg. §1.451-7(e):

    Classification 1:  ___________________________
       (Income from sales) $__________ /
       (Total number sold) ___________ ×
       (Excess sold) ____________ = $ ___________

    Classification 2:  ___________________________
       (Income from sales) $__________ /
       (Total number sold) ___________ ×
       (Excess sold) ____________ = $ ___________

    Classification 3:  ___________________________
       (Income from sales) $__________ /
       (Total number sold) ___________ ×
       (Excess sold) ____________ = $ ___________

    Total Amount Deferred:                       $ ___________
```

**Notes / Pitfalls.**

- "Weather-related conditions" must be the **forcing cause** of
  the early sales. Drought, flood, fire, blizzard, etc.
- Many farmers attach federal disaster declaration documentation
  (USDA, FEMA) to substantiate.
- Calculation requires three-year baseline of normal sales by
  livestock classification (cattle, hogs, sheep, etc.).
- Election deferral is **one year only** — proceeds deferred to
  Year 2 must be recognized in Year 2 unless §1033(e) replacement
  is also elected.
- §451(e) covers **all** livestock; §1033(e) covers only **draft,
  breeding, or dairy** livestock. Other livestock (e.g., feeders for
  immediate sale) qualify under §451(e) but not §1033(e).
- §1033(e) (4-year replacement) is generally more powerful than
  §451(e) (one-year deferral) for breeding livestock. Coordinate.
- Election is annual.
- Practitioners should also consider:
  - Deferred income may push the next-year tax bracket higher.
  - Self-employment tax applies to the deferred income in the year
    of recognition.
  - Coordination with §175 soil/water conservation expense, §263A
    UNICAP exemption for farmers, and §471(c) inventory simplification.
