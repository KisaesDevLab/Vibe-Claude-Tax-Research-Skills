# AR-C 60 — General Principles for SSARS Engagements

AR-C 60 is the umbrella section. It sets out general principles that
apply to all preparation, compilation, and review engagements
performed under SSARS. Pin-cite to AR-C 60 paragraphs in this format:
"AR-C §60.16" or "AR-C §60.A21".

## Scope (AR-C §60.01)

AR-C 60 applies to all SSARS engagements: preparation (AR-C 70),
compilation (AR-C 80), and review (AR-C 90). Review of pro forma
financial information has its own section (AR-C 120). AR-C 60 does
NOT apply to:
- Audits (AU-C).
- Other attestation engagements (AT-C).
- Engagements to compile pro forma F/S (AR-C 120).

## General principles

### Ethical requirements (AR-C §60.14)

The practitioner must comply with relevant ethical requirements,
including the AICPA Code of Professional Conduct (route any
threats-and-safeguards or §1.295 questions to
`compliance-aicpa-code`).

### Professional judgment (AR-C §60.15)

The practitioner exercises professional judgment in planning and
performing the engagement.

### Competence and capabilities (AR-C §60.16)

The practitioner must have the competence, capabilities, and
resources to perform the engagement. If the practitioner cannot
acquire competence (or hire a qualified specialist), they should
decline.

### Engagement acceptance and continuance (AR-C §60.25–.27)

Before accepting:
- Confirm the firm's preconditions for SSARS engagements:
  - Acceptable financial reporting framework (GAAP, tax basis,
    cash basis, regulatory basis, contractual basis, other-
    special-purpose with adequate description).
  - Management acknowledgment of its responsibility for:
    - The financial statements.
    - Designing, implementing, and maintaining internal control
      over financial reporting.
    - Preventing and detecting fraud.
    - Compliance with laws and regulations.
    - Providing the practitioner with access to information.
- Confirm management's understanding of the practitioner's
  responsibilities.

### Engagement letter (AR-C §60.A21)

Required before the practitioner begins work. The letter must
include:
- Objectives of the engagement.
- Responsibilities of management.
- Responsibilities of the practitioner.
- Limitations of the engagement.
- Identification of the financial reporting framework.
- Expected form and content of any report (or notation that no
  report will be issued for preparation).
- Effect of any restrictions on the engagement.
- For compilations and reviews: a statement that the firm will
  perform the engagement in accordance with SSARS.
- For compilations: language about NO assurance.
- For reviews: language about LIMITED assurance.

The engagement letter must be signed by the practitioner AND by
management or those charged with governance, before the engagement
begins.

Cross-reference: `engagement-letter-library/references/letters/`
provides per-engagement-type templates with these required
elements.

### Communication with management and those charged with governance (AR-C §60.A30+)

- Significant findings or issues during the engagement.
- Material modifications proposed by the practitioner.
- Going-concern uncertainties.
- Fraud and noncompliance with laws and regulations encountered.

### Documentation (AR-C §60.27–.29)

The engagement file should include:
- The engagement letter.
- The financial statements (or working drafts).
- Documentation of the procedures performed (compilation /
  review).
- Communications with management and governance.
- The conclusions reached and the basis for them.
- Any consultations.

Documentation should be sufficient for an experienced practitioner
not previously connected with the engagement to understand:
- The nature, timing, and extent of procedures performed.
- The results of those procedures.
- The conclusions reached.

Retention period: per firm policy (SQMS 1) and applicable law /
regulation. AICPA practice aid generally suggests minimum 5 years.

### Quality management (AR-C §60.A4)

The engagement is performed within a system of quality management
designed in accordance with SQMS 1 (firm-level) and, when
applicable, SQMS 2 (engagement quality reviews). Route to
`compliance-attestation-qm` for SQMS 1 / SQMS 2 implementation.

### Fraud and noncompliance with laws and regulations (AR-C §60.A41)

If the practitioner becomes aware of fraud or noncompliance:
- Discuss with management at appropriate level.
- Consider implications for the engagement and for the firm's
  continuation.
- Document the matter.
- For review engagements, modify report if disclosure or accrual
  is required and management does not comply.
