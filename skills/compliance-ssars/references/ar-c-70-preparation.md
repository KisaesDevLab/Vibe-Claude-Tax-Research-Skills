# AR-C 70 — Preparation Engagements

AR-C 70 governs engagements where the practitioner prepares
financial statements but does NOT issue any report and does NOT
provide any assurance. The "preparation engagement" is the lowest-
service tier under SSARS.

## When AR-C 70 applies

- Practitioner prepares the financial statements (or substantially
  prepares them; mere mechanical assistance is not a preparation
  engagement).
- Practitioner does NOT issue a compilation, review, or audit
  report.
- Engagement is for a nonissuer.

AR-C 70 does NOT apply when:
- The practitioner only assists with bookkeeping (no F/S preparation).
- The practitioner issues a compilation report (AR-C 80 governs).
- The practitioner is engaged for review (AR-C 90) or audit
  (AU-C 200+).

## Engagement letter (AR-C §70.10)

Required before the engagement begins. Beyond AR-C 60.A21 elements,
the AR-C 70 engagement letter must include:
- A statement that the engagement is to prepare financial statements
  and is conducted in accordance with SSARS.
- Specification that the practitioner is NOT required to verify
  accuracy or completeness of information provided.
- Notation that NO assurance is provided.
- Statement that each page of the financial statements will include
  a "no assurance is provided" legend (or that a disclaimer will be
  issued in lieu of the per-page legend).

## Required procedures (AR-C §70.13–.16)

### Understanding of the framework

Practitioner must obtain an understanding of the financial
reporting framework and of the significant accounting policies the
client uses or will use. If the framework is unfamiliar, the
practitioner must acquire competence (per AR-C 60).

### Preparation of the financial statements

Practitioner prepares F/S based on records and information provided
by management. Practitioner is NOT required to verify accuracy or
completeness; HOWEVER:
- If information appears incorrect, incomplete, or otherwise
  unsatisfactory, the practitioner must request management to
  provide additional or corrected information.
- If management does not provide it, the practitioner must withdraw
  or modify the work product appropriately.

### "No assurance" legend (AR-C §70.14)

Each page of the F/S must include a statement such as:
- "No assurance is provided on these financial statements."
- "These financial statements have not been subjected to an audit,
  review, or compilation engagement, and no assurance is provided."

If the practitioner cannot include this legend on each page (e.g.,
client uses an off-the-shelf accounting package that doesn't
permit a footer), the practitioner must:
- Issue a disclaimer of opinion that accompanies the F/S, OR
- Perform a compilation engagement instead, OR
- Decline the engagement.

### Departure from the framework (AR-C §70.A19)

If the practitioner becomes aware of a material departure from the
framework, the practitioner should:
- Propose appropriate revisions.
- If management refuses revisions: disclose the departure in the
  F/S, withdraw, or change the engagement to a compilation with
  modified report.

### Use of specified elements / supplementary info (AR-C §70.A21)

If the engagement is limited to specified elements, accounts, or
items rather than a complete set of F/S, the practitioner should
ensure the financial information is identified clearly (e.g.,
"Schedule of Cash Receipts and Disbursements — January 1 to
December 31, 20XX").

## Documentation (AR-C §70.18)

Engagement file must include:
- The engagement letter.
- The financial statements with the "no assurance" legend (or
  alternative).
- Significant findings or issues, communications with management.
- A copy of any specified elements / supplementary information
  prepared.

## Independence

AR-C 70 does NOT require independence. The practitioner may prepare
F/S for an entity with which the firm has independence-impairing
relationships. (Compare AR-C 80 compilation, which permits lack of
independence with disclosure; and AR-C 90 review, which REQUIRES
independence.)

## When NOT to use AR-C 70

- If the client expects a report → use AR-C 80 (compilation).
- If the client expects assurance → use AR-C 90 (review) or AU-C
  (audit).
- If the practitioner is engaged only to type up information from
  client trial balance with no F/S preparation judgment → not a
  SSARS engagement at all (mere bookkeeping or mechanical
  assistance).

## Common practitioner errors

- Issuing a "preparation report" — there is no such report under
  AR-C 70. The "no assurance" legend on each page is the only
  required communication.
- Performing AR-C 70 procedures and then signing a compilation
  report — if a report is issued, use AR-C 80, not AR-C 70.
- Skipping the engagement letter — engagement letter is mandatory
  before work begins (AR-C 60.A21 + AR-C 70.10).
