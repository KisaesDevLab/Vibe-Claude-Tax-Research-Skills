# AR-C 90 — Review Engagements

AR-C 90 governs review engagements: practitioner provides LIMITED
assurance via inquiry and analytical procedures and issues a review
report. Independence is REQUIRED. There is NO lack-of-independence
path for a review engagement.

## When AR-C 90 applies

- Nonissuer entity engaged the firm for a review (limited
  assurance).
- Firm is INDEPENDENT (AICPA Code §1.200; if not, decline or route
  to compilation).
- Engagement will result in a review report.

## Engagement letter (AR-C §90.10)

Beyond AR-C 60.A21 elements:
- Statement that the objective of a review is to obtain limited
  assurance as a basis for reporting whether the practitioner is
  aware of any material modifications that should be made to the
  F/S for them to be in accordance with the framework.
- Description of the inquiry + analytical procedures the
  practitioner will perform.
- Statement that the engagement is performed in accordance with
  SSARS.
- Affirmation that the firm IS independent.
- Statement that the engagement is substantially less in scope than
  an audit.

## Required procedures (AR-C §90.A50–.A95)

### Risk assessment and planning

- Obtain understanding of the entity, its environment, internal
  control (limited; less than required for an audit), and the
  framework.
- Identify areas where material modifications might be necessary.

### Inquiry procedures (AR-C §90.A50)

Inquiry of members of management who have responsibility for
financial and accounting matters about:
- Significant accounting policies and any changes.
- Procedures for recording, classifying, and summarizing
  transactions.
- Transactions with related parties.
- Subsequent events.
- Knowledge of fraud or suspected fraud affecting the entity.
- Knowledge of any allegations of fraud or noncompliance.
- Communications from regulatory agencies.
- Litigation, claims, and assessments.
- Significant journal entries and other adjustments.
- Communications from regulatory agencies, if any.
- Any unusual or complex transactions.
- Going-concern conditions.

### Analytical procedures (AR-C §90.A75)

Apply analytical procedures designed to identify relationships and
individual items that appear unusual:
- Compare current-period to prior-period information.
- Compare to budgets or forecasts.
- Study financial relationships (ratios) expected to conform to
  predictable patterns.
- Compare to industry norms (where appropriate and available).

### Investigation of unusual matters (AR-C §90.A82)

For matters that come to the practitioner's attention that appear
to be incorrect, incomplete, or otherwise unsatisfactory:
- Make additional inquiries.
- Perform additional procedures.
- Resolve the matter or modify the review report.

### Going concern (AR-C §90.65)

Required to perform going-concern procedures consistent with the
framework's going-concern guidance (GAAP: ASC 205-40).
- Evaluate management's plan to mitigate substantial doubt.
- If substantial doubt exists and is appropriately disclosed:
  emphasis-of-matter paragraph in review report.
- If substantial doubt exists and is NOT appropriately disclosed:
  qualified or adverse review conclusion.

### Subsequent events (AR-C §90.A89)

Inquire about events occurring between the date of the F/S and the
date of the review report. Modify the F/S or report disclosures
as needed.

### Written representations (AR-C §90.A95)

Obtain written representations from management dated as of the
date of the review report covering:
- Management's responsibility for the F/S and for designing,
  implementing, and maintaining internal control.
- Management's responsibility for preventing and detecting fraud.
- All financial records and related data have been made available.
- Significant assumptions are reasonable.
- Related-party transactions disclosed.
- Subsequent events disclosed.
- Other matters specific to the entity / framework.

## Review report (AR-C §90.A99–.A116)

Standard review report (illustrative only):

> "[I/We] have reviewed the accompanying financial statements of
> [Company X], which comprise the [balance sheets] as of [dates] and
> the related [statements of income, retained earnings, and cash
> flows] for the [periods] then ended, and the related notes to the
> financial statements. A review includes primarily applying
> analytical procedures to management's financial data and making
> inquiries of company management. A review is substantially less
> in scope than an audit, the objective of which is the expression
> of an opinion regarding the financial statements as a whole.
> Accordingly, [I/we] do not express such an opinion.
>
> Management is responsible for the preparation and fair
> presentation of the financial statements in accordance with
> [framework]; this includes the design, implementation, and
> maintenance of internal control relevant to the preparation and
> fair presentation of the financial statements that are free from
> material misstatement, whether due to fraud or error.
>
> [My/Our] responsibility is to conduct the review engagement in
> accordance with Statements on Standards for Accounting and Review
> Services promulgated by the Accounting and Review Services
> Committee of the AICPA. Those standards require [me/us] to
> perform procedures to obtain limited assurance as a basis for
> reporting whether [I am/we are] aware of any material
> modifications that should be made to the financial statements
> for them to be in accordance with [framework]. [I/We] believe
> that the results of [my/our] procedures provide a reasonable
> basis for [my/our] conclusion.
>
> Based on [my/our] review, [I am / we are] not aware of any
> material modifications that should be made to the accompanying
> financial statements in order for them to be in accordance with
> [framework]."

## Modifications (AR-C §90.A105–.A116)

- **Departure from framework (material)**: qualified conclusion or
  adverse conclusion.
- **Scope limitation**: usually withdraw; if circumstances permit,
  qualified conclusion.
- **Going-concern uncertainty**: emphasis-of-matter paragraph if
  appropriately disclosed; qualified / adverse if not.
- **Other emphasis-of-matter**: management has changed framework,
  related-party transactions material, etc.

## Documentation (AR-C §90.A123)

Engagement file must include:
- The engagement letter.
- The F/S and the review report.
- Documentation of inquiries, analytical procedures, and the
  results.
- Documentation of going-concern evaluation, written
  representations, subsequent-events procedures.
- Significant findings, communications with management.
- Documentation of consultation and resolution of significant
  matters.
- Engagement quality review documentation if SQMS 2 applies.

## Independence (mandatory)

Independence per AICPA Code §1.200 is REQUIRED. Route any
threats-and-safeguards questions to `compliance-aicpa-code`. If
independence is impaired, the firm:
- Cannot perform the review.
- May offer a compilation under AR-C 80 with lack-of-independence
  disclosure.
- May decline.
