# AR-C 80 — Compilation Engagements

AR-C 80 governs compilation engagements: practitioner assists
management in presenting financial information in the form of
financial statements and issues a compilation report. NO assurance
is provided. Compilation is the only SSARS engagement that
permits performance without independence (with required
disclosure).

## When AR-C 80 applies

- Practitioner is engaged to perform a compilation of nonissuer
  F/S.
- Practitioner will issue a compilation report.

## Engagement letter (AR-C §80.10)

Beyond AR-C 60.A21 elements:
- Statement that the engagement is to compile financial statements
  and is performed in accordance with SSARS.
- Statement that the engagement does NOT contemplate obtaining or
  providing any assurance.
- Statement that the practitioner is NOT required to perform
  procedures to verify accuracy or completeness.
- If lack of independence: statement that the firm is not
  independent and the report will so disclose (AR-C 80.27).

## Required procedures (AR-C §80.14–.A18)

### Reading the F/S

Read the financial statements and consider whether they:
- Are appropriate in form for the financial reporting framework.
- Are free from obvious material errors.

### Inquiry / additional procedures NOT required

The practitioner is NOT required to:
- Make inquiries of management.
- Perform analytical procedures.
- Test internal controls.
- Verify or corroborate information.

### Departure from the framework (AR-C §80.A29)

If a known departure from the framework is material:
- Propose revisions.
- If management refuses, modify the compilation report by adding a
  paragraph that discloses the departure and its effects (if known).
- If departure is so material that revision would substantially
  re-prepare the F/S, withdraw or change the engagement.

### Going concern (AR-C §80.A24)

If conditions or events that raise substantial doubt about the
entity's ability to continue as a going concern come to the
practitioner's attention, AND management has not addressed them
in the F/S adequately:
- Request management to consider the matter and reflect it in the
  F/S as required by the framework (e.g., ASC 205-40).
- If management does not, modify the compilation report or withdraw.

## Compilation report (AR-C §80.A8–.A18)

Standard compilation report (illustrative only — practitioner must
use AR-C 80 illustrative example for the engagement's specific
framework):

> "Management is responsible for the accompanying financial
> statements of [Company X] which comprise the [balance sheets] as
> of [dates] and the related [statements of income, retained
> earnings, and cash flows] for the [periods] then ended in
> accordance with [framework]. [I/We] have performed the compilation
> engagement in accordance with Statements on Standards for
> Accounting and Review Services promulgated by the Accounting and
> Review Services Committee of the AICPA. [I/We] did not audit or
> review the financial statements nor [was/were I/we] required to
> perform any procedures to verify the accuracy or completeness of
> the information provided by management. Accordingly, [I/we] do
> not express an opinion, a conclusion, nor provide any form of
> assurance on these financial statements."
> [Signature, City and State, Date]

## Lack of independence (AR-C §80.27)

If the firm is NOT independent, the practitioner MAY still issue a
compilation report. The report must include a paragraph disclosing
the lack of independence:

> "[I am / We are] not independent with respect to [Company X]."

The practitioner has options for disclosing reasons for the lack of
independence:
- Disclose all reasons.
- Disclose no reasons (just the fact of non-independence).
- Disclose specific reasons but not all.

If the practitioner chooses to disclose reasons, ALL substantive
reasons must be disclosed (cannot cherry-pick favorable ones).

NOTE: Lack of independence does NOT permit a review (AR-C 90) or
an audit (AU-C). For review engagements, the firm MUST be
independent; if not, route to compilation with disclosure or
decline.

## Special-purpose framework F/S (AR-C §80.A33)

If the F/S are prepared on a special-purpose framework (tax basis,
cash basis, regulatory basis, contractual basis, other), the
compilation report must include:
- Reference to the framework used.
- Statement that the framework is a basis of accounting other than
  GAAP.
- For regulatory basis: description of the regulatory basis and
  intended users.
- For contractual basis: description of the contractual basis and
  intended users.

## Documentation (AR-C §80.30)

Engagement file must include:
- The engagement letter.
- A copy of the F/S and the compilation report.
- Significant findings, communications with management.
- Documentation of consultations and resolution of significant
  matters.
