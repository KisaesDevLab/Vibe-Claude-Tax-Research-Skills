# ASC 740-10-25 — UTP Recognition and Measurement (FIN 48)

ASC 740-10-25 (formerly FIN 48) establishes the two-step
recognition-and-measurement framework for uncertain tax positions
(UTPs).

## Step 1 — Recognition (ASC 740-10-25-6)

A tax position is recognized in F/S only if:
- It is more-likely-than-not (MLTN; > 50%) of being sustained on
  examination by the taxing authority based on the technical
  merits, ASSUMING the examiner has full knowledge of all
  relevant information.

The MLTN evaluation:
- Based on TECHNICAL merits, NOT examination probability or
  audit-lottery considerations.
- Considers all relevant facts and circumstances.
- Considers all applicable authorities (IRC, Treas. Reg., case
  law, IRS published guidance).
- Considers any precedent (prior IRS treatment, prior court
  rulings).

Cross-reference: route to `tax-research-federal` for the
substantial-authority analysis underlying the technical merits.
The ASC 740 MLTN threshold (> 50%) is HIGHER than the substantial-
authority threshold (~ 40%) but LOWER than reasonable-basis
threshold (~ 20%) in §6662 terms.

## Step 2 — Measurement (ASC 740-10-25-13)

If recognized, measure the position at the LARGEST AMOUNT of tax
benefit that is more-than-50%-likely of being realized upon
ultimate settlement with the taxing authority that has full
knowledge of all relevant information.

The cumulative-probability approach:
- Identify the range of possible outcomes upon ultimate
  settlement (full disallowance to full recognition, with
  potential intermediate settlements).
- Estimate the probability of each outcome.
- Calculate the cumulative probability from the largest amount
  downward.
- The recognized amount is the largest amount where cumulative
  probability ≥ 50%.

Example: Position is for a $100 deduction.
- 30% probability of full $100 recognition.
- 30% probability of $80 settlement.
- 20% probability of $50 settlement.
- 20% probability of $0 (full disallowance).

Cumulative from largest:
- $100: 30% (NOT > 50%)
- $80 or more: 60% (> 50%) ✓
- Recognized amount: $80.

Unrecognized tax benefit (UTB) = $20 ($100 - $80).

## Subsequent measurement — changes in judgment

ASC 740-10-25-7 / 25-15: changes in measurement (or
derecognition) reflect new information, new judgments, or
settlement with the taxing authority. Recognized as a discrete
item in the period of change.

If a position is settled (closed examination, statute expired),
derecognition or remeasurement is appropriate.

## Statute-of-limitations expiration

Upon expiration of the statute of limitations:
- The position is no longer "uncertain" with respect to that
  taxing authority.
- Derecognize the UTB and recognize the previously-unrecognized
  benefit in the period the statute expires.
- Disclose this as a reduction in UTB during the year.

## Penalties and interest

- INTEREST: ASC 740-10-25-56 / -57 — companies have an
  accounting policy choice to classify interest on uncertain tax
  positions as (a) part of income tax expense or (b) interest
  expense.
- PENALTIES: ASC 740-10-25-58 — accounting policy choice to
  classify as (a) part of income tax expense or (b) other
  expense.
- Disclose policy choice and effects.

## Required disclosures (ASC 740-10-50)

For UTPs:
- Tabular reconciliation of unrecognized tax benefits beginning-
  to-end of period.
- Total UTB that, if recognized, would affect the effective tax
  rate.
- Total amount of accrued interest and penalties.
- Description of tax years still subject to examination.
- Estimate of UTB-change reasonably possible within the next 12
  months.
- Description of significant uncertain positions if useful to F/S
  users.

ASU 2023-09 effective for PBE Dec. 15, 2024; non-PBE Dec. 15,
2025. Adds:
- Specified categories in rate reconciliation (state income
  taxes, foreign tax effects, federal tax credits, etc.).
- Quantitative thresholds for disclosure.
- Income taxes paid disaggregation by jurisdiction.
