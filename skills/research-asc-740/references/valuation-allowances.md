# ASC 740 — Valuation Allowances on DTAs

A valuation allowance (VA) reduces a deferred tax asset (DTA)
when it is more likely than not that some or all of the DTA will
not be realized.

## When required (ASC 740-10-30-17)

A valuation allowance is required when, based on the WEIGHT of
all available evidence, it is more likely than not (MLTN) that
some or all of the DTA will NOT be realized.

"Available evidence" includes both positive and negative
evidence; both must be evaluated.

## Sources of future taxable income (ASC 740-10-30-18)

Four sources of taxable income that can support DTA realization:

1. **Future reversals of existing taxable temporary differences**
   - DTLs that reverse in the future provide taxable income that
     can offset DTA reversal.
   - Scheduling required: when do existing DTLs reverse vs. when
     do DTAs reverse?
2. **Future taxable income** exclusive of reversing temporary
   differences and carryforwards
   - Forecasted future operating income.
   - Reliable to the extent of objectively-determinable
     evidence.
3. **Tax-planning strategies (TPS)** that, if implemented, would:
   - Be prudent and feasible.
   - Be primarily implemented to realize the DTA.
   - Result in additional taxable income.
   - TPS examples: changing from accelerated to straight-line
     depreciation; selling appreciated assets; intercompany
     loan restructurings.
4. **Carryback** to prior taxable years (where permitted by the
   tax law)
   - For NOLs: TCJA eliminated NOL carryback for years
     beginning after 2017 (with specific exceptions). CARES Act
     temporarily restored 5-year carryback for 2018–2020 NOLs.
     Verify current rules.

## Negative evidence (ASC 740-10-30-19)

Strong negative evidence:
- Cumulative losses in recent years (typical 3-year cumulative
  loss test).
- A history of operating loss or tax credit carryforwards
  expiring unused.
- Losses expected in early future years.
- Unsettled circumstances that, if unfavorable, would adversely
  affect future operations.

Cumulative losses are particularly difficult to overcome with
projections of future income. ASC 740 generally requires
"objectively-verifiable" evidence to overcome cumulative-loss
negative evidence.

## Positive evidence (ASC 740-10-30-22)

- Existing contracts or firm sales backlog producing taxable
  income.
- Excess of fair value over book value of net assets sufficient
  to realize the DTA.
- A strong earnings history exclusive of the loss creating the
  DTA, supporting the conclusion that loss is aberrational.
- Favorable resolution of significant uncertainties.

## Subsequent reversal of valuation allowance

If subsequent events provide sufficient positive evidence:
- Reverse the VA.
- Recognized in income tax expense (continuing operations) in
  the period of reversal — generally NOT through OCI / equity
  unless the original DTA was tied to OCI / equity items.
- Disclosed.

The reversal can be substantial; partial reversal is permitted
when partial DTA realization is supported.

## Specific situations

### Pass-through entity transition (S-corp election, etc.)
- C-corp electing S-corp status: existing C-corp DTAs subject to
  realization analysis on the assumption that there is no
  future C-corp income (other than built-in-gain period under
  IRC §1374). VA likely on most DTAs.
- Partnership / S-corp: ASC 740 applies only to entity-level
  taxes (e.g., state-level entity tax, BIG tax for S-corps). No
  DTAs / DTLs for federal income tax that flows through.

### Acquisitions (ASC 805-740)
- Acquired DTAs are recognized at acquisition date with VA
  evaluated using post-combination assumptions.
- Subsequent VA reversal of acquired DTA generally goes to
  income tax expense (not goodwill).

### NOL and credit carryforwards
- Indefinite-lived NOLs (post-TCJA, for tax years beginning after
  2017) may be more readily realizable than pre-TCJA NOLs that
  had 20-year limits.
- Limit on NOL deduction (80% of taxable income for post-TCJA
  NOLs) affects scheduling.
- §382 ownership change limitations on acquired NOLs further
  affect realizability.

### Foreign DTAs / DTLs
- ASC 740 applies to foreign income taxes under entity-level
  tax regimes. APB 23 (now ASC 740-30-25-17) outside-basis
  exception for foreign subsidiary undistributed earnings.
- Tax-rate differences and currency translation considerations
  (ASC 740 + ASC 830).

## Disclosures (ASC 740-10-50-2)

- Total amount of DTAs and DTLs.
- Total VA recognized.
- Net change in VA during the year.
- Approximate amount of NOLs and credit carryforwards by tax
  jurisdiction with expiration dates.
- Significant components of income tax expense for each year
  presented.
