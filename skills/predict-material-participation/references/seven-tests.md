# Seven §1.469-5T(a) material-participation tests

The taxpayer materially participates in an activity if ANY of the
seven tests below is satisfied. First test satisfied wins; cite the
specific test number in the conclusion.

## Test 1 — More than 500 hours

§1.469-5T(a)(1). The clearest test. > 500 hours during the taxable
year. Document with daily logs.

## Test 2 — Substantially all participation

§1.469-5T(a)(2). Taxpayer's participation constitutes substantially
all participation in the activity by all individuals (including
non-owners) during the year. Useful for sole-proprietor-like
ventures with no employees or vendors logging significant hours.

## Test 3 — More than 100 hours AND ≥ any other individual

§1.469-5T(a)(3). > 100 hours AND not less than any other
individual's participation. The "any other individual" clause
includes non-owners (employees, vendors). For solo-managed
properties or one-person operations, this is often the cleanest
path when test 1 is not met.

## Test 4 — Significant-participation activity (SPA) aggregation

§1.469-5T(a)(4). Activity is an SPA (taxpayer's participation
> 100 hours), AND taxpayer's participation in ALL SPAs aggregates
to > 500 hours.

To use test 4, the practitioner must:
1. Identify each activity where the taxpayer's hours exceed 100.
2. Confirm the activities are NOT material-participation activities
   under tests 1, 2, 3, 5, 6, or 7 standing alone.
3. Sum the hours across SPAs and confirm > 500.

§1.469-5T(c) defines "significant-participation activity" with
reference to (a)(4). Document each SPA's hours separately.

## Test 5 — 5 of last 10 years

§1.469-5T(a)(5). Taxpayer materially participated in the activity
for ANY 5 (consecutive or not) of the immediately preceding 10
taxable years. Useful when the taxpayer is scaling back hours
after an active history.

## Test 6 — Personal-service activity, any 3 prior years

§1.469-5T(a)(6). Activity is a personal-service activity (defined
in §1.469-5T(d) — fields of health, law, engineering, architecture,
accounting, actuarial science, performing arts, consulting, "or any
other trade or business in which capital is not a material
income-producing factor"), and taxpayer materially participated in
the activity for ANY 3 prior years.

## Test 7 — Facts and circumstances

§1.469-5T(a)(7). Based on all the facts and circumstances, the
taxpayer participates on a regular, continuous, and substantial
basis. > 100 hours required as a floor.

§1.469-5T(b)(2)(ii) excludes "investor-type" participation:
- Studying and reviewing financial statements / operations reports.
- Preparing summaries or analyses for the taxpayer's own use.
- Monitoring finances or operations in a non-managerial capacity.

## Limited-partner restriction

§1.469-5T(e)(2): A limited-partner interest is treated as passive
unless tests 1, 5, or 6 are satisfied.

LLC interests have produced litigation:
- Garnett v. Commissioner, 132 T.C. 368 (2009) — LLC member who
  has management rights treated as general partner.
- Thompson v. United States, 87 Fed. Cl. 728 (2009) — same.
- Newell v. Commissioner, T.C. Memo. 2010-23 — confirmed.
- Senra v. Commissioner, T.C. Memo. 2009-79 — same.

Proposed regulations (REG-109369-10, 2011) would have codified the
Garnett / Thompson approach but were withdrawn in 2018 without
finalization. The IRS has not formally conceded; the taxpayer's
LLC-as-GP position remains supported only by case law in the
specific contexts litigated.

## Documentation discipline

§1.469-5(f) — "any reasonable means". Practical IRS / Tax Court
expectation:

- Bailey v. Commissioner, T.C. Memo. 2001-296 — disallowed
  reconstructed log of 1,150 hours where calendar entries lacked
  detail.
- Hakkak v. Commissioner, T.C. Memo. 2020-46 — disallowed
  reconstructed estimate of 800+ hours.
- Pohoski v. Commissioner, T.C. Memo. 1998-17 — accepted
  contemporaneous appointment book.

Audit-defense file:
- Daily log of date, hours, activity description.
- Calendar / appointment cross-reference.
- Communications (emails, texts, vendor calls) date-stamped.
- For test 3: vendor / property-manager hour estimates to satisfy
  the "≥ any other individual" prong.
- For test 4: separate logs by SPA with aggregate computation.
- For tests 5 / 6: prior-year tax returns and Schedule E / K-1
  attestations supporting the historical-participation claim.

## Common audit triggers

- Reconstructed log that conflicts with the taxpayer's W-2 hours
  or known schedule.
- Test 3 claimed but a property manager / GC / vendor logs more
  hours than the taxpayer.
- Test 4 claimed but SPAs were not separately identified or
  aggregated; or SPAs include investor-type activities excluded by
  §1.469-5T(b)(2)(ii).
- Test 7 claimed for an activity that involves only investor-type
  participation.
- LLC-as-GP position taken in a limited-partner master agreement
  that retains substantial limited-partner-like restrictions.
