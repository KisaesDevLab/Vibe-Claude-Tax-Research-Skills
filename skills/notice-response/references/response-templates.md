# Notice-response letter templates

## General format

```
[Practitioner / firm letterhead]

[Date]

Internal Revenue Service
[Address from notice]

Re: [Taxpayer Name]
    [SSN/EIN — last 4 digits only]
    Tax Year(s): [YYYY]
    Notice No.: [CP XXXX / Letter NNNN]
    Notice Date: [MM/DD/YYYY]
    Response Deadline: [MM/DD/YYYY]

Dear [Examiner Name / Sir or Madam]:

This letter responds to the above-referenced notice on behalf of
[Taxpayer Name]. We are authorized to represent the taxpayer
pursuant to Form 2848 [filed MM/DD/YYYY / submitted herewith].

[POSITION STATEMENT — agree / partial agree / disagree]

[STATEMENT OF FACTS]

[APPLICATION OF LAW TO FACTS]

[REQUESTED RELIEF]

[CLOSING]

Sincerely,

[Practitioner Name, CPA / EA]
[License #]
[Phone / email]

Enclosures:
1. Form 2848 (if not previously filed)
2. [Substantiation documents]
3. [Additional exhibits]
```

## CP2000 disagree template (substantiation-based)

```
We respectfully disagree with the proposed adjustment of
$[amount] for [year]. The Form 1099-[type] referenced in the
notice reports $[amount] of [income type] from [Payor]; the
taxpayer's records confirm receipt of $[amount] which was
properly reported on Schedule [letter] Line [number] of the
[year] Form 1040.

The proposed adjustment double-counts the income. Enclosed are
the following substantiating records:

1. Schedule [letter] page from the original return showing the
   reported amount.
2. Form 1099-[type] from [Payor] dated [MM/DD/YYYY].
3. [Bank statement / brokerage statement / other reconciliation]
   tracing the $[amount] to its reporting line.

We respectfully request the proposed adjustment be withdrawn.
```

## Letter 525 (30-day) — request for Appeals Conference

```
We respectfully disagree with the examination findings
concerning [issue summary]. Pursuant to Pub. 5 and IRM 8.6, we
request an Appeals Conference to address the disputed
adjustments.

Disputed adjustments and brief grounds for objection:

1. [Adjustment 1] — $[amount] — [grounds: e.g., the deduction
   was properly substantiated under §170(f)(8) per the appraisal
   submitted at examination; the appraiser meets §1.170A-17
   qualified-appraiser requirements].

2. [Adjustment 2] — $[amount] — [grounds].

[Statement of facts as to each disputed adjustment.]

[Application of law: cite IRC, Treas. Regs., case law,
Rev. Ruls. supporting taxpayer's position.]

We have a good-faith basis for the positions taken and request
the opportunity to present our case to Appeals.
```

## Letter 1153 (TFRP) protest

```
We respectfully protest the proposed assessment of the Trust
Fund Recovery Penalty against [Individual Name] for the trust-
fund liability of [Entity] for tax periods [periods].

[Individual Name] respectfully contends:

1. [Individual Name] was not a "responsible person" within the
   meaning of §6672 and Treas. Reg. §301.6672-1 because:
   - [Specific factor — no check-signing authority, no financial
     decision-making authority, etc.];
   - [Specific factor].

2. Even if [Individual Name] were considered a responsible person,
   the failure to pay over trust-fund tax was not "willful" within
   the meaning of Pomponio v. United States, 429 U.S. 10 (1976),
   because:
   - [Specific factor — no knowledge of unpaid liability at the
     time other creditors were paid, etc.];
   - [Specific factor].

[Statement of facts.]

[Application of law.]

We request that the proposed assessment be withdrawn and the case
returned to the field for further investigation, or in the
alternative that we be afforded an Appeals Conference to address
these issues.

This protest is filed within 60 days of the [date] Letter 1153.
```

## Reasonable-cause / FTA request

```
We respectfully request abatement of the [§6651(a)(1) / §6651(a)(2) /
§6699 / §6698] penalty assessed against [Taxpayer] for [period]
in the amount of $[amount].

[FTA option:]
We request abatement under the First-Time Penalty Abatement
administrative waiver per IRM 20.1.1.3.6. The taxpayer satisfies
all four conditions:
1. The penalty is for a [§6651 family] failure.
2. The taxpayer filed all required returns AND paid (or arranged
   payment for) any tax due for tax years [prior 3 years].
3. The taxpayer has not used FTA in the prior 3 years.
4. This is a single-period penalty.

[Reasonable-cause option:]
In the alternative, we request abatement under §6651's reasonable-
cause exception. The taxpayer's failure to [file / pay] timely
was due to circumstances beyond his control:

[Statement of facts: serious illness, casualty, etc., with
contemporaneous documentation.]

The taxpayer exercised "ordinary business care and prudence"
within the meaning of Treas. Reg. §301.6651-1(c)(1) by:
[Specific actions taken].

Enclosed:
1. [Documentation of cause — medical records, casualty report,
   etc.];
2. Compliance history for [prior years];
3. [Other supporting documents].
```

## CDP request (Form 12153 narrative)

```
[Filed via Form 12153 with this narrative attached]

[Taxpayer Name] respectfully requests a Collection Due Process
Hearing under §[6320 / 6330] in response to the [Letter 3172
Notice of Federal Tax Lien Filing / Letter 1058 Final Notice of
Intent to Levy / CP90 Final Notice] dated [MM/DD/YYYY].

Issues to be addressed at the hearing:

1. [Verification] — verify that the [lien filing / levy
   issuance] was procedurally proper.

2. [Spousal defense if applicable] — the taxpayer has filed Form
   8857 requesting innocent-spouse relief under §6015 [and / or
   the existing Form 8857 has not been adjudicated].

3. [Underlying liability if not previously challenged] — the
   taxpayer did not have a prior opportunity to challenge the
   underlying liability for [period] because [no statutory notice
   was received / receipt was disputed].

4. [Collection alternative] — the taxpayer requests consideration
   of [installment agreement / OIC / currently-not-collectible /
   release of levy with payment plan].

5. [Hardship] — [if applicable, document financial hardship].

We request a face-to-face hearing at [Office of Appeals
location].
```

## Tax Court petition (Letter 3219 response)

This is a JURISDICTIONAL filing; format per Tax Court Rule 34. The
notice-response skill identifies the deadline and recommends the
filing path. Drafting the petition itself is OUT OF SCOPE for this
skill — recommend engagement of Tax Court counsel for the
substantive petition.

Key elements (for orientation only):
- Caption: "[Taxpayer Name], Petitioner v. Commissioner of
  Internal Revenue, Respondent."
- Filing fee: $60 (verify current).
- Petition contents: (a) name and address of petitioner; (b)
  statutory notice attached; (c) assignment of errors; (d)
  statement of facts; (e) requested relief.
- Filing methods: DAWSON e-filing or paper to Tax Court at 400
  Second Street NW, Washington DC.
