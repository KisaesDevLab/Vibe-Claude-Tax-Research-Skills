# §6015 relief pathways — practitioner reference

## Three pathways at a glance

| Pathway | IRC | Eligibility focus | Relief type |
|---|---|---|---|
| Traditional innocent-spouse | §6015(b) | No knowledge of understatement; equitable | Full relief from joint liability for understatement |
| Separation of liability | §6015(c) | Divorced / separated / 12-months apart | Allocation as if separate returns |
| Equitable relief | §6015(f) | Catch-all when (b)/(c) unavailable | Full or partial relief based on facts |

Form 8857 lets the requesting spouse claim all three at once;
the IRS routes to the most-applicable pathway.

## Statute of limitations

| Pathway | Deadline |
|---|---|
| §6015(b) | 2 years from first collection activity |
| §6015(c) | 2 years from first collection activity |
| §6015(f) | 10-year §6502 collection statute (Notice 2011-70 abandoned 2-year regulatory deadline) |

Lantz v. Commissioner, 132 T.C. 131 (2009), rev'd 607 F.3d 479
(7th Cir. 2010), and Mannella v. Commissioner, 132 T.C. 196
(2009), aff'd 631 F.3d 115 (3d Cir. 2011), validated the IRS's
former 2-year regulatory deadline; subsequently the IRS
abandoned it in Notice 2011-70 and Rev. Proc. 2013-34.

## Knowledge / reason to know

The most-litigated factor across all three pathways.

### §6015(b)(1)(C) — "did not know, and had no reason to know"

Reasonable person in the requesting spouse's circumstances would
not have known of the understatement. Treas. Reg. §1.6015-2(c)
factors:
- Nature of erroneous item; complexity of return.
- Financial situation of requesting spouse.
- Educational background.
- Involvement in business or financial affairs leading to error.
- Departure from a prior pattern of expense or income.
- Spouse's evasiveness or deceit.

### §6015(c)(3)(C) — actual-knowledge bar

For separation-of-liability allocation, the rule is stricter:
ACTUAL knowledge of the item bars allocation, even if the
requesting spouse did not know it was erroneous. Constructive
knowledge alone does not bar §6015(c).

### §6015(f) facts-and-circumstances knowledge factor

Rev. Proc. 2013-34 §4.03(2)(c) treats knowledge as one factor
among many. Critical: §4.03(2)(c)(ii) — "Abuse or financial
control":

> "If the requesting spouse establishes that he or she was the
> victim of abuse prior to the time the return was signed, and
> that, as a result of the prior abuse, the requesting spouse
> did not challenge the treatment of any items on the return ...
> for fear of the nonrequesting spouse's retaliation, this factor
> will weigh in favor of relief even if the requesting spouse
> knew or had reason to know of the items giving rise to the
> understatement or deficiency."

Abuse can ALSO override unfavorable factors like:
- Lack of significant economic harm.
- Significant benefit received.
- Lack of compliance with subsequent returns.

## Rev. Proc. 2013-34 streamlined relief (§4.02)

If ALL three met, relief granted without further facts-and-
circumstances analysis:

1. Marital status: divorced, legally separated, OR ≥ 12 months
   not residing together at the time of the request.
2. Economic hardship if relief denied (per §6343(e) — inability
   to pay reasonable basic living expenses).
3. No knowledge / reason to know of the item or underpayment;
   abuse / financial control overrides.

## Rev. Proc. 2013-34 facts-and-circumstances (§4.03)

If streamlined unavailable, balance:

Pro-relief factors:
- Marital status (divorced / separated favors relief).
- Economic hardship if denied.
- No knowledge / reason to know.
- No legal obligation under divorce decree.
- No significant benefit received from the item.
- Compliance with subsequent years.
- Mental / physical health limitations.
- Abuse history.

Anti-relief factors (mirror images):
- Still married.
- No economic hardship.
- Knew or had reason to know.
- Divorce decree assigns liability.
- Received significant benefit.
- Out of compliance after the relief year.

No factor is dispositive; the IRS weights based on the totality.

## Significant-benefit analysis

"Significant benefit" goes beyond normal household expenses.
Typical bar: a vacation, a car, an education that the requesting
spouse received as a direct result of the unreported income or
the unpaid tax.

Living in a household whose expenses were paid from unreported
income is NOT itself "significant benefit" for §6015(f) purposes;
the IRS distinguishes baseline household support from windfall
benefits.

## Abuse and financial control

Rev. Proc. 2013-34 §4.03(2)(c)(ii) recognizes:
- Physical abuse.
- Psychological abuse.
- Sexual abuse.
- Financial control (one spouse withholds money, prevents
  employment, controls all financial decisions).

Documentation:
- Police reports / restraining orders.
- Therapy records.
- Affidavits from neighbors, family, friends.
- Bank-account access records demonstrating financial control.
- Communications evidencing intimidation.

The IRS treats abuse claims confidentially under Form 8857's
abuse-disclosure section. Practitioners should facilitate
disclosure without re-traumatizing the requesting spouse.

## Tax Court review under §6015(e)

If the IRS denies relief at the appeals level, the requesting
spouse has 90 days from the notice of final determination to
petition the Tax Court. The Tax Court reviews:
- §6015(b) and (c): scope-of-review under §6015(e)(1).
- §6015(f): de novo standard (post-Pension Protection Act of 2006);
  no deference to the IRS determination.

A timely §6015(e) petition is the only way to preserve judicial
review; appeal-level denial alone does not exhaust remedies for
collection-due-process purposes.

## Refund of payments

If the requesting spouse has already paid the tax and obtains
relief, refunds are limited:
- §6015(b) and (f): refunds available subject to §6511 limitations.
- §6015(c): NO refund — only prospective relief from the
  allocated portion not yet paid.

## Form 8857 mechanics

- Filed at any IRS office within the deadline windows.
- Triggers IRS notification of the nonrequesting spouse with an
  opportunity to participate.
- The nonrequesting spouse's intervention rights are limited
  (§6015(g)).
- Confidential information about abuse can be redacted from the
  nonrequesting spouse's notice copy.

## Practitioner checklist

- [ ] Joint return for relief year confirmed.
- [ ] Threshold conditions per Rev. Proc. 2013-34 §4.01 verified.
- [ ] Filing deadline determined (2-year for (b)/(c); 10-year §6502
  for (f)).
- [ ] Streamlined relief conditions evaluated first.
- [ ] If streamlined unavailable, full facts-and-circumstances
  factor analysis documented.
- [ ] Abuse-override considered if applicable (Rev. Proc.
  2013-34 §4.03(2)(c)(ii)).
- [ ] Form 8857 prepared with confidential abuse disclosure if
  applicable.
- [ ] §6015(e) 90-day Tax Court petition path preserved if denied.
