# §199A SSTB analysis

## Statutory list (§199A(d)(2)(A))

The following are Specified Service Trades or Businesses; income
is disqualified above the upper income threshold:

- Health
- Law
- Accounting
- Actuarial science
- Performing arts
- Consulting
- Athletics
- Financial services
- Brokerage services
- Any trade or business where the principal asset is the
  reputation or skill of one or more of its employees or owners
- Investing and investment management
- Trading or dealing in securities, partnership interests, or
  commodities

## Health (Treas. Reg. §1.199A-5(b)(2)(ii))

INCLUDED: physicians, pharmacists, nurses, dentists, veterinarians,
physical therapists, psychologists, "and other similar healthcare
professionals performing services in their capacity as such".

EXCLUDED: services not directly related to a medical-services field
(operation of health clubs / spas, payment processing, research /
testing of pharmaceuticals or medical devices).

## Law (§1.199A-5(b)(2)(iii))

INCLUDED: lawyers, paralegals, legal arbitrators, mediators, "and
similar professionals performing services in their capacity as
such".

EXCLUDED: providing services that do not require legal training
(printing, delivery, stenography).

## Accounting (§1.199A-5(b)(2)(iv))

INCLUDED: accountants, enrolled agents, return preparers,
financial auditors, "and similar professionals performing services
in their capacity as such" — regardless of state licensure.

EXCLUDED: payment processing or billing analysis.

## Consulting (§1.199A-5(b)(2)(vii))

INCLUDED: providing professional advice and counsel to assist a
client in achieving goals.

EXCLUDED: consulting that is "embedded in" or "ancillary to" the
sale of goods or services if there is no separate payment for the
consulting services. The "ancillary" exclusion is the most-
litigated edge of the SSTB rule.

## Financial services (§1.199A-5(b)(2)(ix))

INCLUDED: financial advice, investment-banking, wealth-management,
retirement planning, M&A advisory.

EXCLUDED: banking — taking deposits or making loans.

## Brokerage services (§1.199A-5(b)(2)(x))

INCLUDED: services for which a person acts as an agent for the
sale of securities (as defined in §475(c)(2)).

EXCLUDED: real-estate brokerage, insurance brokerage.

## Investing and investment management (§1.199A-5(b)(2)(xi))

INCLUDED: receiving fees for providing investing, asset-management,
or investment-management services, including providing advice on
buying / selling investments.

EXCLUDED: directly managing real property.

## Reputation or skill (§1.199A-5(b)(2)(xiv))

NARROWED to three situations only:

1. Endorsement income.
2. Licensing the use of an individual's likeness, name, or voice.
3. Appearance fees, including media appearances.

Service businesses outside the enumerated list are NOT SSTBs
merely because they involve professional skill (e.g., a
pet-grooming salon or a custom-furniture shop is not an SSTB even
if reputation drives sales). This narrowing was the most
practitioner-relevant element of the final regulations.

## De-minimis rule (§1.199A-5(c)(1))

A trade or business is NOT treated as an SSTB if:

- Gross receipts < $25M and < 10% of gross receipts attributable to
  SSTB performance; OR
- Gross receipts ≥ $25M and < 5% of gross receipts attributable to
  SSTB performance.

If the de-minimis threshold is exceeded, the ENTIRE trade or
business is treated as an SSTB; there is no allocation. Proper
structuring (e.g., separating an SSTB activity into a sister entity
under common ownership) requires close attention to the
crack-and-pack anti-abuse rules at §1.199A-5(c)(2).

## Anti-abuse: crack-and-pack (§1.199A-5(c)(2))

If a trade or business that would otherwise be an SSTB:
- Has 50% or more common ownership (direct or indirect) with a
  separate non-SSTB trade or business; AND
- Provides 80% or more of its property or services to the SSTB; OR
- Shares facilities or significant centralized business elements,

then the non-SSTB is treated as part of the SSTB to the extent it
provides services to the SSTB. The 80% rule is well-known; the
anti-cracking provisions go further than the 80% test by also
catching common facilities.

## Phase-in

Within the threshold phase-in (e.g., MFJ $383,900 to $483,900 for
2024), the SSTB disqualification phases in proportionally. Verify
year-specific thresholds via the year-appropriate Rev. Proc.

## Practitioner audit-defense file

- Gross-receipts breakdown supporting the de-minimis test.
- Documentation of SSTB-vs-non-SSTB activity classification.
- For "ancillary" carve-outs (e.g., a software company that
  consults during deployment): contracts and revenue allocation
  separating consulting from core service.
- For crack-and-pack structures: ownership chart, services /
  facilities provided to the SSTB sister entity, anti-abuse
  analysis under §1.199A-5(c)(2).
