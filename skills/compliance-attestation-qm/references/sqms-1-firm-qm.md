# SQMS 1 — Firm's System of Quality Management

SQMS 1 ("A Firm's System of Quality Management") is the foundation
of the AICPA quality-management framework. It applies to firms
that perform engagements in accordance with SAS, SSARS, or SSAE.
Implementation deadline: December 15, 2025.

## Scope (SQMS 1)

SQMS 1 supersedes the prior QC Section standards and reorganizes
firm-level quality management into a **risk-based** framework.
Firms identify quality risks specific to their practice, design
responses, and continuously monitor and remediate.

## The eight components (SQMS 1)

1. **Risk assessment process** — firm identifies quality
   objectives, then identifies quality risks that could prevent
   achieving those objectives, then designs responses.
2. **Governance and leadership** — firm leadership commits to
   quality, allocates resources, and is accountable.
3. **Relevant ethical requirements** — firm complies with AICPA
   Code, state board rules, and any other applicable ethical
   frameworks. Independence is monitored.
4. **Acceptance and continuance of client relationships and
   specific engagements** — firm screens new clients and
   engagements, and reassesses continuance for existing clients.
5. **Engagement performance** — firm has policies for direction,
   supervision, review, consultation, differences of opinion,
   engagement documentation.
6. **Resources** — human resources (training, competence),
   technological resources, intellectual resources, service
   provider arrangements.
7. **Information and communication** — firm enables information
   flow within and outside the firm to support quality.
8. **Monitoring and remediation process** — firm monitors the
   system, identifies deficiencies, and remediates.

## Risk-based design (the heart of SQMS 1)

The firm follows this iterative process:
1. **Establish quality objectives** — what does quality look like
   for this firm's practice?
2. **Identify quality risks** — what could prevent achieving the
   quality objectives?
3. **Design responses** — what policies and procedures will the
   firm implement to address the risks?
4. **Implement** — operationalize the responses.
5. **Monitor** — does the system operate as designed?
6. **Remediate** — when deficiencies are identified, fix them.

## Documentation (SQMS 1)

The firm documents:
- The system of quality management.
- The quality risks identified and the responses.
- Monitoring activities and findings.
- Deficiencies and remediation.
- Annual evaluation of the system's effectiveness.
- Communications about quality matters.

## Annual evaluation

Each year, the firm's leadership performs an evaluation of the
system of quality management and concludes whether:
- The system provides reasonable assurance that:
  - The firm and its personnel fulfill responsibilities under
    professional standards and applicable legal/regulatory
    requirements.
  - Engagements are performed and reports issued appropriately.

If deficiencies prevent reasonable assurance, the firm must
remediate.

## Peer review interaction

SQMS 1 implementation is **peer-review scrutinized**. Firms
undergoing peer review (under the AICPA Peer Review Program or
state-board equivalent) will have their SQMS 1 system evaluated.
Peer reviewers examine:
- Whether the firm has designed an SQMS 1 system.
- Whether the system is operating effectively.
- Whether deficiencies are being identified and remediated.

Peer-review findings of SQMS 1 deficiencies can result in
remediation requirements, additional monitoring, or in extreme
cases (combined with engagement deficiencies), termination from
the peer review program.
