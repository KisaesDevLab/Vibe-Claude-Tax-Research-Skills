# SQMS 2 — Engagement Quality Reviews (EQR)

SQMS 2 ("Engagement Quality Reviews") provides the framework for
EQRs that the firm's SQMS 1 system identifies as required.
Implementation deadline: December 15, 2025 (aligned with SQMS 1).

## Objective (SQMS 2)

To provide an objective evaluation, before issuance of the report,
of the significant judgments made by the engagement team and the
conclusions reached.

## When EQR is required

The firm's SQMS 1 risk-assessment process identifies the
engagements that require EQR. SQMS 1 mandates EQR for:
- All audits of listed entities (issuer audits — out of scope here;
  PCAOB has its own EQR analogue under PCAOB AS).
- Engagements identified by the firm's risk assessment.

For nonissuer engagements, SQMS 1 risk factors that often trigger
EQR:
- Listed-entity-equivalent (entity has public debt or securities
  registered without being a registrant).
- High-profile or media-attention entities.
- Entities with complex industries (financial services, insurance,
  oil & gas, technology with revenue-recognition complexity).
- Engagements with significant judgments (going concern, fair
  value, valuation allowances, complex consolidations).
- First-year engagements.
- Engagements with prior-year independence or quality issues.
- Engagements with significant changes in engagement team.

## EQR objective

The EQR provides an objective evaluation of:
- Whether the engagement team complied with relevant ethical
  requirements, particularly independence.
- Whether the engagement team identified and addressed significant
  matters appropriately.
- Whether the engagement team's significant judgments and
  conclusions are appropriate.
- Whether the audit, attest, or other report is appropriate.

## EQR procedures (SQMS 2)

The engagement quality reviewer:
- Performs the review with adequate involvement at appropriate
  points throughout the engagement.
- Discusses significant matters with the engagement partner.
- Reviews engagement documentation related to significant
  judgments.
- Evaluates the basis for significant judgments and conclusions.
- Confirms that the engagement team has resolved differences of
  opinion (if any).

The EQR is NOT a re-audit; the engagement team remains responsible
for the engagement.

## Engagement quality reviewer eligibility

- Sufficient and appropriate experience and authority.
- Not a member of the engagement team.
- Not subject to undue influence by the engagement partner.
- Cooling-off period before assuming the engagement partner role on
  the same engagement (firm policy).

## Documentation (SQMS 2)

The EQR documentation includes:
- Procedures performed by the engagement quality reviewer.
- Conclusions reached.
- Date of review completion.
- Resolution of differences of opinion (if any).
- Confirmation that the engagement quality reviewer has not become
  aware of any unresolved matters that would cause the reviewer
  to believe that significant judgments and conclusions are not
  appropriate.

## Timing

The EQR must be completed BEFORE the report is dated and issued.
The engagement partner cannot date the report until:
- The engagement quality reviewer has completed the review and is
  not aware of unresolved matters.
- All other engagement-completion requirements are met.

## Differences of opinion

If the engagement quality reviewer disagrees with the engagement
team:
- Discuss with engagement partner.
- Escalate per firm policy if not resolved.
- Document the resolution.

The engagement should not be issued until differences are resolved.
