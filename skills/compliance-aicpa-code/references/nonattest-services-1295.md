# ET §1.295 — Nonattest services to attest clients

ET §1.295 governs whether nonattest services (bookkeeping, tax,
advisory, IT, internal audit, etc.) provided to an attest client
impair the firm's independence. The default answer is "they will
impair independence" unless the firm can establish four conditions
AND the specific service is not in the prohibited / restricted
catalog.

## The four general conditions (§1.295.040)

All four must hold for ANY nonattest service performed for an attest
client:

1. **Firm does not assume management responsibilities.** The firm
   cannot:
   - Set strategic direction.
   - Hire / fire / supervise client employees.
   - Authorize / sign / execute transactions.
   - Have custody of client assets.
   - Approve or be responsible for management's decisions.
2. **Client agrees in writing** to take responsibility for:
   - Designating an individual with suitable skill, knowledge, and
     experience (SKE) to oversee the service.
   - Evaluating adequacy of services performed.
   - Accepting responsibility for the results.
   - Establishing and maintaining internal control.
3. **Firm establishes and documents in writing** the firm's
   understanding with the client about:
   - Objectives of the service.
   - Services to be performed.
   - Client's acceptance of responsibilities.
   - Firm's responsibilities.
   - Limitations of the engagement.
4. **Firm evaluates whether the engagement creates or contributes to
   threats** at a level that requires safeguards beyond the four
   conditions.

If any of the four general conditions fails: **independence is
impaired.**

## Service-by-service screen

### Bookkeeping (§1.295.115)

- **Permitted** with §1.295 conditions if:
  - Firm posts only routine entries the client has authorized.
  - Client maintains books at all times (firm's work product is
    not the books of record).
  - Firm does not supervise client accounting personnel.
- **Prohibited** if:
  - Firm posts non-routine entries that are management decisions
    (e.g., write-offs, accrual adjustments requiring judgment).
  - Firm acts as outsourced controller / CFO.

### Tax services (§1.295.135)

- Tax return preparation: permitted with §1.295 conditions.
- Tax planning: permitted with §1.295 conditions; advocacy threat
  in tax controversy must be evaluated separately.
- Aggressive tax position advocacy at IRS Appeals or in court:
  advocacy threat at potentially-not-acceptable level for the same
  client's attest engagement.

### Internal audit outsourcing (§1.295.140)

- **Permitted** only if:
  - Client maintains its own internal audit function.
  - Firm does NOT perform ongoing internal audit monitoring as a
    management function.
  - Client management is responsible for the internal audit
    function's design, monitoring, and risk assessment.
- **Prohibited** if firm acts as the client's de facto internal
  audit function.

### Financial information system design and implementation (§1.295.145)

- **Permitted** for routine items: chart of accounts, COA mapping
  to GL, transaction processing implementation.
- **Prohibited**:
  - Designing the architecture of a system that produces the
    financial statements.
  - Operating or supervising the operation of client's IT systems.
  - Customizing software in a manner that constitutes management
    decisions.

### Appraisal, valuation, actuarial services (§1.295.155)

- **Permitted** if:
  - Result is not material to the financial statements (de minimis).
  - OR the matter does not involve a significant degree of subjectivity.
  - OR the firm does not provide the service to the same audit
    client whose financial statements include the valuation result.
- **Prohibited** otherwise (impairs independence).

### Forensic accounting / litigation support (§1.295.160)

- Investigative services for client management (fact-finding):
  permitted with §1.295 conditions.
- Litigation services where firm is providing expert testimony AGAINST
  a third party on client's behalf: advocacy threat at potentially-
  not-acceptable level.
- Expert witness services in adversarial proceedings to which client
  is a party: typically impairs independence (advocacy threat
  cannot be mitigated).

### Legal services (§1.295.180)

- Generally **prohibited** because legal services nearly always
  involve advocacy on the client's behalf or management decisions.
- Limited exceptions for ministerial matters (recording deeds, etc.).

### Hosting services (§1.295.143)

- Firm hosting client data (financial records, working files) can
  create custody / management threats. Permitted with appropriate
  controls (read-only for the firm, client-controlled access,
  segregation, regular handoffs).

### Recruiting and HR (§1.295.165)

- Recruiting: permitted (advise on competencies, do not make hiring
  decisions, do not supervise candidates).
- Compensation design / executive search for senior positions:
  threat at higher level; firm cannot select candidates or set
  compensation amounts.

## Joint nonattest engagements (multiple services)

When a firm provides several nonattest services to the same attest
client, the **cumulative effect** of threats must be evaluated.
Permitted services individually may, in combination, exceed an
acceptable threat level.

## Documentation

§1.295 requires written documentation:
- Of the four general conditions being met.
- Of the specific nonattest service scope.
- Of the client's designated SKE individual.
- Of the firm's evaluation of threats and safeguards.

The engagement file should reflect this in the engagement letter
and in the firm's quality management documentation under SQMS 1.
