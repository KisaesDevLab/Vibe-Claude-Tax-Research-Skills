# Subchapter K reference

## Formation (§721)

§721(a) nonrecognition on contribution of property to a partnership
in exchange for partnership interest. Exceptions:
- §721(b) — investment-company partnerships (transfer to a
  partnership that would be an investment company under §351 if
  incorporated).
- §707(a)(2)(B) — disguised sale doctrine.
- §704(c) — built-in gain/loss tracking under remedial /
  traditional / curative methods.

## Basis

### Outside basis (§722, §733, §752)

Outside basis = contributed cash + adjusted basis of contributed
property + share of partnership liabilities (§752).

§752 liability allocation:
- Recourse: allocated under §1.752-2 economic-risk-of-loss test.
- Nonrecourse: allocated under §1.752-3 three-tier framework
  (built-in gain, minimum gain, profit-share).

§733 reductions for distributions; §704(d) limits losses to
outside basis.

### Inside basis (§723, §1014)

Partnership's basis in contributed property = contributing
partner's basis at contribution. §704(c) tracks differences
between FMV and contributed-property basis at contribution.

## Allocations (§704)

### §704(a) general rule

Allocations follow the partnership agreement.

### §704(b) substantial-economic-effect

Treas. Reg. §1.704-1(b)(2) safe harbors:
1. Capital-account maintenance per §1.704-1(b)(2)(iv).
2. Liquidating distributions in accordance with positive capital-
   account balances.
3. Deficit-restoration obligation (DRO) OR qualified-income-offset
   (QIO) under the alternate test.

§1.704-1(b)(2)(iii) substantiality test:
- Pre-tax economic effect substantial in relation to tax effect.
- Shifting allocations and transitory allocations subject to
  scrutiny.

### §704(c) built-in gain/loss

Required for property contributed with FMV ≠ basis at contribution.
Methods:
- Traditional (default).
- Traditional with curative allocations.
- Remedial.

§704(c)(1)(B) — distribution of contributed property within 7 years
to non-contributing partner triggers gain.

§737 — distribution to contributing partner of other property
within 7 years triggers gain to extent of net §704(c) gain.

## Distributions

### §731 — non-liquidating distribution

Generally nonrecognition. Cash in excess of outside basis = gain.
Property distributed = basis equal to lesser of partnership
inside basis or distributee's outside basis (§732(a)).

### §732 — basis of distributed property

§732(a)(1) — non-liquidating: distributed property basis = lesser
of partnership inside basis or partner's pre-distribution outside
basis.

§732(b) — liquidating: distributed property basis = partner's
pre-distribution outside basis less cash distributed.

### §736 — payments to retiring or deceased partner

§736(a) — payments treated as distributive share or guaranteed
payment (deductible by partnership).
§736(b) — payments for partner's interest in partnership property
(treated as §731 distribution).

§736(b)(2) carve-out: §736(b) treatment NOT available to general
partners of partnerships where capital is not a material income-
producing factor (so §736(a) treatment applies in personal-service
partnerships).

## Sale of partnership interest

§741 — generally capital gain/loss treatment.

§751 — "hot assets" recharacterize a portion of gain/loss as
ordinary:
- §751(a) — sale of partnership interest: unrealized receivables
  and inventory items.
- §751(b) — disproportionate distributions: similar
  recharacterization for distributions that change a partner's
  share of hot assets.

## §754 election and basis adjustments

### §743(b) — partnership interest sale

If §754 election in effect, the new partner gets a basis adjustment
in partnership assets equal to the difference between the new
partner's outside basis and his share of inside basis.

§743(d) — mandatory basis adjustment if substantial built-in loss
(> $250K aggregate inside basis above FMV) at the time of
transfer.

### §734(b) — distribution to partners

If §754 election in effect, the partnership adjusts inside basis
of remaining property when:
- Cash distributed exceeds outside basis (gain to distributee);
- Distributee takes carryover basis below partnership's inside
  basis (loss recognition).

§734(d) — mandatory basis adjustment if substantial basis
reduction (> $250K).

## §707 — partner-partnership transactions

§707(a) — non-partner-capacity transactions; arm's-length
treatment.
§707(b) — related-party loss disallowance and gain
recharacterization.
§707(c) — guaranteed payments.

## Partnership audit regime — BBA / §6221

Bipartisan Budget Act of 2015 replaced TEFRA. §6221(a) — audits
conducted at the partnership level; partnership pays imputed
underpayment unless:
- Push-out election under §6226 (notify partners of adjustments).
- Pull-in procedure (modify partner returns).
- Eligible partnerships may opt out under §6221(b) (≤ 100
  partners, all eligible).

## §163(j) at partnership level

§163(j)(4) — limitation applied at partnership level. Excess
business interest expense (EBIE) allocated to partners; partners
carry forward at partner level.

## §199A QBI from partnerships

Pass-through computation; W-2 wages and UBIA computed at
partnership level and reported on K-1. Aggregation election
under §1.199A-4 may include multiple partnerships under common
control.

## State conformity

PTET (Pass-Through Entity Tax) elections operate at the
partnership level. Each state has its own:
- Election deadline.
- Election form.
- Computation rules.
- Credit at the partner level.

Route state-specific PTET questions to
`tax-research-state-income` per-state file.
