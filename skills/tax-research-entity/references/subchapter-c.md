# Subchapter C reference

## Formation (§351)

§351 nonrecognition on transfer of property to a corporation in
exchange for stock if transferors are in control (§368(c) — 80%
voting power and 80% of each class of nonvoting stock) immediately
after the exchange.

Boot recognition limited to FMV of non-stock consideration.

## Distributions

### §301 — non-liquidating distribution

Treated as:
1. Dividend to extent of E&P (§316).
2. Return of basis (§301(c)(2)).
3. Capital gain to extent in excess of basis (§301(c)(3)).

Constructive dividends from related-party transactions, excessive
compensation, below-market shareholder loans (§7872), bargain
sales, etc.

### §302 — redemption

Recharacterized as a §301 distribution UNLESS one of the §302(b)
exceptions:
- §302(b)(1): not essentially equivalent to dividend (United
  States v. Davis, 397 U.S. 301 (1970)).
- §302(b)(2): substantially disproportionate (50% / 80% tests).
- §302(b)(3): complete termination of interest.
- §302(b)(4): partial liquidation (corporate-level test).
- §302(b)(5): family-attribution waiver (§318 attribution).

§318 family attribution: spouse, children, grandchildren, parents.
§318 entity attribution: partnerships, S-corps, estates, trusts.

### §316 — earnings and profits

Current vs. accumulated E&P determine dividend treatment.
§312 governs adjustments to E&P. Practitioners should maintain
running E&P schedule for closely-held C-corps.

## Liquidations

### §331 — taxable liquidation (recipient side)

Distributees treat receipts as full payment for stock; capital
gain or loss to the extent of difference from stock basis.

### §332 — parent-subsidiary liquidation

Nonrecognition if the parent corporation owns 80% (§1504(a)(2))
of voting and 80% of value of subsidiary stock immediately before
liquidation. Subsidiary's basis in assets carries over to parent
(§334(b)).

### §336 / §337 — corporate-level recognition

§336 general rule: corporation recognizes gain or loss as if it
sold the assets at FMV (post-General Utilities repeal).
§337 nonrecognition for §332 parent-subsidiary liquidation.

Loss limitation: §336(d)(1) anti-stuffing rule for liquidation
distributions to related parties.

## Reorganizations (§368)

§368(a)(1)(A) statutory merger / consolidation.
§368(a)(1)(B) stock-for-stock; ≥ 80% control after.
§368(a)(1)(C) stock-for-substantially-all-assets.
§368(a)(1)(D) divisive: §355 spin-offs / split-offs / split-ups.
§368(a)(1)(E) recapitalization (single corporation).
§368(a)(1)(F) mere change in form.
§368(a)(1)(G) bankruptcy.

Continuity-of-interest, continuity-of-business-enterprise, and
business-purpose doctrines apply across all reorganizations
(Treas. Reg. §1.368-1(b)).

§355 spin-off requirements:
- Active trade or business: 5-year rule for both distributing
  and controlled.
- Distribution of stock: ≥ 80% control distributed.
- Not a device for distribution of E&P.
- Business purpose distinct from federal-tax avoidance.

## Acquisitions

### §338 — qualified stock purchase election

§338(g) — taxable transaction; §338(h)(10) — joint election with
seller for asset-deal treatment. Common in S-corp acquisitions
where buyer wants stepped-up basis but parties want stock-form
mechanics.

### §382 — NOL limitations

§382 limits NOL carryforwards after an "ownership change" (50%
change in 5% shareholder ownership over 3-year testing period).

## §163(j) — interest deduction limit

Post-TCJA: limit to sum of business interest income + 30% of ATI
+ floor-plan interest. ATI calculation: post-2022, EBIT-based
(EBITDA-based for 2018-2021).

Carryforward indefinite. Partnership-level rules at §163(j)(4):
limitation applied at partnership level, with excess business
interest expense (EBIE) allocated to partners.

## §245A — DRD on foreign-source dividends

100% dividends-received deduction on §245A-eligible foreign
dividends (10%-or-more owned non-CFC corp, etc.). §245A(e)
hybrid-dividend exception. §1059(e)(3) interaction with extraordinary
dividend rules. §245A regs at §1.245A.

## §250 — FDII / GILTI deduction

Reduces effective rate on FDII (Foreign-Derived Intangible Income)
and GILTI inclusion. Interplay with §951A GILTI inclusion under
the broader international-tax framework.

## State conformity issues

C-corp issues frequently implicate state conformity:
- §163(j) interaction with state-level interest-deduction limits.
- §382 carry-forward limitations and state NOL conformity.
- §1031 carry-forward (post-TCJA) and CA Form 3840.
- PTET elections operate at the partnership / S-corp level, not
  C-corp.
