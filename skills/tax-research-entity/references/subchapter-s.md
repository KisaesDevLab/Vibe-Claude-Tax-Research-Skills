# Subchapter S reference

## Eligibility (§1361)

S-corporation qualifying corporation:
- §1361(b)(1)(A) — domestic corporation.
- §1361(b)(1)(B) — not an ineligible corporation:
  - financial institution using the reserve method (§585);
  - insurance company;
  - §936 corporation;
  - DISC.
- §1361(b)(1)(C) — only allowable shareholders:
  - individuals (NOT nonresident aliens);
  - decedents' estates;
  - bankruptcy estates;
  - trusts described in §1361(c)(2);
  - §501(c)(3) tax-exempt charitable organizations;
  - employee stock-ownership plans (ESOPs).
- §1361(b)(1)(D) — ≤ 100 shareholders (with §1361(c)(1) family
  attribution allowing related individuals to count as one).
- §1361(b)(1)(E) — only ONE class of stock outstanding (voting
  differences allowed; economic differences not).

## Election (§1362)

§1362(a) — election by all shareholders on Form 2553.
- Made before the 16th day of the 3rd month of the year for which
  effective; or after that date for the next year.
- Late elections under Rev. Proc. 2013-30 (relief for taxpayers
  who reasonably relied on tax professional, etc.).

§1362(d) — termination by:
- Revocation by majority of shareholders (§1362(d)(1)).
- Loss of small-business-corporation status (§1362(d)(2)).
- §1362(d)(3) — passive-investment-income > 25% of gross receipts
  for 3 consecutive years AND C-corp E&P at end of those years.

## Pass-through (§1366)

§1366(a) — taxable income / loss / credits / deductions pass
through pro rata to shareholders based on stock ownership during
the year (per-share-per-day rule unless §1377 election).

§1366(d) — losses limited to:
1. Stock basis;
2. Loan basis (direct loans from shareholder to S-corp;
   guarantees do NOT create loan basis — Selfe v. United States,
   778 F.2d 769 (11th Cir. 1985), is the exception, not the rule).

§1366(d)(2) — disallowed loss carries forward indefinitely until
basis restored.

## Distributions (§1368)

§1368(a) — non-liquidating distributions:
- §1368(b) — S-corp without C-corp E&P: tax-free to extent of
  stock basis; gain to extent in excess.
- §1368(c) — S-corp with C-corp E&P:
  - Step 1: tax-free to extent of accumulated adjustments account
    (AAA).
  - Step 2: dividend to extent of C-corp E&P.
  - Step 3: tax-free to extent of remaining basis.
  - Step 4: capital gain to extent in excess of basis.

§1368(e) — AAA computation: similar to E&P but excludes tax-exempt
income and related expenses.

## Reasonable compensation

§3121(d)(2) — common-law employee.
§1366(e) — reasonable compensation must be paid to shareholder-
employee performing services BEFORE FICA/distributions
recharacterized.
Watson v. United States, 668 F.3d 1008 (8th Cir. 2012) — leading
8th Cir. case on S-corp reasonable comp.
Route to `predict-reasonable-comp` skill.

## §1377(a) — per-share-per-day allocation

Default rule under §1377(a)(1): items allocated pro rata to
shareholders based on stock owned each day.

§1377(a)(2) — closing-of-the-books election: with consent of all
affected shareholders, the year is split at the date of a
shareholder's complete termination of interest, with items
allocated to the relevant period.

## §1374 — built-in gains tax

C-corp converting to S-corp pays §1374 BIG tax on built-in gains
realized within the recognition period (5 years, post-2009).

§1374 base: lesser of (a) recognized BIG for the year; or (b)
taxable income computed as if a C-corp.

## §1361(b)(3) — Qualified Subchapter S Subsidiary (QSub)

S-corp parent can elect to treat a 100%-owned domestic subsidiary
as disregarded. Election on Form 8869.

## Election errors and §1362(f) inadvertent termination relief

§1362(f) — IRS may treat termination as inadvertent if:
- Corporation failed to qualify under §1361(b);
- IRS finds the failure was inadvertent;
- Corrective steps taken within reasonable period;
- All shareholders agree to adjustments consistent with treating
  the corporation as having continued to be an S-corp.

PLR practice: numerous §1362(f) PLRs each year.

## State conformity

States generally recognize federal S-corp election but with
exceptions:
- New Hampshire — does not recognize S-corp at all (taxed as
  C-corp for state).
- New York City — UBT (Unincorporated Business Tax) and General
  Corporation Tax interaction.
- Texas margin tax — operates regardless of federal S-corp
  status.
- Tennessee F&E tax — applies to S-corps.

Route state-specific S-corp issues to
`tax-research-state-income` per-state file.

## §199A interaction

S-corp QBI flows through on K-1. W-2 wages computed at S-corp
level; UBIA likewise. SSTB classification determines whether
above-threshold shareholders get the deduction.

S-corp shareholders who are reasonable-comp under-paid risk a
double penalty: §1366 / §1366(e) recharacterization PLUS reduced
QBI base for the recharacterized portion.
