# Check-the-box — entity classification

## Treas. Reg. §301.7701-1 through -3

The "check-the-box" regulations (1996) replaced the Kintner
fact-pattern test. Treats unincorporated entities as flexibly
classified.

### §301.7701-2 — per-se corporations

Certain entities are per se corporations and cannot elect:
- Domestic per se: business entities organized under federal or
  state statute as "incorporated" (or "incorporation" / "joint-
  stock company" / "joint-stock association" / "insurance
  company").
- Foreign per se: list at §301.7701-2(b)(8). Includes most
  foreign equivalents of corporations.

### §301.7701-3 — eligible entities

Default rules:
- Domestic eligible entity (e.g., LLC):
  - Single-member: disregarded.
  - Multi-member: partnership.
- Foreign eligible entity:
  - Single-member: disregarded if member has unlimited liability;
    foreign corporation if member has limited liability.
  - Multi-member: partnership if any member has unlimited
    liability; foreign corporation if all members have limited
    liability.

### Election mechanics

Form 8832:
- Filed within 75 days of effective date or up to 12 months after.
- Late-election relief under Rev. Proc. 2010-32 (LLCs that
  intended to be a corporation but failed to file).
- Election binding for 60 months once made; cannot be re-elected
  for another 60 months absent change in control or other §301.7701-3(c)(1)(iv) conditions.

### Single-member LLC default

Without election, a single-member LLC is a disregarded entity for
federal tax purposes. The owner reports operations on Schedule C
(individual), Schedule E (rental), or directly on the entity's
return (if owned by a corporation).

### Multi-member LLC default

Without election, a multi-member LLC is a partnership for federal
tax purposes. Form 1065 + K-1 mechanics.

### Election to be an S-corp

A domestic LLC can elect S-corp treatment by filing:
1. Form 8832 (entity classification election to corporation); OR
2. Form 2553 (S-corp election directly — IRS treats as both
   classification election and S-corp election).

## Disregarded-entity owners

§301.7701-2(c)(2) — disregarded entity treated as:
- Branch / division of owner for income tax.
- Separate entity for employment tax (§301.7701-2(c)(2)(iv)).
- Separate entity for federal tax liability collection
  (§301.7701-2(c)(2)(iii)).
- Separate entity for excise tax (Notice 2003-46).

The split treatment creates reporting traps: a single-member LLC
files Schedule C for income but Form 941 / 940 separately for
employment.

## Series LLC

§301.7701-1 proposed regulations (2010) would have treated each
series of a series LLC as a separate entity. The proposed
regulations were not finalized.

## Foreign entity classification

Foreign per se list (§301.7701-2(b)(8)) is comprehensive but
country-specific. Re-classification of a foreign per se entity
requires:
- Domestication into a U.S. eligible entity; OR
- Conversion under foreign law to a non-per-se form.

For multinational structures, §367 / §367(a) / §367(b) outbound
and inbound recognition rules govern check-the-box transactions
involving foreign entities.

## Common practitioner traps

- Late election filing under Rev. Proc. 2010-32 (relief available
  but procedural compliance critical).
- Single-member LLC with state-law liability protection but
  federal disregard — creates state-law / federal-tax mismatch
  that often confuses clients.
- Foreign-entity check-the-box election triggering deemed
  liquidation under §367 — unintended gain recognition.
- Disregarded-entity employment-tax separate-entity treatment
  missed; LLC treated as DRE for income but employer for FICA.
- §761 partnership election to be excluded from Subchapter K (rare
  but useful for joint-venture / co-ownership arrangements).

## Federal vs. state classification

Most states conform to federal check-the-box, BUT:
- Some states tax LLCs at the entity level regardless of federal
  classification (CA $800 LLC fee; TX margin tax; TN F&E).
- Some states have separate election forms or default rules.

Route state-specific classification questions to
`tax-research-state-income` per-state file.
