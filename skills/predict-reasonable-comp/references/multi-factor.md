# Multi-factor reasonable-compensation framework

The reasonableness of compensation paid by a closely-held
corporation is a fact-and-circumstances inquiry. Different circuits
apply different factor lists; the skill must identify the
taxpayer's circuit (Golsen) before weighting authority.

## Mayson Manufacturing factors (Sixth Circuit roots, widely cited)

From Mayson Mfg. Co. v. Commissioner, 178 F.2d 115 (6th Cir. 1949):
1. Employee's qualifications.
2. Nature, extent, and scope of work.
3. Size and complexity of the business.
4. General economic conditions.
5. Comparison of salaries with distributions and shareholder
   earnings.
6. Salaries paid by similar businesses for similar positions.
7. Salary policy of the corporation as to all employees.
8. Salary history of the employee.
9. Internal consistency in establishing compensation.

## Independent-investor test (Seventh Circuit)

Exacto Spring Corp. v. Commissioner, 196 F.3d 833 (7th Cir. 1999):
"The test is whether the company's compensation policy is
consistent with what an independent investor would have approved."
Subsequent cases: Menard, Inc. v. Commissioner, 560 F.3d 620 (7th
Cir. 2009); Eberl's Claim Service v. Commissioner, 249 F.3d 994
(10th Cir. 2001) (adopting the Seventh Circuit test).

The independent-investor test essentially asks: does the after-comp
return on equity satisfy a hypothetical outside investor?

## Watson framework — S-corps

Watson v. United States, 668 F.3d 1008 (8th Cir. 2012): an S-corp
sole shareholder paid himself $24K in compensation while taking
$203K in distributions. Court applied "replacement cost" analogy —
what the firm would pay to replace the owner's services — and
upheld IRS reclassification. Watson is the bellwether case for
S-corp reasonable-comp recharacterization for FICA purposes.

## C-corp constructive-dividend pathway

Where a C-corp pays compensation found to be excessive, the excess
is recharacterized as a constructive dividend (§ 316) — non-
deductible to the corporation, taxable to the shareholder at
ordinary or qualified-dividend rates.

## S-corp recharacterization pathway

Distribution recharacterized as wages → subject to FICA (employer
+ employee), Medicare, and FUTA. The corporation may also owe
penalties under § 6651 / § 6656 for unpaid employment taxes.

## Comparable-position data sources (persuasive non-authority)

Tag all of the below as `persuasive_non_authority`:
- Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS) Occupational Employment and Wage
  Statistics (OEWS): https://www.bls.gov/oes/
- ERI Economic Research Institute compensation data (proprietary)
- IRS Reasonable Compensation Job Aid for IRS Valuation
  Professionals (internal IRS reference)
- IRS Pub. 535 (Business Expenses) — describes IRS position; not
  authority

## Documentation that supports reasonableness

- Comparable-position research at the time compensation was set
- Board minutes / shareholder consents documenting comp committee
  rationale
- Written compensation policy
- Performance metrics tied to comp
- Bonuses paid via formal plan (especially helpful for C-corps —
  see Treas. Reg. §1.162-7(b)(2))
