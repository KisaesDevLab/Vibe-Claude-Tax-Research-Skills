# §469(c)(7) and §1.469-5T quantitative tests

## §469(c)(7) two-prong test (per spouse, per year)

1. **More than half of personal services** performed by the taxpayer
   in trades or businesses during the taxable year are performed in
   real-property trades or businesses in which the taxpayer
   materially participates.
2. **More than 750 hours** of service during the taxable year are
   performed in real-property trades or businesses in which the
   taxpayer materially participates.

## "Real-property trades or businesses" (§469(c)(7)(C))

Any real-property:
- development or redevelopment;
- construction or reconstruction;
- acquisition;
- conversion;
- rental;
- operation or management;
- leasing; or
- brokerage trade or business.

A taxpayer holding a real-estate brokerage license and actively
brokering qualifies for the time-categorization side; passive
investment in REITs or rental funds does not.

## §469(h) / Treas. Reg. §1.469-5T material-participation tests

A taxpayer materially participates in an activity if ANY of:

1. > 500 hours of participation in the activity during the year.
2. Substantially all of the participation in the activity is by
   the taxpayer (no other significant participants).
3. > 100 hours of participation, and not less than any other
   individual's participation.
4. Significant-participation activity (> 100 hours), and the
   aggregate of significant-participation activities exceeds 500
   hours.
5. Materially participated in 5 of the prior 10 years.
6. Personal-service activity, materially participated in any 3
   prior years (rare for rentals).
7. Facts and circumstances — > 100 hours and regular, continuous,
   substantial involvement (with limitations under (a)(7)(ii) for
   investor-type activities).

For real-estate professionals, the §1.469-9(g) aggregation election
treats all rental real-estate interests as a single activity for
material-participation purposes only. Without it, each property
must independently satisfy one of the seven tests.

## Documentation expectations

Treas. Reg. §1.469-5T(f)(4): "extent of an individual's
participation … may be established by any reasonable means."
However, the IRS and Tax Court demand contemporaneous logs in
practice:

- **Bailey v. Commissioner, T.C. Memo. 2001-296** — disallowed
  reconstructed log of 1,150 hours where calendar entries lacked
  detail.
- **Hakkak v. Commissioner, T.C. Memo. 2020-46** — disallowed
  reconstructed estimate; emphasized the spouse-by-spouse nature
  of the §469(c)(7) test.
- **Pohoski v. Commissioner, T.C. Memo. 1998-17** — accepted
  contemporaneous appointment book as adequate evidence.

Practitioner audit-defense file should include:
- Daily log of hours by property and activity type.
- Mileage log for travel-time substantiation (separate from
  business-mileage records).
- W-2 hours from primary employment with documentation of any
  overtime / off-hours allocation to RE.
- Service contracts, vendor invoices, tenant communications
  date-stamped and tied to log entries.
- §1.469-9(g) aggregation election statement (if filed) with the
  filed-with-return year identified.

## Aggregation election (§1.469-9(g))

- Made by attaching a statement to the original return.
- Binding for all future years; revoked only on a "material change
  in facts and circumstances".
- A late-filed election may be possible under Treas. Reg.
  §301.9100-2 (12-month automatic extension) or §301.9100-3
  (discretionary relief).
- Hardy v. Commissioner, T.C. Memo. 2017-16: failure to file
  §1.469-9(g) was fatal where taxpayer's per-property hours fell
  below the 500-hour test.

## Short-term-rental ("STR") carve-out

Treas. Reg. §1.469-1T(e)(3)(ii) excludes from "rental activity":

- Average customer-use period ≤ 7 days; OR
- Average customer-use period ≤ 30 days AND significant personal
  services are provided; OR
- Extraordinary personal services are provided regardless of period.

Consequence: STRs are non-rental trade-or-business activities; the
REPS test does NOT apply, but the seven §1.469-5T material-
participation tests DO. The "STR loophole" combines this carve-out
with test 1 (> 500 hours) or test 4 (significant participation
+ aggregate > 500 hours).

Note that §469 classification differs from §1402 self-employment
treatment, §6041/§6050W reporting, and state classification — STR
analysis should be separately verified for each.

## Common audit-trigger patterns

- Taxpayer with full-time W-2 job claiming REPS.
- > 750 hours but log reconstructed from memory.
- Aggregation never elected; multi-property losses claimed.
- STR / REPS confusion: taxpayer claiming REPS on properties with
  ≤ 7-day average use (REPS not needed; potentially mis-positioned).
