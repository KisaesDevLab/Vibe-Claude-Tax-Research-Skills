## A2. LLC Late C Corp Election Relief (Rev. Proc. 2009-41)

**Authority:** Rev. Proc. 2009-41; Form 8832 (Entity Classification
Election).

**Purpose.** Provides streamlined relief for an eligible entity (LLC)
that intended to be classified as a corporation but failed to file
Form 8832 timely. Avoids the need for a private letter ruling under
§9100 relief.

**Eligibility.** All four conditions must be satisfied:

1. **Failure type.** The entity failed to obtain its desired
   classification because either:
   - An initial classification of a newly-formed entity failed
     because Form 8832 was not timely filed; OR
   - A requested change in classification failed solely because
     Form 8832 was not timely filed.

2. **Tax return filings.** Either:
   - The entity has not filed a federal tax or information return
     for the first year in which the election was intended because
     the due date has not passed; OR
   - The entity timely filed all required federal tax and information
     returns consistent with its requested classification for all
     intended-effective years, and no inconsistent returns have been
     filed during any of the taxable years.

3. **Reasonable cause.** The entity has reasonable cause for its
   failure to timely make the check-the-box election.

4. **Time limit.** Three years and 75 days from the requested
   effective date have not passed.

**When to file.** Within 3 years and 75 days of the requested
effective date.

**Where to file.** Form 8832 with the IRS service center, marked at
the top "FILED PURSUANT TO REV. PROC. 2009-41." Reasonable cause
statement attached.

**Mechanics.**

1. Complete Form 8832 with the original requested effective date.
2. At the top of the form, write "FILED PURSUANT TO REV. PROC.
   2009-41."
3. Attach a statement signed by an officer/owner explaining
   reasonable cause for the late filing.
4. Send to the IRS service center where the entity files (or would
   have filed) its return.
5. Attach a copy of Form 8832 with the entity's first return for
   the year the election is to be effective (or the most recent
   return if returns have already been filed).

**Notes / Pitfalls.**

- Rev. Proc. 2009-41 is the streamlined alternative to §9100 relief
  via PLR. PLR fees can run $30,000+; Rev. Proc. 2009-41 is free.
- Track the 3-years-and-75-days clock carefully. Once expired, only
  §9100 PLR relief remains.
- Distinct from Rev. Proc. 2013-30 (S corp late election relief) —
  see A3.
