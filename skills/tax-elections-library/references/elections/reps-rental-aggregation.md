## D2. Real Estate Professional Rental Aggregation (Reg. §1.469-9(g)(3))

**Authority:** IRC §469(c)(7); Treas. Reg. §1.469-9; Rev. Proc.
2011-34.

**Purpose.** A qualifying real estate professional (REPS) under
§469(c)(7) treats rental real estate activities as nonpassive only
if the taxpayer **materially participates** in the activity. By
default, each rental property is a separate activity, requiring the
taxpayer to materially participate in each. The §469(c)(7)(A)
election aggregates **all** rental real estate interests into a
single activity, making it possible to satisfy material participation
across the portfolio collectively.

**Eligibility.**

1. **Real estate professional status (§469(c)(7)(B)):**
   - More than 50% of personal services performed in trades or
     businesses are performed in real property trades or businesses
     in which the taxpayer materially participates; AND
   - More than 750 hours of services during the tax year are
     performed in real property trades or businesses in which the
     taxpayer materially participates.

2. **Material participation in the aggregated rental activity** —
   typically the 500-hour test under §469(h).

**When to file.** Attach to the timely-filed return for the
**first year** in which the election is made. Election is binding
for all subsequent years until the taxpayer **revokes** it under
Rev. Proc. 2011-34 (which requires showing a material change in
facts and circumstances).

**Where to file.** With Form 1040.

**Sample election language:**

```
AGGREGATION ELECTION FOR REAL ESTATE PROFESSIONAL

Taxpayer:           ___________________________________________
FEIN/SSN:           ___________________________________________
Tax Year:           ___________________________________________

In accordance with Treas. Reg. § 1.469-9(g)(3), the taxpayer
hereby states that he (or she) is a qualifying real estate
professional under IRC § 469(c)(7), and elects under
IRC § 469(c)(7)(A) to treat all interests in the real estate
listed below as a single rental real estate activity.

Property 1:
  Description / Address: _______________________________________
Property 2:
  Description / Address: _______________________________________
Property 3:
  Description / Address: _______________________________________
Property 4:
  Description / Address: _______________________________________
```

**Notes / Pitfalls.**

- **Time logs are critical.** Maintain contemporaneous logs of
  hours by activity to substantiate the 750-hour test and the
  material participation tests. *Hailstock*, *Hassanipour*, and
  *Frank Aragona Trust* establish the documentation standard.
- The 50%-of-services test counts ALL personal services, not just
  rental. A full-time W-2 employee in a non-real-estate field
  generally cannot satisfy 50%.
- Spouses may aggregate hours for the 750-hour test and material
  participation, but **only one spouse** must satisfy the 50% test
  (Reg. §1.469-9(c)(4)).
- Once made, the election is binding. Revocation under Rev. Proc.
  2011-34 requires demonstrating a "material change in facts and
  circumstances." Mere change in tax preference is not sufficient.
- The aggregation election is independent of the §1.469-4 grouping
  election (D1). REPS may make both.
- Suspended passive losses from years prior to REPS qualification
  remain suspended until the property generating them is fully
  disposed of in a fully-taxable transaction.
- Election permits material participation to be satisfied on the
  aggregate. Once satisfied, ALL rentals in the aggregate are
  nonpassive — losses from any of them offset W-2 / portfolio
  income.
- Self-rental rule (Reg. §1.469-2(f)(6)) still applies even after
  aggregation: rental income from a property leased to a
  taxpayer-controlled business is recharacterized as nonpassive
  income (one-way recharacterization).
