## A3. S Corp Late Election Relief (Rev. Proc. 2013-30)

**Authority:** Rev. Proc. 2013-30; IRC §1362(b)(5); Form 2553.

**Purpose.** Provides streamlined relief for late S corp elections
without need for private letter ruling. Consolidates earlier relief
procedures (Rev. Procs. 97-48, 2003-43, 2004-48, 2007-62) into a
single procedure.

**Required documentation.** A late S election relief filing under
Rev. Proc. 2013-30 must include:

1. **A newly completed Form 2553** with the original requested
   effective date entered on Line E.
2. **Note at the top of the new Form 2553:** "FILED PURSUANT TO
   REV. PROC. 2013-30."
3. **Shareholder signatures.** All persons who were shareholders
   from the original requested effective date until the late filing
   date must sign the new Form 2553.
4. **Individual shareholder statements.** Each shareholder must
   complete and sign, individually, the Part I Box K statement (or
   on the separate statement provided in Form 2553 instructions),
   covering the period from the requested effective date.
5. **Reasonable cause statement.** An officer of the corporation
   prepares and signs a "reasonable cause" statement in Box H of
   page 1 of Form 2553.
6. **For LLCs.** Also file Form 8832 (Entity Classification Election)
   contemporaneously, requesting the same effective date.

**When to file.** Within 3 years and 75 days of the requested
effective date.

**Where to file.**

- Send 1 copy of Form 2553 (and Form 8832 if required) directly to
  the IRS service center.
- Send a 2nd copy attached to the corporation's return for the year
  the S election is to be effective (or the most recent return if
  prior years have been filed).

**Notes / Pitfalls.**

- All shareholders during the entire intended-S-election period must
  consent. Missing consents = relief denied.
- Reasonable cause is fact-intensive. Examples that work: reliance
  on competent tax professional who failed to file; entity-level
  illness/death; loss of records due to disaster. Examples that fail:
  taxpayer was unaware of S election requirement.
- Entity must have filed all returns consistent with S corp
  treatment from the intended effective date forward.
- 3-year-and-75-day window is strict — past that, only PLR §9100
  relief remains.
