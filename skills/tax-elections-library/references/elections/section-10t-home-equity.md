## B2. 10-T Election (Home Equity Debt Treated as Trade or Business)

**Authority:** Notice 89-35; "10-T" is the practitioner's shorthand
for the trace-from-home-equity-debt election under interest tracing
rules in Temp. Reg. §1.163-8T.

**Purpose.** Allows a taxpayer to treat interest on home equity debt
as trade or business interest (instead of investment, personal, or
home mortgage interest) when the debt proceeds are used in a trade
or business. This reclassification permits the interest to be
deducted against business income (typically on Schedule C, E, or F)
rather than being subject to the home mortgage interest limits or
the §163(d) investment interest limit.

**When to file.** Attach to the **initial-election-year return only**.
Once the election is made, no further annual attachment is required
unless the use of debt proceeds changes.

**Where to file.** With the Form 1040 for the year of election.

**Sample election language:**

```
10-T ELECTION
(Attach to Initial Election Year Return Only)

Taxpayer: ________________________________________________________
Form: 1040
FEIN/SSN: ______________________________
Tax Year: ______________________________

The above taxpayer hereby elects to treat $____________ of home
equity debt as trade or business debt. The proceeds of this home
equity debt were utilized in the trade or business of the above
taxpayer. During the above tax year, the taxpayer incurred and paid
$___________ of interest which was deducted on Schedule ___,
Line ___.
```

**Notes / Pitfalls.**

- **TCJA limitation.** Beginning 2018, §163(h)(3)(F) generally
  suspended deduction of home equity debt interest as an itemized
  deduction except where proceeds are used to buy/build/substantially
  improve the residence securing the loan. The 10-T election preserves
  deduction for trade-or-business uses.
- Document the actual use of proceeds (deposit-to-disbursement
  trace) per Temp. Reg. §1.163-8T.
- Keep records of the trace for as long as the debt remains
  outstanding — IRS may challenge the characterization in any open
  year.
- The 30-day deposit rule under Reg. §1.163-8T(c)(4): an expenditure
  from any account, within 30 days of depositing debt proceeds into
  that account, is treated as a disbursement of those proceeds.
