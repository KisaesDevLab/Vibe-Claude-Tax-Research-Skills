## F8. Corporate Revocation of S Election (§1362(d)(1))

**Authority:** IRC §1362(d)(1); Treas. Reg. §1.1362-6.

**Purpose.** Revokes the S corporation election, returning the
corporation to C corporation status. Effective on the date specified
(prospectively) or on the first day of the year of revocation if
filed by the 15th day of the 3rd month of that year.

**Required consent.** Shareholders owning **more than 50% of the
corporation's stock** (voting + non-voting combined) must consent to
the revocation. Non-consenting shareholders cannot block revocation
if the >50% threshold is met.

**When to file.**

- **Effective for current tax year:** File on or before the **15th
  day of the 3rd month** of the current tax year.
- **Effective for future tax year:** File at any time, specifying
  the effective date.

**Where to file.** IRS Service Center where the S election (Form
2553) was originally filed. (Generally the corporation's regular
filing center.)

**Filing components.**

1. **Corporate statement of revocation** signed by an authorized
   officer.
2. **Shareholder consent statement** signed by shareholders owning
   >50% of stock — see F9.

**Sample corporate statement of revocation:**

```
CORPORATE STATEMENT OF REVOCATION OF S ELECTION
Under IRC § 1362(a)

[TODAY'S DATE]

Internal Revenue Service Center
[Service Center Address]

Re:  [CORPORATION NAME]
     [CORPORATION ADDRESS]
     [FEIN]

Subject: Revocation of S Corporation election

Please be advised that [CORPORATION NAME], a corporation with its
principal office located at [CORPORATION ADDRESS], hereby revokes
the Subchapter S election it previously made under §1362(a) of
the Internal Revenue Code.

The first year for which this revocation is intended to be
effective is the corporation's tax year beginning _________.

The corporation has _______ shares of stock issued and
outstanding at the effective date including all voting and
non-voting stock.

A statement signed by shareholders owning more than 50% of the
corporation's stock and consenting to the revocation is attached.

Sincerely,

[CORPORATE OFFICER NAME]
Title: _____________________
```

**Notes / Pitfalls.**

- Revocation is **prospective only** unless filed by the 15th day of
  the 3rd month of the current year.
- Once revoked, the corporation **cannot re-elect S status for 5
  years** under §1362(g) without IRS consent.
- A revocation triggers a "**S termination year**" under §1362(e)
  if it occurs mid-year, requiring allocation of income between
  S short period and C short period (closing-of-books or pro-rata
  daily, election available under §1362(e)(3)).
- Built-in gains tax under §1374 may apply to the C corp upon sale
  of pre-conversion built-in-gain assets (10-year recognition
  period).
- All shareholders, voting and non-voting, count toward the >50%
  threshold; ownership is by share, not headcount.
