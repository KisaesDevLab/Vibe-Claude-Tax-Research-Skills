## C1. §266 Property Tax / Carrying-Cost Capitalization

**Authority:** IRC §266; Treas. Reg. §1.266-1.

**Purpose.** Permits taxpayer to elect to capitalize (rather than
deduct) property taxes, mortgage interest, and other carrying costs
on unimproved/unproductive real property, real property under
construction, or personal property in installation. The election is
typically used when the deduction would otherwise be lost (e.g.,
taxpayer in low/no income year, or property not yet income-producing
so deduction has limited current benefit). Capitalized costs increase
basis.

**When to file.** Annual election; attach statement to the timely-filed
return (with extensions) for the year of the capitalization. Election
is **made annually** — capitalization in one year does not bind future
years.

**Where to file.** With Form 1040 / 1120 / 1065 / 1120-S.

**Sample election language:**

```
IRC SECTION 266 ELECTION TO CAPITALIZE CARRYING COSTS

Taxpayer: ____________________________
FEIN/SSN: ____________________________
Tax Year: ______

For tax year ______ the taxpayer hereby elects under Internal
Revenue Code Section 266 and related Regulations at 1.266-1 to
capitalize rather than deduct property tax paid, interest, and
other carrying costs during the year on the following property:

Property Description                                    Amount
________________________________________            __________
________________________________________            __________
________________________________________            __________
________________________________________            __________
```

**Notes / Pitfalls.**

- Three categories of costs may be capitalized: **(1) taxes**,
  **(2) interest** (must satisfy §163(d) or §163(h) deduction tests
  separately), and **(3) other carrying charges** (insurance,
  maintenance, etc.).
- Election can be made for **any one, two, or all three** categories
  per year. Categories selected are independent.
- Must be elected for **each year separately**. No automatic
  carryforward.
- Property must be one of the qualifying types: unimproved /
  unproductive real property; real property under construction;
  personal property in installation phase.
- Once a property becomes income-producing, the election is no
  longer available for that property.
- Useful interaction with §263A UNICAP for builders/developers, but
  do not duplicate capitalization.
