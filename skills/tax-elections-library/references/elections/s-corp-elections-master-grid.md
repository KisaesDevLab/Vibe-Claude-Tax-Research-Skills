## F1. S Corp Elections Master Grid

The following grid summarizes the principal S corporation elections,
their authority, filing mechanics, and effect.

### Initial Return Elections

| Election | Method of Making Election | Date for Making Election | Authority | Explanation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Organization Costs — *Deduction* | None required; deduct on return | With return | IRC §709(b)(1)(A)(ii) | Allows initial expensing of up to $5,000 of organizational costs by deducting on original return |
| Organization Costs — *Amortization* | Capitalize and amortize; no separate election required | Due date of 1st return plus extension | Treas. Reg. §1.709-1T(b) | Allows amortization of organization costs over 180 months |
| S Corporation Election | File Form 2553 signed by officer and every shareholder | By the 15th day of the 3rd month of the tax year the election is to be effective, or after the earliest day of shareholders, assets, or business | IRC §1362(a) | Initial S election filing |
| LLC Election to be Taxed as S Corp | File Form 2553 signed by officer and every member | By the 15th day of the 3rd month of the tax year the election is to be effective, or after the earliest day of shareholders, assets, or business | IRC §1362(a) | Initial S election filing for LLC |
| Election to Treat 100%-Owned Subsidiary as QSub | File Form 8869 | Any time within 12 months prior to desired effective date or 2½ months after same date | IRC §1361(b)(3) | Establishes QSub for consolidated tax treatment |
| Election to Treat a Trust as a QSST | Form 2553 | Within 2 months and 15 days after first day on which trust qualifies as QSST | IRC §1361(d)(2) | Grantor trust treatment for QSST |
| Election to Treat a Trust as an ESBT | Separate election statement by trustee | Within 2 months and 15 days after first day on which trust qualifies as ESBT | IRC §1361(e)(3) | Treatment for ESBT |
| Startup Costs — *Deduction* | None required; deduct on return | Due date of 1st return plus extension | IRC §195(b)(1) | Allows initial expensing of up to $5,000 of startup costs by deducting on original return |
| Startup Costs — *Amortization* | Capitalize and amortize; no separate election required | Due date of 1st return plus extension | Treas. Reg. §1.195-1T | Allows amortization of startup costs over 180 months |
| §444 Election (Tax Year Other Than Required) | File Form 8716 | Earlier of 15th day of 5th month following 1st day of election year, or unextended due date of return | IRC §444; Reg. §1.444-3T | Allows taxpayer to adopt September, October, or November year-end in normal calendar year entity |
| Recurring Item Election | By deducting item on return in 1st year incurred | Due date of 1st return plus extension where applicable expenses are incurred | IRC §461(h)(3); Reg. §1.461-4(g)(7) | Allows deduction in year expenses are fixed and determinable if performance occurs by earlier of filing date or 8½ months after year-end |
| Accrual of Property Taxes | Attach statement to return | Due date of 1st return plus extension where applicable expenses are incurred | IRC §461(c); Reg. §1.461-1(c) | Allows ratable accrual of real property taxes over period to which they relate |

### Operating Elections

| Election | Method of Making Election | Date for Making Election | Authority | Explanation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Election to Terminate "S" Corporation Tax Treatment | File a statement signed by stockholders owning >50% of stock with IRS Service Center where corporation's S election was filed | On or before the 15th day of the 3rd month of the current taxable year | IRC §§1372(a) and 1362(d)(1)(c) | Terminates S corporation election; reverts to C corporate tax treatment |
| Closing-of-Books Allocation of Income Election | Separate statement signed by corporate officer and each affected shareholder | Timely-filed (with extension) Form 1120-S | IRC §1377(a)(2) | Allocates income in manner other than pro-rata |
| Election to Allocate Income in "S" Termination Year by Closing of the Books | Separate election by corporation and each shareholder | Due date (with extension) of C corporate short-year return | IRC §1362(e)(3) | Allocates income in manner other than pro-rata |
| Election to Distribute E&P Before AAA | Separate election statement from shareholders | Due date (with extension) | IRC §1368(e)(3) (per regs) | Allows distribution of taxable income from C-era earnings before AAA tax-free |
| Termination of §444 Election to Use Special Year-End | Attach statement to return for short period resulting from election | Due date (with extension) of short-period return | Temp. Reg. §1.444-1T | Allows taxpayer to change back to a permissible tax year |
| Deemed Dividend Election | Separate election statement from shareholders | Due date (with extension) of Form 1120-S | Reg. §1.1368-1(f)(3) | Allows non-cash book entry to record deemed dividend |
| Grouping Activities | Attach identifying statement to return when changing, adding, or disposing | Due date plus extension of first return where grouping is made | Reg. §1.469-4 | Allows one or more trades, businesses, or rentals to be treated as one activity for passive loss purposes |
| Election by Shareholder to Re-Order Basis Adjustments | Separate statement by shareholder attached to Form 1120-S | Due date (with extension) of Form 1120-S | Reg. §1.1367-1(g) | Allows shareholder to re-arrange ordering rules on expense allocations |
| Election to Override Interest-Tracing Rules | No specific guidance — categorization on return appears sufficient | Due date of return (with extension) for 1st use of debt proceeds | IRC §163; Reg. §1.163-8T(c)(4) | Allows S corp to treat an expenditure from any account, within 30 days of depositing debt proceeds into that account, as a disbursement of such proceeds |
