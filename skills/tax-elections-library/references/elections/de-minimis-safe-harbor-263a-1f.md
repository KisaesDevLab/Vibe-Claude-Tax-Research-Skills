## C2. De Minimis Safe Harbor (Reg. §1.263(a)-1(f))

**Authority:** Treas. Reg. §1.263(a)-1(f); the tangible property
regulations finalized in T.D. 9636 (2013).

**Purpose.** Provides a safe harbor under which a taxpayer can deduct
amounts paid for tangible property (rather than capitalize) if the
amount per item or per invoice does not exceed:

- **$5,000** for taxpayers with an Applicable Financial Statement
  (AFS), defined in Reg. §1.263(a)-1(f)(4).
- **$2,500** for taxpayers without an AFS.

**Effect.** Removes the capitalize-vs-expense judgment for items
under threshold. Eliminates §263A UNICAP issues for those items.
Eliminates depreciation tracking and recapture.

**When to file.** Annual election; attach statement to the timely-filed
return (with extensions) for each year the election is to apply.
Must be made annually; not automatic.

**Where to file.** With Form 1040 / 1120 / 1065 / 1120-S.

**Required policy.** The taxpayer must have a written accounting
procedure in place at the **beginning** of the tax year that:

1. Treats amounts paid for property costing less than the applicable
   threshold (or having an economic useful life of 12 months or less)
   as expenses for non-tax (i.e., book) purposes.
2. Is followed consistently for both book and tax purposes.

**Sample written accounting policy (must be adopted before year start):**

```
[ENTITY NAME], address, FEIN, hereby adopts for book and Federal
income tax purposes the following policy regarding capitalization
expenses for the year beginning January 1, 20__.

In accordance with Internal Revenue Code Sections 167 and 168 and
related Regulations at 1.263(a)-1(f), [ENTITY NAME] has determined
that amounts whose individual cost (including tax, installation,
and delivery costs) does not exceed $2,500 [or $5,000 for AFS
taxpayers] will be deducted as incurred as an operating expense.
Amounts exceeding this dollar limit will be examined individually
to determine if their use or purpose requires capitalization under
the betterment, adaptation, or restoration rules used by the
Internal Revenue Service and will be capitalized or expensed as
incurred as a result of the application of those rules.

Date adopted: _______________
Signed: _____________________
Title:  _____________________
```

**Sample election attachment to return:**

```
DE MINIMIS SAFE HARBOR ELECTION
Treas. Reg. § 1.263(a)-1(f)

Taxpayer:       __________________________________________
FEIN/SSN:       __________________________________________
Tax Year:       __________________________________________

The taxpayer hereby elects under Treas. Reg. § 1.263(a)-1(f) to
apply the de minimis safe harbor for the above tax year.

The taxpayer's threshold under the safe harbor is:
   ☐ $5,000 per item (AFS taxpayer)
   ☐ $2,500 per item (non-AFS taxpayer)

The taxpayer has had a written accounting procedure in place since
the beginning of the tax year that treats amounts paid for property
under the threshold as expenses for non-tax purposes. The taxpayer
treats such amounts consistently as expenses for both book and tax
purposes.
```

**Notes / Pitfalls.**

- Threshold applies **per item** (or per invoice if invoice itemizes
  multiple items, then per item on invoice). Cannot aggregate
  multiple items to stay under threshold.
- Must have written policy **before** year begins. Mid-year adoption
  of policy does not qualify the prior portion of the year.
- AFS = audited financial statement, SEC-filed financial statement,
  or a financial statement required by federal/state government
  agency other than the IRS. Reviewed financial statement does NOT
  qualify as AFS.
- Election is **annual**; must be made each year on a timely-filed
  return (with extensions).
- Items deducted under safe harbor are not subject to §263A UNICAP
  (Reg. §1.263A-1(b)(13)).
- Deducted items still must be ordinary and necessary §162 business
  expenses.
- Different from §179 expensing — de minimis is automatic if elected;
  §179 has annual limits and income limitations.
