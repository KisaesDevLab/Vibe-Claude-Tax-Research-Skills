## H1. §451(d) Defer Crop Insurance Proceeds

**Authority:** IRC §451(d); Treas. Reg. §1.451-6.

**Purpose.** A cash-method farmer may elect to defer reporting of
crop insurance and disaster payments received in the current year
to the **following year**, when the insurance proceeds compensate
for crop damage or destruction that would normally have produced
income in the following year under the farmer's normal business
practice.

**Eligibility (§451(d)):**

1. Taxpayer is in the **trade or business of farming**.
2. Taxpayer reports income on the **cash receipts and disbursements
   method** of accounting.
3. Insurance / disaster payment is received because of **destruction
   or damage** to crops.
4. Under the farmer's **normal business practice**, the income from
   the damaged or destroyed crops would have been included in gross
   income for a tax year **following** the year of destruction or
   damage.

**When to file.** Attach to the timely-filed (with extensions)
return for the year the crop insurance proceeds are received.

**Where to file.** With Form 1040 / Schedule F.

**Notes / Pitfalls.**

- "Normal business practice" must support that income from the
  damaged crops would have been recognized in the **following**
  year. Documentation of the farmer's typical sale timing
  (storage, contract dates, etc.) is critical.
- One-year deferral only — cannot defer 2+ years.
- Election applies to **all** qualifying crop insurance / disaster
  payments received in the year, not selectively per payment.
- Disaster-related payments under federal disaster declarations may
  qualify even if not technically "crop insurance" — see Treas.
  Reg. §1.451-6.
- Cannot be combined with §1033 involuntary conversion deferral
  for the same proceeds.
- Election is annual; must be made each year crop insurance is
  received and deferral is desired.
