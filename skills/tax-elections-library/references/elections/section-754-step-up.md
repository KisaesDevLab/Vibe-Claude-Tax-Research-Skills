## E1. §754 Step-Up Election

**Authority:** IRC §754; Treas. Reg. §1.754-1; IRC §734(b); §743(b);
§755.

**Purpose.** Permits a partnership to elect to adjust the basis of
partnership property in two situations:

1. **§743(b) — Transfer of partnership interest.** Upon sale,
   exchange, or transfer-on-death of a partnership interest, the
   basis of partnership property is adjusted with respect to the
   transferee partner only, generally to step up (or step down) the
   transferee's share of inside basis to match outside basis.
2. **§734(b) — Distribution of partnership property.** Upon
   distribution of partnership property to a partner, the basis of
   remaining partnership property is adjusted.

**§755** allocates the §734/§743 adjustments among the partnership's
specific assets.

**When to file.** Attach the election statement to a timely-filed
(with extensions) Form 1065 for the year in which the triggering
event occurs (sale, exchange, transfer, or distribution).

**Where to file.** With Form 1065.

**Effect.** Once made, the §754 election applies to all subsequent
transfers and distributions until revoked. **Revocation requires IRS
consent.** The election is therefore essentially permanent in
practice.

**Mandatory adjustment scenarios (without election).** Even without a
§754 election, mandatory adjustments may occur under:

- **§743(b) substantial built-in loss (>$250,000)** — mandatory step-
  down on transfer of partnership interest.
- **§734(b) substantial basis reduction (>$250,000)** — mandatory
  step-down on certain distributions.

**Sample election language:**

```
SECTION 754 ELECTION STATEMENT
(Pursuant to Treas. Reg. § 1.754-1(b)(1))

Partnership/LLC Name: ____________________________________________
Federal ID #:         ____________________________________________
Tax Year Ending:      ____________________________________________

The partnership hereby elects, pursuant to IRC Section 754, to
adjust the basis of partnership property as a result of a
distribution of property or a sale or exchange of a partnership
interest as provided in IRC Sections 734(b) and 743(b).

____________________________     ____________________________
General Partner Signature        Print General Partner's Name

Date: ______________________
```

**Notes / Pitfalls.**

- Effect is **forward-looking and permanent** without IRS consent
  to revoke. Many practitioners advise carefully because a §754
  election creates ongoing compliance burdens (annual basis
  adjustment tracking; allocation among assets; potential for
  step-downs in future transfers).
- §734(b) and §743(b) adjustments are **partner-specific** for §743
  (only the transferee partner is affected) and **partnership-wide**
  for §734 (basis of remaining assets is adjusted).
- §755 allocations among partnership assets follow detailed rules;
  often requires appraisal.
- For transferee partner under §743(b), Form 8308 (Report of a Sale
  or Exchange of Certain Partnership Interests) reporting may also
  be required.
- §754 election attaches at the **partnership** level; affects all
  partners.
- A §754 election cannot be made retroactively — only for the year
  of the triggering event.
