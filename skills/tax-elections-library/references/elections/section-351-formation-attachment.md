## A1. §351 Corporate Formation Filing Attachment

**Authority:** IRC §351; Treas. Reg. §1.351-3.

**Purpose.** §351 permits tax-free transfer of property to a
corporation in exchange for stock if the transferor(s) are in
"control" (defined as 80% by vote and value) immediately after the
exchange. Treas. Reg. §1.351-3 requires both transferor and
transferee to attach a statement to their respective tax returns
disclosing details of the transaction.

**When to file.** Attach to the timely-filed (with extensions)
return for the tax year of the transaction. One copy attached to
the transferor's return; one copy attached to the transferee
corporation's return. One statement per transferor.

**Where to file.** With the federal income tax return of each
party.

**Notes / Pitfalls.**

- "Control" is tested *immediately after* the exchange. Pre-arranged
  share dispositions may break control.
- Boot received (cash + non-stock property + assumed liabilities in
  excess of basis) triggers gain recognition under §351(b).
- Liabilities assumed in excess of aggregate basis of property
  transferred = gain under §357(c).
- Service contributions are NOT property and are taxable as
  compensation under §351(d).
- Disclosure failure does not invalidate the §351 treatment but is
  required by regulation; accuracy-related penalties may apply for
  inadequate disclosure.
