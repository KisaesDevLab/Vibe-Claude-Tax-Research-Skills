## F4. §1377(a)(2) Closing-of-Books Election

**Authority:** IRC §1377(a)(2); Treas. Reg. §1.1377-1(b).

**Purpose.** When a shareholder's interest is terminated during an S
corp's tax year (e.g., by sale, gift, or redemption), §1377(a)(1)
generally requires pro-rata daily allocation of S corp items between
the terminating shareholder and the continuing shareholders. The
§1377(a)(2) election permits the corporation to treat the year as if
it consisted of two short tax years — the first ending on the date
of termination, the second running from the day after termination
through year-end.

This more-precisely allocates income, gain, loss, deduction, and
credit between pre- and post-termination periods.

**Eligibility.**

- A shareholder's interest must be **completely terminated** during
  the year (not merely reduced).
- The corporation and **all affected shareholders** must consent.
- "Affected shareholders" = the terminating shareholder and any
  shareholder to whom the terminating shareholder transferred shares.

**When to file.** Attach to the timely-filed (with extensions) Form
1120-S for the year of termination.

**Where to file.** With Form 1120-S.

**Sample election language:**

```
ELECTION TO TREAT TAX YEAR AS IF IT CONSISTED OF
2 TAX YEARS UPON DISPOSITION OF SHAREHOLDER'S STOCK
(Attach to timely filed 1120-S for year of transfer)

Company Name:                 _____________________________________
Address:                      _____________________________________
FEIN:                         _____________________________________
IRS Service Center:           _____________________________________
Corporate Year End:           _____________________________________

Name of Terminating Shareholder(s):
  _____________________________________________________________

Cause of Termination:
  _____________________________________________________________

Name of Acquiring Shareholder(s):
  _____________________________________________________________

Date of Event:
  _____________________________________________________________

The above corporation hereby elects under §1377(a)(2) and
Reg. §1.1377-1(b) to have the rules provided in §1377(a)(1)
applied as if the taxable year consisted of two taxable years.
All affected shareholders consent to this election.

Terminating Shareholder Consent:        Acquiring Shareholder Consent:
___________________________             ___________________________
Name: _____________________             Name: _____________________
Address: __________________             Address: __________________
SSN: ______________________             SSN: ______________________
Date: _____________________             Date: _____________________
```

*Note: Treas. Reg. §1.1377-1 eliminated the formal signature
requirement, although signed consents remain best practice.*

**Notes / Pitfalls.**

- Election applies **only to the affected shareholders' allocations**;
  remaining shareholders' allocations are still pro-rata daily for
  the full year unless §1362(e)(3) (S termination year) is also in
  play.
- If the corporation experiences a §1362(e) termination event during
  the year (e.g., loss of S status), §1362(e)(3) closing-of-books is
  separately available.
- Useful when income is concentrated in one period (e.g., a large
  sale of inventory or recognition of capital gain near year-end)
  and pro-rata daily allocation would distort the terminating
  shareholder's K-1.
- All affected shareholders' consent is required — single
  shareholder dissent defeats the election.
- Once made, election is binding for the year.
