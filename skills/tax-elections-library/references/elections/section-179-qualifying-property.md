## C3. §179 Qualifying Property Reference (2025)

**Authority:** IRC §179; Treas. Reg. §1.179-1 through §1.179-6.

**2025 limits.**

- **§179 expense limit:** $2,500,000 (subject to verification under
  current law / OBBBA).
- **Phase-out threshold:** $4,000,000.
- **SUV limit (§179(b)(5)):** Verify current-year SUV cap.

**Qualifying property — tangible personal property in 3-10 year asset
class lives**, with the following exceptions and additions:

**Excluded from §179:**

- Air conditioning and heating systems (when not classified as
  qualifying improvement property — see below).
- Property purchased from related parties (per §179(d)(2)).
- Property used predominantly outside the United States.
- Orchards, groves, and citrus trees.
- Residential rental property and its personal property.

**Specifically included in §179:**

- **Single-purpose agricultural structures**, drainage tile, and
  agricultural fences.
- **Property contained in or attached to a building** (other than
  structural components), such as refrigerators, grocery store
  counters, office equipment, printing presses, testing equipment,
  and signs.
- **Gasoline storage tanks and pumps** at retail service stations.
- **Livestock**, including horses, cattle, hogs, sheep, goats, mink,
  and other furbearing animals.
- **Property used predominantly to furnish lodging** (lodging
  property exclusion eliminated by 2017 Tax Cuts Act). IRC
  §50(b)(2) includes any facility where sleeping accommodations are
  rented.

**Improvements to nonresidential real property** placed in service
**after** the date the building was originally placed in service
(2017 Tax Cuts Act addition):

- **Qualified improvement property** (interior improvements to
  nonresidential real property; excludes enlargements, internal
  structural framework, elevators/escalators).
- **Commercial roofs.**
- **Heating, ventilation, and air-conditioning (HVAC) property.**
- **Fire protection and alarm systems.**
- **Security systems.**

**Notes / Pitfalls.**

- §179 cannot create or increase a Schedule C / Schedule F / K-1
  active business loss — limited to taxable income from active
  trades or businesses (§179(b)(3)). Excess carries forward.
- §179 reduces basis for §168(k) bonus calculation; bonus applies to
  remaining basis.
- §179 election is per-asset on Form 4562; can elect for some assets
  and not others.
- State decoupling is widespread — track separately.
- For light passenger autos, §280F luxury auto cap interacts with
  §179.
- For heavy SUVs (>6,000 lbs GVWR), §179(b)(5) imposes a separate
  SUV-specific cap (verify current-year amount).
- OBBBA (2025) — verify final §179 limits under Pub L 119-21.
