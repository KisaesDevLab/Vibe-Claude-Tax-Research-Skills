## D1. §469 Activity Grouping Election (Reg. §1.469-4)

**Authority:** Treas. Reg. §1.469-4; Rev. Proc. 2010-13.

**Purpose.** Permits a taxpayer to group two or more trade-or-business
activities or rental activities together as a single activity for
§469 passive activity purposes if they constitute an "appropriate
economic unit." Grouping affects:

1. **Material participation.** A taxpayer can satisfy material
   participation across grouped activities collectively rather than
   for each separately.
2. **Loss release on disposition.** Suspended passive losses
   released only on full disposition of grouped activity.
3. **Self-rental rule (Reg. §1.469-2(f)(6)).** Grouping interaction
   with self-rental.

**When to file.** New or modified groupings made on or after
**1/25/2010** must be disclosed on a written statement attached to
the return for the year of formation, addition, regrouping, or
disposition. Failure to disclose creates each activity as separate
unless reasonable cause is shown.

**Where to file.** With Form 1040 / 1120-S / 1065.

**Annual disclosure required for:**

1. **New grouping** — first year a grouping is established.
2. **Addition to grouping** — when an activity is added.
3. **Regrouping** — when a grouping is modified.
4. **Disposition** — when an activity is disposed.

**No annual disclosure required if** the existing grouping is
unchanged from the prior year.

**Sample election language:**

```
GROUPING ELECTION FOR NEW OR MODIFIED ACTIVITIES

  Name / Address / EIN: _______________________________________
```

**Notes / Pitfalls.**

- "Appropriate economic unit" is determined under five facts and
  circumstances factors per Reg. §1.469-4(c): similarities/differences
  in types of trade or business; extent of common control; extent of
  common ownership; geographical location; interdependencies (e.g.,
  buying/selling between activities; single set of books).
- Rental activities **cannot** generally be grouped with non-rental
  activities (Reg. §1.469-4(d)) except where one is "insubstantial"
  in relation to the other or 50% / 50% ownership.
- Grouping is generally **binding in subsequent years** unless the
  facts and circumstances change materially or the taxpayer
  regroups (which itself requires disclosure).
- IRS may regroup activities if the taxpayer's grouping does not
  reflect an appropriate economic unit and a principal purpose is
  tax avoidance.
- Real estate professional aggregation election (D2) is a
  **separate** election under §1.469-9(g) that aggregates rental
  activities specifically. The two operate independently but both
  may apply to the same taxpayer.
