## F3. ESBT Election (§1361(e)(3))

**Authority:** IRC §1361(e); Treas. Reg. §1.1361-1(m).

**Purpose.** Allows an Electing Small Business Trust (ESBT) to hold
S corp stock without disqualifying the S election. Unlike QSSTs,
ESBTs may have multiple beneficiaries and accumulate income.
However, the S corp portion of the ESBT's income is taxed at the
**highest individual rate** (currently 37%) rather than flowing
through to beneficiaries.

**Eligibility (§1361(e)(1)):**

1. The trust does not have any beneficiary other than:
   (i) An individual,
   (ii) An estate, or
   (iii) A §170(c)(2)-(5) charitable organization that holds a
        contingent remainder interest.
2. No interest in the trust was acquired by purchase.
3. The trust files an ESBT election.

**Who makes the election.** The **trustee** files the ESBT election
(unlike QSST, where the beneficiary files).

**When to file.** No later than 2 months and 15 days after the date
the stock was transferred to the trust (or the date the trust first
becomes eligible as an ESBT).

**Where to file.** Mail to the IRS Service Center where the
**corporation** files its return.

**Sample election language:**

```
QUALIFIED ELECTING SMALL BUSINESS TRUST ELECTION
Made under IRC Section 1361(e)(3)

Potential Current Beneficiary:     Trust:                      Corporation:
Name:    ____________________      Name:    _______________    Name:    _______________
Address: ____________________      Address: _______________    Address: _______________
TIN:     ____________________      TIN:     _______________    TIN:     _______________

The potential current beneficiary, ________________ TIN ________
is making the qualified ESBT election herein in accordance with
Internal Revenue Code Section 1361(e)(3) effective ______________
pursuant to Internal Revenue Code Section 1361(c)(2)(A)(v).
_____ shares of stock were transferred to the _________ Trust
TIN _______________ on _________________.

The _____________ Trust (TIN _________________) includes all
provisions of Internal Revenue Regulation 1.1361-1(m)(2):

1. This election is made under Internal Revenue Code §1361(e)(3).

2. The stock was transferred to the trust ________________.

3. The election is to be effective _______________________.

4. The trustee represents:
   (1) The trust meets the definitional requirements of
       §1361(e)(1); and
   (2) All potential current beneficiaries of the trust meet the
       shareholder requirements of §1361(b)(1).

Signed by the trustee, _____________________,
this ___ day of __________, 20__.

___________________________________
Trustee
```

**Notes / Pitfalls.**

- **S corp portion taxed at top individual rate** (37% as of 2025).
  This is generally less favorable than QSST flow-through (where
  beneficiary's marginal rate applies).
- However, ESBT permits **multiple beneficiaries**, **discretionary
  distributions**, and **income accumulation** — features QSST
  cannot accommodate.
- ESBT is preferred when the trust has multiple beneficiaries with
  varying income needs, when the grantor wants discretionary
  distributions, or when minor beneficiaries are involved.
- A "potential current beneficiary" includes any person who, at the
  time, is entitled to or is in the discretion of any person to
  receive a distribution from the principal or income of the trust.
  All potential current beneficiaries are counted as S corp
  shareholders for the §1361(b)(1) 100-shareholder test.
- TCJA permitted nonresident alien (NRA) beneficiaries of an ESBT
  (post-2017) — formerly disqualifying.
- Conversion between QSST and ESBT is permitted under specific
  procedures (Reg. §1.1361-1(j)(12) and (m)(7)).
