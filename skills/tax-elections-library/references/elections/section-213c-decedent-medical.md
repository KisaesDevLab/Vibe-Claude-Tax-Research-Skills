## G1. §213(c) Election to Deduct Decedent's Medical Expenses on Form 1040

**Authority:** IRC §213(c); Treas. Reg. §1.213-1(d)(2).

**Purpose.** When a taxpayer dies, medical expenses paid by the
estate within **one year after the date of death** for medical care
of the decedent provided **before death** are generally deductible on
Form 706 (estate tax) under §2053 OR on Form 1040 (final or amended
final return) under §213. The §213(c) election permits the executor
to claim the deduction on Form 1040 (the decedent's final or amended
final return) instead of Form 706, with a corresponding waiver of
the §2053 estate-tax deduction.

**Why elect Form 1040 deduction instead of Form 706.** Often the
income tax benefit on Form 1040 (effective rate up to 37%) exceeds
the estate tax benefit on Form 706 (top rate 40%, but only if
estate exceeds the exclusion). For estates below the federal
exclusion ($13.61 million in 2024 / $13.99 million in 2025), no
estate tax is owed regardless of the Form 706 medical expense
deduction, so the Form 1040 election is the only path to extract
tax benefit.

**Eligibility (§213(c)):**

1. Medical expenses must be **paid out of the decedent's estate**
   (or by other parties) within **one year after the date of death**.
2. Expenses must be for **medical care of the decedent** provided
   before death.
3. **Waiver** of §2053 estate-tax deduction must be filed.

**Who makes the election.** Generally the executor of the estate,
acting on behalf of the decedent, files the amended or final Form
1040 with the §213(c) election attached.

**When to file.** With the decedent's final Form 1040 for the year
of death, OR amended final Form 1040 if expenses are paid post-filing
but within the one-year-after-death window.

**Sample election language:**

```
ELECTION TO EXPENSE DECEDENT'S MEDICAL EXPENSES

Decedent:                ___________________________________________
SSN:                     ___________________________________________
Date of Death:           ___________________________________________

Election under §213(c) and Treas. Reg. §1.213-1(d)(2):

The taxpayer hereby elects to deduct the following expenses, paid
within one year of the decedent's death, on Form 1040 for the year
ended December 31, [YEAR].

These expenses have not been claimed under §2053 on Form 706; and
further, the taxpayer hereby waives the right to deduct the
expenses at any time for estate tax purposes under §2053.

Expense category:                                         Amount:
   Doctors and hospitals                                 $ ______
   Prescriptions                                         $ ______
   Medical equipment                                     $ ______
   Long-term care                                        $ ______
   Other medical (specify): ______________________       $ ______
   __________________________________________________
   Total                                                 $ ______

Executor:                ___________________________________________
Date:                    ___________________________________________
Signature:               ___________________________________________
```

**Notes / Pitfalls.**

- The waiver of §2053 is **irrevocable** for the items elected.
  Cannot claim the same expense on both Form 1040 and Form 706.
- Expenses paid by the surviving spouse from joint funds are
  generally still eligible; payment source matters less than payment
  timing (within one year of death).
- §213(a) 7.5%-of-AGI threshold still applies on Form 1040.
- For estates below the federal exclusion, the §213(c) election is
  nearly always preferable because the §2053 deduction provides no
  benefit (no federal estate tax liability to reduce).
- For estates above the federal exclusion, run a comparison: Form
  706 marginal rate (40% above the exclusion) vs. Form 1040
  marginal rate. Sometimes the Form 706 deduction provides greater
  benefit.
- State estate tax may also factor in for estates in states with
  decoupled estate taxes (CT, NY, MA, IL, etc.) — coordinate state
  treatment.
