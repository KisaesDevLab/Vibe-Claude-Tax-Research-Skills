## F9. Shareholder Consent to Revocation of S Election

**Authority:** IRC §1362(d)(1)(B); Treas. Reg. §1.1362-6(a)(3).

**Purpose.** The shareholder consent statement that accompanies the
corporate revocation under F8. Each consenting shareholder signs
individually, certifying ownership and consent. Cumulative consents
must represent more than 50% of issued and outstanding shares.

**Sample shareholder consent:**

```
SHAREHOLDER CONSENT TO REVOCATION OF S CORPORATION ELECTION
Under IRC § 1362(d)(1)

Taxpayer Name:                        ___________________________
Address:                              ___________________________
                                      ___________________________
SSN/FEIN:                             ___________________________

Number of Shares Owned:               ___________________________
Tax Year End:                         ___________________________
Date Acquired:                        ___________________________
Percentage of issued & outstanding
shares of stock owned:                ___________________________

S Corporation Name:                   ___________________________
Address:                              ___________________________
                                      ___________________________
FEIN:                                 ___________________________

Total shares issued & outstanding
at effective date:                    ___________________________

Effective date of S revocation:       ___________________________

I hereby consent to the revocation of the S election for the
above corporation effective 1/1/20__. I certify under penalty of
perjury that the personal information given below is to the best
of my belief true, correct, and complete.

Signed: ___________________________   Date: _____________________
```

**Notes / Pitfalls.**

- Consent must be signed individually by each shareholder; corporate
  shareholders sign through authorized officer.
- Aggregate consents must exceed 50% of issued and outstanding
  voting + non-voting shares.
- Consent is **irrevocable** once filed. Shareholder cannot
  withdraw consent after the revocation effective date.
- For a community property state, both spouses generally must sign
  if shares are community property.
- For a trust, the trustee signs in trust capacity; for a QSST, the
  income beneficiary signs (treated as deemed owner under §1361(d)).
