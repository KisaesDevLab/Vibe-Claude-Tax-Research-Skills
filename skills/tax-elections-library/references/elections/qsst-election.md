## F2. QSST Election (§1361(d)(2))

**Authority:** IRC §1361(d)(2); Treas. Reg. §1.1361-1(j).

**Purpose.** Allows a Qualified Subchapter S Trust (QSST) to hold S
corp stock without disqualifying the S election. The current income
beneficiary is treated as the deemed owner of the S corp stock under
§1361(d)(1)(B), with all income, deductions, and credits flowing
through to the beneficiary.

**Eligibility (Reg. §1.1361-1(j)):**

A QSST must satisfy ALL of the following requirements:

1. **One income beneficiary.** During the life of the current income
   beneficiary, there will be only one income beneficiary of the
   trust. If husband and wife are co-beneficiaries, both must be
   U.S. residents or citizens AND must file joint returns.
2. **Corpus distribution restriction.** Any corpus distributed during
   the life of the current income beneficiary may be distributed
   only to that beneficiary.
3. **Termination event.** The current beneficiary's income interest
   in the trust terminates on the earlier of the beneficiary's death
   or upon termination of the trust.
4. **Asset distribution on termination.** Upon termination of the
   trust during the life of such income beneficiary, the trust
   distributes all its assets to such beneficiary.
5. **All income distributed currently.** The trust is required by
   its terms to distribute all of its income currently, OR the
   trustee distributes all of its income currently if not so
   required.
6. **No support obligation use.** No distribution of income or corpus
   by the trust will be in satisfaction of the grantor's legal
   obligation to support or maintain the income beneficiary.

**Who makes the election.** The **current income beneficiary** makes
the election (not the trustee).

**When to file.** No later than 2 months and 15 days after the date
the stock was transferred to the trust.

**Where to file.** Mail to the IRS Service Center where the
**corporation** files its return.

**Sample election language:**

```
QUALIFIED SUBCHAPTER S TRUST ELECTION
Made under IRC Section 1361(d)(2)

Current Income Beneficiary:        Trust:                      Corporation:
Name:    ____________________      Name:    _______________    Name:    _______________
Address: ____________________      Address: _______________    Address: _______________
TIN:     ____________________      TIN:     _______________    TIN:     _______________

The current income beneficiary, _________________ TIN __________
is making the qualified QSST election herein in accordance with
Internal Revenue Code Section 1361(d)(2) effective ______________
to treat the trust as a qualified Subchapter S trust pursuant to
IRC § 1361(c)(2)(A)(i). _____ shares of stock were transferred to
the _________ Trust TIN ________________ on _________________.

The _________ Trust (TIN _____________) includes all provisions of
Internal Revenue Regulation 1.1361-1(j)(6)(ii)(E) under the terms
of the trust and applicable local law:

(i)   During the life of the current income beneficiary, there
      will be only one income beneficiary of the trust (if husband
      and wife are beneficiaries, that they will file joint
      returns and that both are U.S. residents or citizens);

(ii)  Any corpus distributed during the life of the current income
      beneficiary may be distributed only to that beneficiary;

(iii) The current beneficiary's income interest in the trust will
      terminate on the earlier of the beneficiary's death or upon
      termination of the trust; and

(iv)  Upon the termination of the trust during the life of such
      income beneficiary, the trust will distribute all its assets
      to such beneficiary.

(2)   The trust is required to distribute all of its income
      currently, or that the trustee will distribute all of its
      income currently if not so required by the terms of the
      trust.

(3)   No distribution of income or corpus by the trust will be in
      satisfaction of the grantor's legal obligation to support or
      maintain the income beneficiary.

Signed by the current income beneficiary, _________________,
this ___ day of __________, 20__.

___________________________________
Current Income Beneficiary
```

**Notes / Pitfalls.**

- **Beneficiary makes the election**, not the trustee. Trustee
  cannot consent on the beneficiary's behalf.
- 2-month-and-15-day deadline runs from **date of stock transfer**,
  not from formation of the trust.
- The trust **must distribute all income currently**. If the trust
  document does not require this, the trustee must do it as a matter
  of practice — but the better practice is to amend the trust
  document.
- Once made, election is **binding** until trust terminates or
  beneficiary changes via permitted means.
- Each successive income beneficiary must file a new QSST election
  within 2 months and 15 days after becoming the income beneficiary.
- ESBT election (F3) is the principal alternative; ESBTs are taxed at
  trust-level top rates but offer more flexibility.
