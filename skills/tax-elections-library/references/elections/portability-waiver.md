## G2. Portability Election Waiver (§2010(c)(5)(A))

**Authority:** IRC §2010(c)(5)(A) (Deceased Spouse Unused Exclusion
— DSUE / "portability"); Form 706.

**Purpose.** A portability election allows the surviving spouse to
use the decedent spouse's unused federal estate tax exclusion
(DSUE) by adding it to the surviving spouse's own exclusion.
Portability is elected by **timely filing a complete Form 706** for
the decedent's estate, even if no estate tax is otherwise owed.

The "portability election waiver" is the **executor's affirmative
decision NOT to file Form 706** when filing would otherwise be
optional, thereby **waiving** the portability benefit. This
document records the executor's informed decision and shifts
liability for that decision from the tax preparer to the executor.

**Why a waiver document is needed.** When an estate is below the
federal exclusion ($13.61M for 2024 / $13.99M for 2025), Form 706
is not statutorily required. However, electing portability requires
filing Form 706 — usually at significant cost ($5,000 - $25,000+
in preparation fees plus appraisals). The executor must weigh:

- **Cost of Form 706** (preparer fees, appraisals, attorney review).
- **Probability that surviving spouse's combined estate** will
  exceed the surviving spouse's own exclusion at her/his death.
- **Risk that exclusion amounts decline** in future years (e.g.,
  TCJA exclusion sunset, scheduled to occur after 2025 absent
  legislation; OBBBA may have addressed).
- **Investment growth** of marital assets potentially pushing
  the survivor over her/his exclusion.

For modest combined estates, the cost of Form 706 may exceed the
reasonably-likely portability benefit. The waiver documents the
executor's decision and protects the tax preparer.

**Sample waiver language:**

```
PORTABILITY EXECUTOR STATEMENT

As executor of the estate, it is your responsibility to determine
if the cost of preparing the Federal Form 706 estate tax return
exceeds the potential benefit of the portability amount to be
derived from filing the Federal Estate Tax Form 706. Please
indicate below your decision whether to incur the cost of filing
Form 706 for the estate in order to protect the portability
amount or to not file Form 706 and forego any potential benefit
to be derived from filing a Federal Form 706 for the estate.

Estate of:        _______________________________________________
Date of Death:    _______________________________________________
FEIN:             _______________________________________________
Executor:         _______________________________________________

☐  I wish to file Form 706 for the above estate to elect
   portability of the deceased spouse's unused exclusion (DSUE).

   _______________________________________   _______________
   (Executor Signature)                       (Date)


-- OR --

ELECTION TO NOT FILE FORM 706
COMBINED WITH EXECUTOR ASSUMPTION OF LIABILITY

☐  I do not wish to file Form 706 for the above estate and
   understand that by not filing Form 706 in a timely and
   complete manner I am foregoing the ability to transfer the
   unused estate tax equivalent amount to the surviving spouse.
   I further understand that this action relieves the tax
   professional from any and all liability from the failure to
   make a portability election and transfers this liability to
   the executor.

   _______________________________________   _______________
   (Executor Signature)                       (Date)
```

**Notes / Pitfalls.**

- **Portability filing deadline:** Generally **9 months after date
  of death**, with 6-month extension available via Form 4768 (total
  15 months). **Rev. Proc. 2022-32** provides simplified late-filing
  relief for portability-only Form 706 filings up to **5 years after
  the date of death** without the need for a private letter ruling.
- The 5-year window under Rev. Proc. 2022-32 **only applies** if
  the estate is otherwise not required to file Form 706 (i.e.,
  below the exclusion threshold).
- Portability is **not available** if the surviving spouse has
  remarried at the time of subsequent death — only the most recent
  deceased spouse's DSUE is transferable.
- Generation-skipping transfer (GST) tax exemption is **NOT
  portable** — only the estate / gift exclusion. The executor's
  decision to forgo portability does not affect GST.
- Several state estate taxes do NOT have portability (e.g., NY).
  State-only Form 706-equivalent may still be required.
- TCJA doubled the exclusion; the doubled portion sunsets after
  2025 absent legislation. OBBBA — `[CITATION NEEDED — search:
  OBBBA estate tax exclusion Pub L 119-21]`. If the exclusion
  reverts to ~$7M (TCJA pre-doubling level adjusted for inflation),
  more estates will benefit from portability — making the waiver
  decision more consequential.
- Document the executor's decision in writing **regardless of
  outcome** (file or waive). This is core practitioner risk
  management.
