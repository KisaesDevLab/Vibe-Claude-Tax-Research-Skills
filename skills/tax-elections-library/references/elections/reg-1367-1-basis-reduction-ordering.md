## F5. Reg. §1.1367-1 Change in Basis-Reduction Ordering Rules

**Authority:** Treas. Reg. §1.1367-1(g).

**Purpose.** S corp shareholder basis is reduced by losses,
deductions, distributions, and certain nondeductible expenses /
oil-and-gas depletion. The default ordering rule reduces basis FIRST
by **distributions**, THEN by **nondeductible expenses and oil-and-
gas depletion**, and FINALLY by **deductible losses and deductions**.

The Reg. §1.1367-1(g) election reverses the second and third
priorities, reducing basis FIRST by **deductible losses and
deductions** before nondeductible expenses and oil-and-gas
depletion. This permits the shareholder to use deductible losses
in the current year (within basis) at the cost of deferring
nondeductible expenses / oil-and-gas depletion to future years
(carrying them over until basis exists).

**Effect.** Maximizes current deductible loss utilization at the
expense of nondeductible expense / depletion deferral.

**Who makes the election.** Each individual shareholder, not the
corporation. Each shareholder's election is independent.

**When to file.** Attach to the timely-filed (with extensions)
shareholder Form 1040 for the first year the election applies.
Once made, it is binding for that year and all future years until
revoked (revocation requires IRS consent).

**Where to file.** With Form 1040.

**Sample election language:**

```
S CORPORATION SHAREHOLDER ELECTION
TO CHANGE ORDERING RULES

Name of Individual:    _________________________________________
SSN:                   _________________________________________
Tax Year Ending:       _________________________________________

Election to Reduce Basis by Items of Loss or Deduction
Before Reducing Basis by Nondeductible Expenses
And Certain Oil and Gas Depletion

The taxpayer hereby elects, pursuant to Treas. Reg. §1.1367-1, to
reduce basis by items of loss or deduction before nondeductible
expenses and certain oil and gas depletion. The taxpayer agrees
to carry over to the succeeding year the nondeductible,
non-capital expenses and certain oil and gas depletion deductions
that exceed stock and debt basis.
```

**Notes / Pitfalls.**

- Election is **per shareholder**, not per corporation. Different
  shareholders of the same S corp may make different elections.
- Election is **binding in subsequent years** unless revoked.
- Useful when the shareholder has substantial losses that would
  otherwise be deferred under at-risk / passive activity rules but
  could be used currently if basis allowed.
- Carryover of nondeductible expenses / oil-and-gas depletion is
  preserved in subsequent years when basis becomes available.
- Coordinate with §469 passive activity, §465 at-risk, and §461(l)
  excess business loss limitations — basis is the **first**
  limitation, not the only one.
