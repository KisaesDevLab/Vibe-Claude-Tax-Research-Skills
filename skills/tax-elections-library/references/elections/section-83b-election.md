## B1. §83(b) Election (Restricted Stock / Property for Services)

**Authority:** IRC §83(b); Treas. Reg. §1.83-2.

**Purpose.** Allows a recipient of restricted property (typically
restricted stock) for services to elect to recognize ordinary income
in the year of transfer based on FMV at transfer (less amount paid),
rather than waiting until the property vests. After §83(b), all
post-transfer appreciation is capital gain; without §83(b),
appreciation through vesting is ordinary income.

**When to file.** **Within 30 days of the date of property transfer.**
The 30-day deadline is jurisdictional and cannot be extended. Late
filing voids the election.

**Where to file.**

- Mail the original election to the IRS service center where the
  taxpayer files Form 1040.
- Provide a copy to the person for whom the services were performed
  (typically the employer).
- Attach a copy to the Form 1040 for the year of transfer.

**Sample election language:**

```
SECTION 83(b) ELECTION

The undersigned taxpayer hereby elects, pursuant to § 83(b) of the
Internal Revenue Code of 1986, as amended, to include in gross
income as compensation for services the excess (if any) of the fair
market value of the shares described below over the amount paid for
those shares.

1. The name, taxpayer identification number, address of the
   undersigned, and the taxable year for which this election is
   being made are:

   TAXPAYER'S NAME:  ___________________________________________
   TAXPAYER'S TIN:   ___________________________________________
   ADDRESS:          ___________________________________________
   TAXABLE YEAR:     Calendar Year 20__

2. The property which is the subject of this election is _________
   shares of common stock of _______________________.

3. The property was transferred to the undersigned on [DATE].

4. The property is subject to the following restrictions:
   [Describe applicable restrictions here.]

5. The fair market value of the property at the time of transfer
   (determined without regard to any restriction other than a
   non-lapse restriction as defined in § 1.83-3(h) of the Income
   Tax Regulations) is:
   $______ per share × ________ shares = $___________.

6. For the property transferred, the undersigned paid:
   $______ per share × ________ shares = $______________.

7. The amount to include in gross income is $______________.
   [Item 5 minus Item 6.]

The undersigned taxpayer will file this election with the Internal
Revenue Service office with which taxpayer files his or her annual
income tax return not later than 30 days after the date of transfer
of the property. A copy of the election also will be furnished to
the person for whom the services were performed. Additionally, the
undersigned will include a copy of the election with his or her
income tax return for the taxable year in which the property is
transferred. The undersigned is the person performing the services
in connection with which the property was transferred.

Dated: _________________

Taxpayer: _____________________________________
```

**Notes / Pitfalls.**

- **30-day deadline is absolute.** No §9100 relief.
- File via certified mail with return receipt for proof of timely
  filing.
- Election is **irrevocable** without IRS consent.
- If property is later forfeited (e.g., employee leaves before
  vesting), no deduction is allowed for the §83(b) tax paid on the
  forfeited shares — taxpayer loses the tax.
- Best for situations where: (a) FMV at transfer is low, (b) taxpayer
  is confident of vesting, and (c) significant appreciation expected.
- For ISOs, §83(b) is not necessary (statutory option treatment
  applies); for NSOs and restricted stock, §83(b) is the principal
  planning lever.
