# Payroll engagement letter

Payroll-services engagement letter. Distinct from bookkeeping;
involves federal/state filing and tax-deposit responsibility.
§1.295 applies if client is also attest.

## Required elements

- [ ] Specific scope (calc, payment, filings, W-2/1099 issuance,
      state UI/SDI).
- [ ] Tax deposit responsibility (firm or client signs and
      deposits).
- [ ] Form 941, 940, W-3, state UI returns.
- [ ] §1.295 affirmation if client is attest.
- [ ] FTC Safeguards Rule clause.
- [ ] Penalty allocation if firm error.
- [ ] Fee, termination.

## Template

> **PAYROLL SERVICES ENGAGEMENT LETTER**
>
> [DATE]
>
> [CLIENT NAME / ADDRESS]
>
> Thank you for engaging [FIRM NAME] for payroll services.
>
> **Scope**
>
> We will provide:
> - Payroll calculation (gross-to-net) per pay period.
> - Direct-deposit setup and processing [if engaged].
> - Federal income tax, FICA, and FUTA withholding and deposit
>   computation.
> - State income tax, SUTA, and SDI/UI withholding and deposit
>   computation [for LISTED STATES].
> - Federal Forms 941 (quarterly), 940 (annual), W-2 (annual),
>   W-3 (annual).
> - State unemployment / withholding returns [for LISTED STATES].
> - 1099-NEC issuance to independent contractors (if you provide
>   information).
> - [Other specifically listed services.]
>
> NOT in scope:
> - Worker classification determinations (route to firm's
>   predict-worker-classification service if needed).
> - State registration and filing in additional states.
> - Garnishment, child-support order processing (unless agreed
>   separately).
> - Benefit administration.
>
> **Tax deposit authority**
>
> [SELECT ONE]
>
> [(a) FIRM SIGNS DEPOSITS:] Client authorizes the firm to make
> federal and state tax deposits on its behalf via the IRS EFTPS
> system and corresponding state systems. Client provides
> sufficient funds in the designated account by [DEADLINE]
> before each scheduled deposit.
>
> [(b) CLIENT SIGNS DEPOSITS:] The firm calculates the deposit
> amount and provides reminders. Client makes the deposit and
> retains responsibility for timely deposit.
>
> **§1.295 affirmation [if client is attest]**
>
> [If client is also an attest client, insert §1.295 four-
> condition language:]
>
> Client agrees that:
> - The firm does not assume management responsibilities.
> - Client designates [INDIVIDUAL] with sufficient skill,
>   knowledge, and experience to oversee payroll services.
> - Client evaluates the adequacy and results of services.
> - Client accepts responsibility for the results.
>
> **Penalty / interest allocation**
>
> If the firm errs in calculation or filing and as a result the
> client incurs a penalty or interest, the firm will reimburse
> the client for that penalty / interest, provided the client
> notifies us within [N] days of receiving the IRS or state
> notice. Client retains responsibility for penalties / interest
> arising from late or insufficient funds, late filing of
> 1099s, or client-provided information that was incorrect.
>
> **Data security — FTC Safeguards Rule**
>
> [SEE COMMON-CLAUSES + ftc-safeguards-rule.md]
>
> **Fees, termination, other terms**
>
> [SEE COMMON-CLAUSES TEMPLATE]
>
> Please sign to indicate your agreement.
>
> [FIRM NAME / SIGNATURE]
>
> Acknowledged: [CLIENT / DATE]

## Practitioner notes

- 941/940 corrections (Forms 941-X / 940-X) under separate
  engagement scope.
- For multistate payroll (employees in multiple jurisdictions),
  scope clearly identifies states; new-state additions require
  scope amendment.
- Worker classification (W-2 vs 1099) is a separate analysis;
  firm should not bundle into payroll engagement without
  appropriate scoping.
- §1.295 applies if client is attest; firm cannot supervise client
  payroll personnel and remain independent for attest.

## Authority

- AICPA Code §1.295: cite for §1.295 affirmation.
- IRC §3402, 3401, 3121, 3306, 6601, 6651, 6656: payroll-related
  citation.
- FTC Safeguards Rule: regulatory.
