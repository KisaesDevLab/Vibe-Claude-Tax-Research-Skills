# Consulting engagement letter (non-attest, non-tax)

Consulting / advisory engagements outside tax (financial
operations, business process, M&A due diligence, valuation,
software selection, internal-control consulting). §1.295 applies
if client is attest.

## Required elements

- [ ] Specific consulting scope.
- [ ] Deliverable (report / model / advice).
- [ ] No-assurance language (this is NOT an attest engagement).
- [ ] §1.295 affirmation if client is attest.
- [ ] Reliance on client information.
- [ ] FTC Safeguards Rule (if data security implicated).
- [ ] Limitation of liability (state overlay applies).
- [ ] Fee, termination, IP ownership.

## Template

> **CONSULTING ENGAGEMENT LETTER**
>
> [DATE]
>
> [CLIENT NAME / ADDRESS]
>
> Thank you for engaging [FIRM NAME] for the consulting services
> described below.
>
> **Scope**
>
> We will provide the following consulting services:
>
> [SPECIFIC SCOPE — e.g., "evaluation of the client's accounting
> system options for replacement of [LEGACY SYSTEM]"; "M&A
> financial due diligence on [TARGET]"; "internal-control gap
> assessment under COSO 2013 framework"; "process improvement on
> month-end close"; "valuation of [SUBJECT] for [PURPOSE]"].
>
> Specifically NOT in scope:
> - Audit, review, compilation, attestation engagements.
> - Tax return preparation or tax advisory.
> - Implementation of recommendations.
> - Legal advice.
> - Investment advice.
> - [Other specific exclusions].
>
> **Deliverable**
>
> We will deliver:
> - [Written report / financial model / recommendation memo /
>   other].
> - [Format and length].
> - [Restrictions on use — typically restricted to client's
>   internal use unless separately negotiated].
>
> Our work is for client's internal purposes only and is not
> intended for use by third parties unless we expressly agree
> in writing.
>
> **No assurance**
>
> Our engagement is a consulting engagement and is NOT performed
> in accordance with auditing, review, compilation, or
> attestation standards. We provide no opinion or conclusion on
> the subject matter; our work product is professional advice
> and should not be relied on as an attestation.
>
> **§1.295 affirmation [if client is attest]**
>
> [If client is an attest client, insert §1.295 four-condition
> language. Note: certain consulting services (financial-system
> design, internal-audit outsourcing, legal services) may impair
> independence regardless of §1.295 conditions; consult §1.295
> service-by-service screen before accepting.]
>
> **Reliance**
>
> Our work will rely on information provided by the client. We
> will not audit or independently verify it. Material changes in
> facts may require revision.
>
> **Intellectual property**
>
> [Specify ownership of work product, methodology used, and any
> templates / models retained for firm use. Typically: client
> owns the work product specific to client; firm retains
> ownership of underlying methodology, templates, and tools.]
>
> **Data security**
>
> [SEE COMMON-CLAUSES + ftc-safeguards-rule.md if engagement
> involves access to client tax / financial information.]
>
> **Fees, termination, other terms**
>
> [SEE COMMON-CLAUSES TEMPLATE]
>
> **Limitation of liability**
>
> [Non-attest engagements: limitation of liability is more often
> enforceable but state overlay applies. See
> indemnification-state-overlay.md.]
>
> Please sign to indicate your agreement.
>
> [FIRM NAME / SIGNATURE]
>
> Acknowledged: [CLIENT / DATE]

## Practitioner notes

- AICPA Code §1.295 service-by-service screen (especially
  financial-information system design, internal-audit
  outsourcing, legal services) controls when client is attest.
- For valuation services to attest client where the result is
  material to F/S: §1.295.155 generally PROHIBITS unless de
  minimis.
- For litigation-support services to attest client: advocacy
  threat; usually prohibited.
- Forensic services: investigative for management permitted;
  expert testimony adverse to client / management generally
  prohibited.
