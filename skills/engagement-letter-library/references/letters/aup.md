# Agreed-Upon Procedures (AUP) engagement letter (AT-C §215)

For AUP engagements where the practitioner performs only the
specified procedures the engaging party and intended users have
agreed to. Reports findings; no opinion or conclusion.
Independence is not required (with disclosure).

## Required elements

- [ ] Subject matter and criteria.
- [ ] Specific procedures agreed to (verbatim or by reference).
- [ ] Specified parties.
- [ ] Specified-parties acknowledgment of sufficiency of
      procedures.
- [ ] Findings-only reporting (no opinion / no conclusion).
- [ ] Restriction on use (intended users only).
- [ ] Independence (or disclosure of lack of independence).
- [ ] Fee, termination.

>

> **Specified parties acknowledgment**
>

> we will report findings only.

> - [If applicable: lack-of-independence disclosure.]

>

## Practitioner notes

- AUP is a "specified-party" engagement; the report is restricted
  to the specified parties who agreed to the procedures.
- AUP report does NOT use "opinion" or "conclusion" language.
- "Findings" are factual results of the procedures.
- AUP cannot be performed without the specified parties' written
  agreement that the procedures are sufficient for their
  purposes.
- Independence is NOT required; lack of independence must be
  disclosed in the report.
- Common AUP scopes: lender-required compliance procedures,
  inventory counts, accounts receivable confirmations,
  contractual compliance.
