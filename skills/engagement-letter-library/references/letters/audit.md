# Audit engagement letter (AU-C §210)

Required elements per AU-C §210 plus AU-C 200 series. This template
addresses a NONISSUER audit. For SEC issuer audits, route to PCAOB
externally.

## Required AICPA elements

- [ ] Objective and scope.
- [ ] Auditor responsibilities.
- [ ] Management responsibilities (F/S, internal control,
      compliance, providing access).
- [ ] Identification of framework.
- [ ] Form and content of audit report.
- [ ] Acknowledgment that report could differ if circumstances
      arise.
- [ ] Inherent limitations of an audit (reasonable but not
      absolute assurance).
- [ ] Communication framework (AU-C §260).
- [ ] Reference to AICPA / state-board ethical requirements.
- [ ] Independence affirmation.
- [ ] Fee arrangement.
- [ ] Termination provisions.
- [ ] Predecessor-auditor communication (AU-C §510 if first-year).
- [ ] Signed by both parties.

## Template

> **AUDIT ENGAGEMENT LETTER**
>
> [DATE]
>
> [CLIENT NAME]
> [CLIENT ADDRESS]
>
> Attention: [BOARD CHAIR / AUDIT COMMITTEE CHAIR / CEO]
>
> We are pleased to confirm our understanding of the audit
> engagement we will perform for [CLIENT NAME] for the
> [PERIOD/YEAR] ending [DATE]. This letter sets out the terms of
> our engagement.
>
> **Objective and scope**
>
> We will audit the financial statements of [CLIENT], which
> comprise the balance sheet as of [DATE], and the related
> statements of [income / changes in stockholders' equity / cash
> flows] for the [year/period] then ended, and the related notes
> to the financial statements. The financial statements will be
> prepared in accordance with [accounting principles generally
> accepted in the United States of America (U.S. GAAP) /
> tax-basis / cash-basis / regulatory-basis / contractual-basis].
>
> The objective of our audit is the expression of an opinion as
> to whether your financial statements are fairly presented, in
> all material respects, in conformity with [framework]. Our
> audit will be conducted in accordance with auditing standards
> generally accepted in the United States of America (GAAS) as
> issued by the AICPA.
>
> **Management's responsibilities**
>
> Management is responsible for:
> - The financial statements and their fair presentation in
>   accordance with [framework].
> - Designing, implementing, and maintaining internal control
>   over financial reporting.
> - Identifying and ensuring compliance with laws and regulations
>   applicable to the entity's activities.
> - Making all financial records, related information, and
>   personnel available to us.
> - Adjusting the financial statements to correct material
>   misstatements identified.
> - Providing written representations at the date of the audit
>   report.
>
> **Auditor's responsibilities**
>
> Our responsibilities under GAAS are to:
> - Plan and perform the audit to obtain reasonable assurance
>   that the financial statements are free from material
>   misstatement, whether due to fraud or error.
> - Express an opinion based on our audit.
>
> An audit involves performing procedures to obtain audit
> evidence about amounts and disclosures. The procedures
> selected depend on the auditor's judgment, including
> assessment of risks of material misstatement, whether due to
> fraud or error.
>
> Because of the inherent limitations of an audit, together with
> the inherent limitations of internal control, an unavoidable
> risk that some material misstatements may not be detected
> exists, even though the audit is properly planned and
> performed.
>
> **Form and content of report**
>
> Upon completion, we will issue a written report addressing the
> financial statements. Circumstances may arise in which our
> report may differ in form and content from the standard report
> (modified opinion, emphasis-of-matter, going-concern
> consideration, etc.). If we determine that we are unable to
> issue an audit report, or to complete the engagement, we may
> withdraw and so report.
>
> **Communication**
>
> We will communicate as required by AICPA standards (AU-C 260,
> 265). We will discuss significant matters with [audit
> committee / those charged with governance] including:
> - The auditor's responsibilities under GAAS.
> - Planned scope and timing.
> - Significant findings (significant judgments, difficulties,
>   disagreements, written representations).
> - Material weaknesses and significant deficiencies.
> - Independence representations.
>
> **Independence**
>
> We are independent of [Client] in accordance with the AICPA
> Code of Professional Conduct, applicable AICPA pronouncements,
> and applicable state-board requirements.
>
> **Predecessor-auditor communication** [if first-year audit]
>
> [Pursuant to AU-C §510, we will communicate with the
> predecessor auditor [NAME] before commencing the audit. Client
> agrees to authorize predecessor auditor to respond fully to our
> inquiries.]
>
> **Fees**
>
> [SEE COMMON-CLAUSES TEMPLATE — fee section]
>
> **Termination**
>
> [SEE COMMON-CLAUSES TEMPLATE]
>
> **Other terms**
>
> [Confidentiality / records retention / dispute resolution from
> common-clauses.]
>
> Please sign and return one copy of this letter to indicate your
> agreement.
>
> Sincerely,
>
> [PARTNER NAME]
> [FIRM NAME]
>
> Acknowledged and agreed:
>
> [CLIENT REPRESENTATIVE NAME]
> [TITLE]
> [DATE]

## Practitioner notes

- For SOC-served entities (using a service organization for
  significant transaction processing), AU-C §402 considerations
  apply; engagement letter may reference firm's intent to consume
  SOC reports.
- For group audits (AU-C §600), engagement letter must address
  reference / no-reference decision regarding component auditors.
- For nonissuer audits with KAM communication required by the
  engagement, reference AU-C §701.
- Indemnification of firm by client for firm's own acts: NOT
  permitted on attest engagements (AICPA Ethics Interpretation
  §1.400.205); see `../indemnification-state-overlay.md`.
- For Yellow Book / Single Audit / federal grant audits, route
  externally — out of scope.
