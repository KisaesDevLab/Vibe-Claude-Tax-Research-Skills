# Individual tax engagement letter (Form 1040)

Required elements per AICPA Tax Section practice aid + AICPA SSTS
+ FTC Safeguards Rule.

## Required elements

- [ ] Returns covered (1040, schedules, state filings, FBAR,
      FATCA, etc.).
- [ ] Tax year.
- [ ] Reliance-on-client-records disclaimer.
- [ ] FTC Safeguards Rule data-security clause.
- [ ] §7216 disclosure consent (for any third-party use).
- [ ] §6695 paid-preparer compliance affirmation.
- [ ] Penalty / interest exposure framework.
- [ ] Limitation of liability / indemnification (state overlay
      applies).
- [ ] Fee arrangement.
- [ ] Termination provisions.
- [ ] Out-of-scope work language.
- [ ] Records retention.

## Template

> **INDIVIDUAL INCOME TAX RETURN PREPARATION ENGAGEMENT LETTER**
>
> [DATE]
>
> [CLIENT NAME(S)]
> [CLIENT ADDRESS]
>
> Thank you for engaging [FIRM NAME] to prepare your individual
> income tax returns for the year ending [TAX YEAR]. This letter
> sets out the terms of our engagement.
>
> **Returns covered**
>
> We will prepare the following:
> - U.S. Form 1040 (federal individual income tax return) and
>   accompanying schedules.
> - State income tax returns for [LIST STATES].
> - [If applicable: Form 1040-SS / 1040-NR / FBAR / Form 8938 /
>   Schedule C / Schedule E / etc.]
>
> Returns NOT covered (out of scope):
> - [Form 706/709 estate/gift] — separate engagement.
> - [Form 1041] — separate engagement.
> - [Audit representation, IRS or state notice response] —
>   separate engagement.
> - [Any return not specifically listed above].
>
> Out-of-scope work will be billed separately at our standard
> hourly rates after additional written engagement letter.
>
> **Reliance on client records**
>
> We will rely on the information you provide us. We will not
> audit or independently verify the accuracy of the information.
> You are responsible for maintaining adequate records to support
> the items reported on your returns. We may, in our discretion,
> ask for clarification or additional records.
>
> **Penalty and interest exposure**
>
> We will advise you of significant penalty exposure under
> applicable provisions (e.g., IRC §6651, §6654, §6662). We will
> recommend disclosure on Form 8275 or Form 8275-R when warranted.
> The "audit lottery" is not a basis for return positions per
> Circular 230 §10.37.
>
> **Estimated tax payments**
>
> [If applicable: We will compute estimated tax payments for the
> [NEXT YEAR] tax year. You are responsible for making the
> estimated payments timely.]
>
> **Data security — FTC Safeguards Rule**
>
> The firm is a "financial institution" under the FTC Safeguards
> Rule (16 CFR Part 314) and has implemented a written
> information security program. The firm will maintain
> administrative, physical, and technical safeguards for your
> information. Please send sensitive information via our secure
> portal or encrypted email; do not transmit Social Security
> Numbers, bank account numbers, or other sensitive data via
> unencrypted email.
>
> **Confidentiality and §7216**
>
> The firm will maintain confidentiality per AICPA Code §1.700.
> IRC §7216 prohibits us from using or disclosing return
> information for any purpose other than tax preparation
> without your specific written consent. Disclosure to a related
> CPA-firm affiliate, lender, financial advisor, or other third
> party requires a separate §7216 consent that you sign before
> we disclose.
>
> **Records retention**
>
> The firm will retain workpapers and engagement files for [N]
> years from filing date in accordance with firm policy and
> applicable regulatory requirements. Originals provided by
> client will be returned promptly.
>
> **Fees**
>
> [SEE COMMON-CLAUSES TEMPLATE — fee section.] Note that
> contingent fees are NOT permitted for return preparation per
> AICPA Code §1.510.
>
> **Limitation of liability**
>
> [STATE OVERLAY APPLIES. Per `indemnification-state-overlay.md`,
> insert engagement-state-appropriate limitation language. For
> non-attest tax engagements, limitation of liability is more
> often enforceable but should be reasonable in light of fees and
> exposure.]
>
> **Termination**
>
> [SEE COMMON-CLAUSES TEMPLATE]
>
> **Other terms**
>
> [Confidentiality / dispute resolution / governing law from
> common-clauses.]
>
> Please sign to indicate your agreement.
>
> [FIRM NAME / SIGNATURE]
>
> Acknowledged: [CLIENT / DATE]

## Practitioner notes

- §7216 consent for any third-party use is mandatory before
  disclosure (criminal statute).
- Form 8879 e-file authorization is a separate document, not part
  of the engagement letter.
- AICPA SSTS §1.4 (engagement agreements) — confirm the agreement
  is made in writing.
- Circular 230 §10.32 (compilation of returns) — practitioner
  must comply with diligence-as-to-accuracy.

## Authority

- AICPA SSTS §1.4 (engagement agreements): cite as `AICPA_Code`
  (SSTS is part of the Code), `weight: binding_on_member`.
- IRC §7216 disclosure rules: cite as `IRC`, `weight: primary_statute`.
- FTC Safeguards Rule (16 CFR Part 314): cite as TreasReg-equivalent

- AICPA Code §1.510 (contingent fees): cite as `AICPA_Code`,
