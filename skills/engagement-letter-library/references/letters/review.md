# Review engagement letter (AR-C §90.10)

Required elements per AR-C §60.A21 + AR-C §90.10. Independence
REQUIRED — there is no lack-of-independence path for review.

## Required AICPA elements

- [ ] Objective: limited assurance via inquiry + analytical procs.
- [ ] Description of inquiry + analytical procedures.
- [ ] Performance per SSARS.
- [ ] Affirmation of independence.
- [ ] Statement that engagement is substantially less in scope
      than an audit.
- [ ] Management responsibilities (F/S, internal control, fraud
      prevention, compliance, providing access).
- [ ] Identification of framework.
- [ ] Form and content of review report.
- [ ] Going-concern evaluation.
- [ ] Communication framework.
- [ ] Fee arrangement.
- [ ] Termination provisions.
- [ ] Signed by both parties.

## Template

> **REVIEW ENGAGEMENT LETTER**
>
> [DATE]
>
> [CLIENT NAME]
> [CLIENT ADDRESS]
>
> We are pleased to confirm the terms of our engagement to
> review the financial statements of [CLIENT NAME] for the
> [PERIOD/YEAR] ending [DATE].
>
> **Objective**
>
> The objective of our engagement is to obtain limited assurance
> as a basis for reporting whether we are aware of any material
> modifications that should be made to the financial statements
> for them to be in accordance with [framework].
>
> **Procedures**
>
> Our review will consist primarily of:
> - Inquiries of company management and personnel responsible
>   for financial and accounting matters.
> - Analytical procedures applied to financial data.
>
> A review is substantially less in scope than an audit, the
> objective of which is the expression of an opinion regarding
> the financial statements as a whole. We will not express such
> an opinion. Our procedures do not contemplate obtaining an
> understanding of the entity's internal control or testing
> internal control.
>
> Our engagement will be performed in accordance with Statements
> on Standards for Accounting and Review Services (SSARS) issued
> by the AICPA.
>
> **Independence**
>
> We are independent of [Client] in accordance with the AICPA
> Code of Professional Conduct, applicable AICPA pronouncements,
> and applicable state-board requirements.
>
> **Management's responsibilities**
>
> Management is responsible for:
> - The financial statements and their fair presentation in
>   accordance with [framework].
> - Designing, implementing, and maintaining internal control.
> - Preventing and detecting fraud.
> - Compliance with laws and regulations.
> - Making records, information, and personnel available.
> - Providing written representations at the date of the review
>   report.
>
> **Going concern**
>
> Per AR-C §90.65, our procedures include consideration of
> conditions that may raise substantial doubt about the entity's
> ability to continue as a going concern within one year of the
> issuance date of the financial statements. The financial
> reporting framework's going-concern guidance (e.g., ASC 205-40
> for U.S. GAAP) governs disclosure.
>
> **Form and content of report**
>
> Upon completion, we will issue a written review report. The
> report will state our limited-assurance conclusion. The report
> may be modified for material departures from the framework,
> scope limitations, or going-concern uncertainty.
>
> **Communication**
>
> We will communicate with management regarding significant
> matters arising during the review.
>
> **Fees, termination, other terms**
>
> [SEE COMMON-CLAUSES TEMPLATE]
>
> **Indemnification limitation**
>
> The firm's liability is governed by applicable law and AICPA
> Ethics Interpretation §1.400.205. Indemnification provisions
> favoring the firm for the firm's own acts are not included on
> this attest engagement.
>
> Please sign to indicate your agreement.
>
> [PARTNER NAME / FIRM NAME]
>
> Acknowledged: [CLIENT / TITLE / DATE]

## Practitioner notes

- AR-C §90 requires independence; no lack-of-independence path
  for review. If firm is not independent, route to compilation
  with disclosure (AR-C §80.27).
- Going-concern integration with ASC 205-40 — disclosure
  inadequacy triggers report modification.
- Subsequent events procedures + written representations at
  report date.

## Authority

- AR-C §60 (general principles), §90 (review): primary.
- ASC 205-40 (going concern): cross-domain for U.S. GAAP F/S.
- AICPA Code §1.200, §1.400.205: independence + indemnification.
