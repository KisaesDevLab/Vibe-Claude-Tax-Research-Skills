# AU-C 600 — Audits of Group Financial Statements

AU-C 600 governs audits of group financial statements (a parent's
F/S consolidating one or more components). It addresses the work
of the **group engagement team** and the **component auditor**.

## Definitions

- **Group F/S**: F/S that include the financial information of more
  than one component.
- **Component**: an entity or business activity for which group or
  component management prepares financial information that is
  required by the framework to be included in the group F/S.
- **Group engagement team / partner**: the audit team / partner
  responsible for the group audit and for forming the opinion on
  the group F/S.
- **Component auditor**: an auditor who, at the request of the
  group engagement team, performs work on the financial information
  of a component.
- **Significant component**: a component that is of individual
  financial significance to the group OR is likely to include
  significant risks of material misstatement.

## Group engagement team responsibilities (AU-C §600.20)

The group engagement partner is responsible for:
- The direction, supervision, and performance of the group audit.
- The auditor's report on the group F/S.
- Sufficient appropriate audit evidence regarding the financial
  information of the components and the consolidation process.

## Reference / no-reference decision (AU-C §600.26+)

The group engagement team decides whether to **make reference to**
the component auditor in the group audit report or to **assume
responsibility** for the work of the component auditor.

### Reference path (AU-C §600.A86–.A100)

The group auditor refers to the component auditor's report in the
group audit opinion. Conditions:
- Component auditor's report meets AICPA / equivalent framework
  requirements.
- Component auditor is independent of the group F/S entity (per
  AICPA Code).
- Component F/S are presented in accordance with the same framework
  as the group F/S (or differences are immaterial).
- Component auditor performed audit procedures sufficient to
  support the audit report.
- The group engagement team has communicated with the component
  auditor regarding the work performed.

When the reference path is used, the group audit report explicitly
states which components were audited by other auditors and refers
to those auditors' reports.

### No-reference path (assume responsibility)

The group engagement team takes responsibility for the work
performed on the component. Conditions:
- The group engagement team must be involved in the component
  auditor's work to a degree sufficient to take responsibility:
  - Involvement in component auditor's risk assessment.
  - Determination of significant components.
  - Determination of materiality (component-level).
  - Approval of significant judgments.
  - Review of working papers (in person or remotely) at component
    auditors' files.
- The group engagement team is the signing auditor on the group
  audit report; no reference to the component auditor.

## Determining materiality (AU-C §600.31)

The group engagement team determines:
- **Group materiality** for the group F/S as a whole.
- **Component materiality** for components on which the group
  engagement team or component auditor will perform audit
  procedures. Component materiality is lower than group materiality
  to permit aggregation.
- **Component performance materiality** for component-level
  testing.
- **Threshold for trivial** for clearly-trivial misstatements at
  the component level.

## Communications with component auditors (AU-C §600.40+)

Group engagement team communicates:
- Inclusion of the component F/S in the group F/S.
- Group engagement team's expectations for component auditor work.
- Component materiality / threshold for trivial.
- Significant fraud / non-compliance risks.
- Identified related parties.
- Required communications back to group engagement team.

Component auditor communicates back:
- Sufficient relevant ethical requirements compliance.
- Information used in identifying significant components.
- Identified instances of fraud or noncompliance.
- Identified material misstatements.
- Going-concern matters.
- Corrected and uncorrected misstatements.
- Engagement quality review status.

## Documentation (AU-C §600.50)

- Analysis of components identifying significant components.
- Nature, timing, extent of involvement with component auditors.
- Reference / no-reference decision and rationale.
- Component-level materiality and procedures.
- Sufficient appropriate audit evidence.

## Special considerations

### Component auditor in another country
- Verify component auditor's professional qualifications.
- Understand the framework differences (if any).
- Reassure independence under both AICPA and the component auditor's
  jurisdiction.
- Communicate framework adjustments necessary for consolidation.

### Variable-interest entities (VIEs)
- Components may include VIEs consolidated under ASC 810.
- VIE-specific risks: primary-beneficiary determination,
  reconsideration events, complex consolidation entries.

### Foreign private-fund / fund-of-funds
- Component auditor often required.
- Materiality scaling for sub-fund testing.
