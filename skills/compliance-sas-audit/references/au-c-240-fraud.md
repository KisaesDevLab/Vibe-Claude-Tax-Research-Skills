# AU-C 240 — Fraud in a Financial Statement Audit

AU-C 240 establishes the auditor's responsibilities relating to
fraud. It applies a presumption that fraud risk exists in revenue
recognition and that management override of controls is a fraud
risk in every audit.

## Auditor objectives (AU-C §240.10)

- Identify and assess risks of material misstatement due to fraud.
- Obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence regarding the
  assessed risks.
- Respond appropriately to fraud or suspected fraud identified
  during the audit.

## Professional skepticism (AU-C §240.12)

Maintain professional skepticism throughout the audit. Recognize
the possibility that fraud may exist notwithstanding past honest
experience with management.

## Discussion among engagement team (AU-C §240.15)

- Required.
- Topics: how and where the F/S might be susceptible to material
  misstatement due to fraud; opportunities, incentives,
  rationalizations the entity might face.
- Documentation: significant decisions reached, including key
  members of the team and their participation.

## Risk-assessment procedures (AU-C §240.17–.24)

- Inquire of management about:
  - Management's assessment of fraud risk.
  - Management's process for identifying / responding to fraud risk.
  - Communications, if any, with governance regarding management's
    fraud-risk processes.
  - Communications, if any, with employees regarding ethics /
    fraud.
  - Knowledge of any actual, suspected, or alleged fraud.
- Inquire of governance:
  - Their oversight of management's processes for identifying and
    responding to fraud risk.
  - Knowledge of any actual / suspected / alleged fraud.
- Inquire of internal audit (if it exists):
  - Knowledge of fraud / suspected fraud.
- Consider unusual or unexpected relationships identified through
  analytical procedures.
- Consider other information (employee turnover at financial-
  reporting positions, regulatory issues, prior-period
  misstatements).

## Identification and assessment of fraud risk (AU-C §240.25–.28)

- Identify and assess risks of material misstatement due to fraud
  at the F/S level and the assertion level.
- Treat as a significant risk:
  - The presumption that fraud risks exist in revenue recognition
    (rebuttable; if rebutted, document the rationale).
  - The risk of management override of controls (cannot be
    rebutted — present in every audit).

## Response to assessed fraud risks (AU-C §240.29–.34)

### Overall responses
- Assignment and supervision of personnel; consider professional
  skepticism, expertise needed.
- Evaluation of accounting policies for inappropriate
  applications.
- Incorporate unpredictability into the nature, timing, extent of
  audit procedures (e.g., visiting different locations / dates,
  testing items not normally tested).

### Responses at the assertion level
- For each significant fraud risk: design audit procedures whose
  nature, timing, and extent are responsive to that risk.
- For revenue-recognition fraud risk: emphasize cut-off testing,
  contract review, journal-entry testing for revenue accounts.

### Responses to management override (mandatory; AU-C §240.32)

- Test journal entries and other adjustments at the end of a
  reporting period and during the period (sample of journal
  entries with fraud-risk characteristics, such as entries to
  unrelated accounts, entries by individuals not normally
  posting, entries booked at unusual times).
- Review accounting estimates for biases.
- Evaluate the business rationale for significant unusual
  transactions.

## Evaluation of audit evidence (AU-C §240.34–.37)

- Communicate any fraud or fraud-related events to the
  appropriate level of management at least one level above where
  the fraud is suspected, and to those charged with governance.
- If the fraud involves senior management: communicate with
  governance directly.
- Reassess the assessed risk of material misstatement due to
  fraud.

## Communication (AU-C §240.40–.42)

- To management at the appropriate level above the suspected
  party.
- To those charged with governance for fraud involving senior
  management OR fraud causing material misstatement.
- To regulators where required by law (be careful: regulatory
  reporting may conflict with confidentiality; consult firm
  counsel).
- AICPA Code §1.700 confidentiality applies to ANY discussion
  with parties outside the engagement.

## Documentation (AU-C §240.A88)

- The discussion among the engagement team.
- The risk-assessment procedures performed.
- The fraud risks identified.
- The responses to those risks.
- The results of audit procedures.
- The communications about fraud made to management, governance,
  regulators.

## Withdrawal (AU-C §240.A85)

If circumstances reveal fraud or suspected fraud the auditor cannot
appropriately address through audit procedures, OR management /
governance is unwilling to address the fraud, the auditor should
consider withdrawing from the engagement and consulting firm
counsel and ethics resources.
