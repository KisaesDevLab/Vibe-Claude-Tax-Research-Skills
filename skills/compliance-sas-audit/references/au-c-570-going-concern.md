# AU-C 570 — Going Concern

AU-C 570 establishes the auditor's responsibility to evaluate
whether substantial doubt exists about the entity's ability to
continue as a going concern for a reasonable period of time, and
to report on the matter.

## "Reasonable period of time"

For audits of nonissuer financial statements prepared under U.S.
GAAP:
- The "reasonable period of time" aligns with ASC 205-40, which
  is one year from the date the financial statements are issued
  (or available to be issued).
- For non-GAAP frameworks: the period defined by the framework, or
  if undefined, one year from the F/S date.

## Auditor objectives (AU-C §570.10)

- Obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence regarding the
  appropriateness of management's use of the going-concern basis
  of accounting.
- Conclude on whether substantial doubt exists about the entity's
  ability to continue as a going concern.
- Report appropriately.

## Risk-assessment procedures (AU-C §570.11–.14)

When performing risk assessment per AU-C 315, the auditor evaluates
whether events or conditions exist that may cast substantial doubt
about the entity's ability to continue as a going concern.

Indicators (AU-C §570.A3):
### Financial indicators
- Recurring operating losses.
- Negative cash flow from operations.
- Working-capital deficiencies.
- Inability to obtain financing.
- Default on outstanding debt or covenant violations.
- Nonpayment of dividends.
- Restructuring of debt.
- Disposal of substantial assets.
- Substantial sales of inventory at greatly reduced prices.

### Operating indicators
- Loss of key management without replacement.
- Loss of major customer / supplier / franchise.
- Labor difficulties.
- Shortages of supplies.
- Emergence of a strong competitor.

### Other indicators
- Pending litigation that, if successful against the entity, could
  result in adverse judgments the entity could not satisfy.
- Noncompliance with capital or other statutory requirements.
- Uninsured or underinsured catastrophe.

## Evaluating management's plans (AU-C §570.16)

When events or conditions are identified that may cast substantial
doubt:
1. Inquire of management about plans for future actions.
2. Evaluate management's plans:
   - Probability that plans can be effectively implemented.
   - Probability that plans will mitigate the conditions.
3. Obtain audit evidence to support the plans.
4. Conclude whether substantial doubt remains.

## Conclusion and reporting (AU-C §570.17–.27)

### Substantial doubt does NOT exist
- No emphasis-of-matter required.
- Standard unmodified opinion.

### Substantial doubt exists; mitigated by management plans
- The going-concern basis of accounting is appropriate.
- Required disclosure under ASC 205-40 (or framework equivalent).
- If disclosure is adequate: emphasis-of-matter paragraph in the
  audit report (AU-C §570.22).
- If disclosure is inadequate: modify opinion (qualified or adverse
  per AU-C 705).

### Substantial doubt exists; not mitigated by management plans
- The going-concern basis is still presumed appropriate UNLESS
  liquidation is imminent (then liquidation basis under ASC 205-30).
- Required disclosure under ASC 205-40 includes:
  - Principal conditions or events.
  - Management's evaluation of the significance of those
    conditions in relation to the entity's ability to meet its
    obligations.
  - Management's plans to mitigate.
- If disclosure is adequate: emphasis-of-matter paragraph stating
  substantial doubt about the entity's ability to continue as a
  going concern.
- If disclosure is inadequate: modify opinion.

### Liquidation basis
- If liquidation is imminent, the entity should report on the
  liquidation basis (ASC 205-30). The audit report addresses this
  framework as applicable.

### Late identification (AU-C §570.A26)
- If substantial-doubt conditions are identified after the
  auditor's report has been issued, consult firm counsel and AU-C
  560 (subsequent events) / AU-C 585 (subsequently discovered
  facts).

## Going-concern in audits of special-purpose F/S (AU-C §570.A29)

- Tax-basis: framework typically does not contemplate going-concern
  evaluation; auditor still considers conditions that affect the
  entity's continued operations.
- Cash basis: similar.
- Regulatory basis: framework requirements may differ.

## Communication (AU-C §570.27)

- Communicate to those charged with governance:
  - Existence of conditions or events that may cast substantial
    doubt.
  - Whether the substantial-doubt conclusion is reached.
  - Nature and adequacy of disclosure.
  - Audit report consequences.

## Documentation

- Conditions or events identified.
- Procedures performed to evaluate them.
- Management's plans evaluated.
- The conclusion reached and the basis for it.
- Audit-report wording considerations.
