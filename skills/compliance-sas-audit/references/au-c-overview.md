# AU-C — Sectional structure overview

The AICPA Auditing Standards Board issues SASs codified into the
AU-C "clarified" sections. The numbering parallels the ISA series
where applicable (200s = general principles, 300s = risk
assessment / response, 500s = audit evidence, 600s = use of others,
700s = reporting). Pin-cite to specific AU-C paragraph.

## Section structure

### AU-C 200 — Overall objectives and conduct of an audit
- AU-C 200 — Overall objectives.
- AU-C 210 — Terms of engagement (engagement letter required).
- AU-C 220 — Quality control for an engagement (now ties to SQMS 1
  / SQMS 2 firm system).
- AU-C 230 — Audit documentation.
- AU-C 240 — Fraud — see `au-c-240-fraud.md`.
- AU-C 250 — Consideration of laws and regulations.
- AU-C 260 — Communication with those charged with governance.
- AU-C 265 — Communicating internal control matters.

### AU-C 300 — Risk assessment and response
- AU-C 300 — Planning the audit.
- AU-C 315 — Understanding the entity and assessing RMM. See
  `au-c-315-330-risk.md`.
- AU-C 320 — Materiality.
- AU-C 330 — Performing audit procedures responsive to RMM. See
  `au-c-315-330-risk.md`.
- AU-C 402 — Audit considerations relating to entities using a
  service organization (SOC 1 reports often consumed under this
  section).

### AU-C 500 — Audit evidence
- AU-C 500 — Audit evidence.
- AU-C 501 — Specific items (inventory, litigation/claims/assess,
  segment information, accounts receivable confirmations).
- AU-C 505 — External confirmations.
- AU-C 510 — Initial audits.
- AU-C 520 — Analytical procedures.
- AU-C 530 — Audit sampling.
- AU-C 540 — Auditing accounting estimates, including fair-value
  estimates and related disclosures (significant 2022+ revisions).
- AU-C 550 — Related parties.
- AU-C 560 — Subsequent events and subsequently discovered facts.
- AU-C 570 — Going concern. See `au-c-570-going-concern.md`.
- AU-C 580 — Written representations.
- AU-C 585 — Consideration of omitted procedures after the report
  date.

### AU-C 600 — Using the work of others
- AU-C 600 — Special considerations: audits of group F/S
  (including the work of component auditors). See
  `au-c-600-group-audits.md`.
- AU-C 610 — Using the work of internal auditors.
- AU-C 620 — Using the work of an auditor's specialist.

### AU-C 700 — Reporting
- AU-C 700 — Forming an opinion and reporting.
- AU-C 701 — Communicating Key Audit Matters (KAMs) — required

- AU-C 705 — Modifications to the opinion.
- AU-C 706 — Emphasis-of-matter and other-matter paragraphs.
- AU-C 708 — Consistency of F/S.
- AU-C 720 — Other information in documents containing audited F/S

- AU-C 725 — Supplementary information in relation to the F/S.

### AU-C 800 — Specialized considerations

- AU-C 905 — Communications by independent auditors regarding
  consistency.
- AU-C 910 — Financial statements prepared under another country's

- AU-C 915 — Reports on application of requirements of an

## Engagement letter (AU-C 210)

Before commencing the audit, the engagement letter must include:
- Objective and scope of the audit.
- Responsibilities of the auditor.
- Responsibilities of management.
- Identification of the framework.
- Reference to the form and content of any reports.
- Statement of inherent limitations of an audit.
- Acknowledgment of fee arrangement.
- Communication framework (with whom, when).
- Going-concern reporting expectation.
- Reference to AICPA / state-board ethical requirements.
- Where applicable: arrangements regarding involvement of others
  (specialists, internal auditors, component auditors, predecessor
  auditor).

Cross-reference: `engagement-letter-library/references/letters/audit.md`.

## Audit documentation (AU-C 230)

- Sufficient for an experienced auditor not previously connected
  with the engagement to understand the work.
- Assembled into the final engagement file no later than 60 days
  after the report release date.
- Retained for AICPA-required minimum (5 years) or longer per
  state board / regulatory requirements.

## Communications with governance (AU-C 260)

- Auditor responsibilities.
- Planned scope and timing.
- Significant findings (qualitative aspects of accounting policies,
  significant difficulties, disagreements, other significant
  findings).
- Independence representations.

## Communicating internal control matters (AU-C 265)

- Material weaknesses and significant deficiencies in writing.
- Differentiation: material weakness (reasonable possibility that
  a material misstatement will not be prevented or detected on a
  timely basis) vs. significant deficiency (less severe but
  important enough to merit governance attention).
