# AU-C 700 series — Audit Reporting

AU-C 700 establishes the auditor's overall responsibility for
forming an opinion on the F/S and for reporting on the audit.
Subsidiary sections handle modifications (AU-C 705),
emphasis-of-matter (AU-C 706), KAMs (AU-C 701), and consistency
(AU-C 708).

## AU-C 700 — Forming an opinion

### Forming the opinion (AU-C §700.13)

The auditor forms the opinion based on an evaluation of:
- Whether sufficient appropriate audit evidence has been obtained.
- Whether uncorrected misstatements are material individually or
  in aggregate.
- Whether the F/S as a whole are free from material misstatement.

### Required elements of the audit report (AU-C §700.21–.34)

- **Title** including the word "Independent."
- **Addressee**.
- **Auditor's opinion** (separate section, headed "Opinion").
- **Basis for opinion** (separate section).
- **Going concern** (separate section if substantial doubt was
  evaluated).
- **Key audit matters** (when communication is required by terms
  of engagement; for many nonissuer audits, KAMs are NOT included).
- **Responsibilities of management for the F/S**.
- **Auditor's responsibilities for the audit of the F/S**.
- **Other reporting responsibilities** (when applicable).
- **Signature, city/state, date of report**.

## AU-C 705 — Modifications to the opinion

### Three types of modifications

1. **Qualified opinion** — F/S are materially misstated OR auditor
   was unable to obtain sufficient evidence; effect is material
   but not pervasive.
2. **Adverse opinion** — F/S are materially misstated; effect is
   both material AND pervasive.
3. **Disclaimer of opinion** — Auditor was unable to obtain
   sufficient evidence; potential effects are material and
   pervasive (and the auditor concludes that the possible effects
   could be both material and pervasive). Or, in extremely rare
   cases, the auditor concludes that, notwithstanding sufficient
   appropriate evidence, multiple uncertainties make it impossible
   to express an opinion.

### Decision matrix

|  | Misstatement | Inability to obtain evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Material but not pervasive | Qualified | Qualified |
| Material AND pervasive | Adverse | Disclaimer |

### Modified report structure
- "Opinion" heading is replaced with "Qualified Opinion",
  "Adverse Opinion", or "Disclaimer of Opinion."
- Basis section explains the matter.
- For qualified / adverse: F/S effects quantified where practicable.
- For disclaimer: scope-limitation explanation.

## AU-C 706 — Emphasis-of-matter and other-matter paragraphs

### Emphasis-of-matter (EOM)
- A matter appropriately presented or disclosed in the F/S that, in
  the auditor's professional judgment, is of such importance that
  it is fundamental to users' understanding.
- Examples:
  - Going-concern uncertainty (per AU-C 570) when the F/S adequately
    disclose.
  - Accounting principle change (per AU-C 708 consistency).
  - Restatement.
  - Subsequent event of unusual nature.
  - Unusual related-party transactions.
- Placement: immediately after the basis for opinion, with a
  heading "Emphasis of Matter" or topic-specific heading.

### Other-matter (OM)
- A matter not presented or disclosed in the F/S that, in the
  auditor's professional judgment, is relevant to users'
  understanding of the audit, the auditor's responsibilities, or
  the auditor's report.
- Examples:
  - Restriction on the use of the auditor's report.
  - Auditor's responsibilities under another set of standards (e.g.,
    when a U.S. audit also addresses other-jurisdiction reporting).

## AU-C 701 — Communicating Key Audit Matters (KAMs)

For nonissuer audits, KAMs are communicated only when terms of
engagement require it. KAMs are:
- Matters that, in the auditor's professional judgment, were of
  most significance in the audit.
- Selected from matters communicated to those charged with
  governance.
- Disclosed in the audit report in a separate "Key Audit Matters"
  section.
- For each KAM:
  - Why the matter was considered to be significant.
  - How the matter was addressed in the audit.

KAMs are NOT a substitute for a modified opinion. A KAM does not
replace required disclosure or accounting in the F/S.

## AU-C 708 — Consistency

If a change in accounting principle is material:
- Confirm the change is appropriate under the framework (ASC 250).
- Add an emphasis-of-matter paragraph noting the change and the
  effect.
- Disclose the change in the F/S as required by the framework.

## Special-purpose framework reporting (AU-C 800)

For audits of special-purpose F/S (cash basis, tax basis,
regulatory basis, contractual basis):
- Modify the audit report to address the framework.
- Restrict the use of the report (regulatory basis, contractual
  basis) where appropriate.
- Disclose the basis of accounting in the F/S.
