# Individual planning checklist categories

For tax year 2024+ — verify year-specific thresholds via the
applicable Rev. Proc. before quoting figures.

## 1. Retirement contributions

- **Traditional / Roth IRA** (§§408 / 408A): annual limit
  $7,000 ($8,000 if 50+) for 2024 — verify year-specific. Income
  phase-outs vary by filing status and pension-plan participation.
- **401(k) elective deferrals** (§402(g)): $23,000 2024
  ($30,500 with 50+ catch-up). Higher catch-up at age 60-63 per
  SECURE 2.0 starting 2025.
- **SEP-IRA** (§408(k)): 25% of net SE earnings, max $69,000
  (2024). Practitioner-friendly with extension deadline filing.
- **Solo 401(k)**: combined employee deferral + employer SEP-style
  contribution; up to $69,000 (2024) plus catch-up.
- **Defined-benefit plan** (§412 / §401(a)): potentially much
  higher annual contribution; complex actuarial computation.
- **Deadline analysis**: IRA contributions due by 4/15 of
  following year (no extension); 401(k) employer contributions by
  return due date (incl. extensions).

## 2. Roth conversions

- §408A(d)(3) — convert Traditional IRA to Roth IRA; pay tax now
  for tax-free growth and withdrawals later.
- Pro-rata rule (§408(d)(2)): if any pre-tax IRA exists,
  conversion is partially taxable.
- Strategy: convert in low-income years; ladder across years to
  manage marginal rates.
- IRMAA cliffs: conversions add to AGI which feeds Medicare
  Part B/D premium tiers (§1839(i)).
- Five-year holding rule (§408A(d)(2)(B)): each conversion has
  its own 5-year clock for early-withdrawal-penalty avoidance.
- Recharacterization eliminated post-TCJA — conversions are
  irrevocable.

## 3. Tax-loss harvesting

- §1091 wash-sale rule: disallowed loss + basis adjustment if
  substantially-identical security purchased within 30 days
  before / after.
- Apply to taxable accounts only (IRAs / 401(k) excluded).
- §1211(b) net capital loss limit: $3,000 / year against
  ordinary income; carryforward.
- Coordinate with charitable-stock-donation strategy (§170(e)).

## 4. Charitable contributions

- **Bunching**: aggregate multiple years into one to clear
  standard deduction.
- **Donor-Advised Fund** (DAF): immediate deduction with later
  granting flexibility. §170 substantiation rules apply.
- **QCD** (§408(d)(8)): up to $105,000 (2024 — verify year-
  specific) from IRA at age 70½+; counts toward RMD without AGI
  inclusion.
- **Appreciated stock donation** (§170(e)): FMV deduction with
  no capital-gain recognition; capped at 30% of AGI for public
  charity.
- **§170(f)(8) substantiation**: contemporaneous written
  acknowledgment for ≥ $250.
- **§170(f)(11) appraisal**: required for non-cash > $5,000
  (§170(f)(11)(C) qualified appraisal).
- **§170(b) AGI-percentage limits**: 60% cash to public charity;
  30% appreciated property; carryforward 5 years.

## 5. HSA / FSA optimization

- **HSA** (§223): triple-tax-free if HDHP-eligible. 2024 limits
  $4,150 self / $8,300 family + $1,000 catch-up at 55+. Verify
  year-specific.
- **FSA** (§125): $3,200 health FSA (2024); $5,000 dependent care.
- **Strategy**: max HSA contributions; treat HSA as retirement
  vehicle; pay current medical out-of-pocket and reimburse later.

## 6. AMT triggers

- §55 / §56 AMT preferences: large §164 SALT deduction (post-TCJA
  $10K cap mostly defangs this), ISO bargain element (§56(b)(3)),
  accelerated depreciation, percentage-depletion.
- AMT exemption 2024: $85,700 single / $133,300 MFJ; phaseout
  begins ~ $609,350 single / $1,218,700 MFJ. Verify year-specific.
- ISO planning: cashless exercise + same-year sale to convert
  ISO bargain element to non-AMT gain.

## 7. NIIT planning

- §1411 — 3.8% NIIT on net investment income.
- Threshold: $200K single / $250K MFJ.
- Material-participation activities can convert investment income
  to non-investment (route to `predict-material-participation`).
- §469 grouping election can convert passive to nonpassive
  rental income for §1411 purposes.

## 8. IRMAA cliffs (Medicare Part B/D premiums)

- §1839(i) — Medicare premium tiers based on AGI 2 years prior.
- 2024 tier thresholds: $103K single / $206K MFJ for first
  surcharge; cliffs at higher levels.
- AGI strategies: defer IRA conversion, defer capital gains,
  HSA / 401(k) deferrals.
- Form SSA-44 — appeal IRMAA based on life-event.

## 9. Withholding and §6654 safe harbors

- §6654(d)(1)(B) safe harbors:
  - 90% of current-year tax; OR
  - 100% of prior-year tax (110% if prior AGI > $150K).
- W-4 adjustments mid-year; W-4P for pensions; W-4R for
  retirement distributions.
- Strategy: end-of-year withholding from RMD or year-end bonus is
  treated as paid evenly per §6654(g).

## 10. §121 home-sale exclusion timing

- §121(a) — exclude up to $250K single / $500K MFJ on sale of
  principal residence.
- §121(b)(3) — ownership AND use of property as principal
  residence for 2 of 5 years preceding sale.
- §121(c) reduced exclusion for change in employment, health, or
  unforeseen circumstances.
- Coordinate with §121 / §1031 hybrid for mixed-use property.

## 11. §1031 like-kind exchange

Route to `predict-1031-qualification` for substantive analysis.
For planning checklist purposes:
- Real-property only (post-TCJA).
- 45-day identification / 180-day completion.
- Qualified Intermediary safe harbor.

## 12. §529 / education planning

- §529 plans: state-tax deduction often available; federal tax-
  free growth.
- §529 to Roth IRA rollover (SECURE 2.0, beginning 2024): up to
  $35,000 lifetime, conditioned on 15-year account age and
  contributions older than 5 years.
- §25A American Opportunity / Lifetime Learning credits.
- Coordinate with §117(b)(2) qualified scholarship.

## 13. Stock-option / RSU / §83(b)

- ISO (§422): no-AMT-friendly cashless-and-sell same-year.
- NSO (§83): ordinary income on exercise; capital gains on sale
  thereafter.
- §83(b) election: 30 days from grant; capture income on grant
  rather than vesting.
- RSU vesting: ordinary income at vesting; basis = ordinary income
  recognized.

## 14. Backdoor / mega backdoor Roth

- **Backdoor Roth**: non-deductible Traditional IRA contribution +
  immediate conversion to Roth. Watch §408(d)(2) pro-rata rule.
- **Mega backdoor**: after-tax 401(k) contribution + in-plan Roth
  conversion or in-service distribution to Roth IRA. Available
  only if plan permits.

## 15. State-residency planning

Route to `tax-research-state-income` for substantive analysis.
For planning checklist:
- 183-day test variations by state.
- Domicile vs. residency distinctions.
- NY convenience-of-employer rule traps for remote workers.
- CA "safe harbor" 18-month rule for employment-based absence.
- Capital-gains-only states (WA cap gains).

## 16. PTET election

Route to `tax-research-state-income` for state-by-state PTET
analysis. Common attributes:
- Election operates at the entity level (S-corp, partnership).
- Deduction at the federal level via §164 / Notice 2020-75.
- Credit at owner level on state return.
- Deadlines vary widely (some states require pre-payment by an
  early-year deadline).

## 17. Estate / gift annual exclusion

Route to `tax-research-estate-gift` for substantive analysis. For
planning checklist:
- §2503(b) annual exclusion: $18,000 (2024); $17,000 (2023).
  Verify year-specific.
- §2522 unlimited spousal annual exclusion.
- §529 5-year-front-load opportunity (§529(c)(2)(B)).
- §2522 / §2055 charitable deductions.

## Disclosure flags (per SSTS / Circular 230)

For any aggressive strategy, the practitioner must:
- Document the substantive position.
- Determine if Form 8275 / 8275-R / 8886 disclosure is required.
- Note the SSTS §1.1 realistic-possibility and SSTS §2.3 use-of-
  estimates checklist items.
- Note Circular 230 §10.37 written-advice standards (reasonable
  factual and legal assumptions, all relevant facts, no audit-
  lottery).

## Dirty-Dozen / Listed Transaction warning

The following strategies are flagged by the IRS as Dirty Dozen
/ Listed / Reportable Transactions and must NOT appear in the
recommendation list without an explicit warning banner and
Form 8886 disclosure analysis:

- §831(b) micro-captives (Notice 2016-66; Avrahami; Reserve
  Mechanical).
- Syndicated conservation easements (§170(h); Notice 2017-10).
- Monetized installment sales.
- Charitable LLC structures.
- Pre-packaged "tax shelters" of any kind.

Route to `predict-economic-substance` for any §7701(o) analysis
required.
