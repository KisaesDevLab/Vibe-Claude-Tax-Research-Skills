# Economic-substance doctrine — judicial history

## Pre-codification foundations

### Frank Lyon Co. v. United States, 435 U.S. 561 (1978)

Sale-leaseback of a Worthen Bank office building. Supreme Court
recognized the lease as a genuine multi-party transaction with
economic substance even though it produced tax benefits to the
taxpayer. Sets the foundational test: a transaction is genuine
when "compelled or encouraged by business or regulatory realities,
is imbued with tax-independent considerations, and is not shaped
solely by tax-avoidance features."

### ACM Partnership v. Commissioner, 157 F.3d 231 (3d Cir. 1998)

Contingent-installment-sale transaction generating capital losses.
3d Cir. applied the conjunctive test and held the transaction
lacked both objective and subjective economic substance. Pivotal
case for the conjunctive approach later codified.

### Compaq Computer Corp. v. Commissioner, 277 F.3d 778 (5th Cir. 2001)

ADR / dividend-stripping transaction. 5th Cir. reversed Tax
Court's economic-substance disallowance, holding that pre-tax
foreign withholding tax did not strip the transaction of objective
substance because Compaq had real risk and a profit. Compaq is the
high-water mark for the disjunctive approach pre-codification.

### Coltec Indus. v. United States, 454 F.3d 1340 (Fed. Cir. 2006)

Asbestos-liability indemnity transaction designed to generate
capital losses. Fed. Cir. held the transaction lacked economic
substance under the objective prong despite a non-tax business
purpose. Major precedent for the conjunctive-and-quantitative
approach.

### Andantech LLC v. Commissioner, 331 F.3d 972 (D.C. Cir. 2003)

Sale-leaseback "SILO" transaction; D.C. Cir. affirmed economic-
substance disallowance.

## Codification — Health Care and Education Reconciliation Act of 2010

§1409 of HCERA (Pub. L. 111-152, March 30, 2010) added IRC
§7701(o), §6662(b)(6), §6662(i), and amended §6664(c)(2). Effective
for transactions entered into after March 30, 2010.

§7701(o)(1) codifies the CONJUNCTIVE test:
"In the case of any transaction to which the economic substance
doctrine is relevant, such transaction shall be treated as having
economic substance only if —
(A) the transaction changes in a meaningful way (apart from
    Federal income tax effects) the taxpayer's economic position,
    AND
(B) the taxpayer has a substantial purpose (apart from Federal
    income tax effects) for entering into such transaction."

§7701(o)(2)(A) — potential for profit:
"In the case of any transaction to which subsection (o)(1) applies,
the potential for profit of a transaction shall be taken into
account in determining whether the requirements of subsections
(o)(1)(A) and (B) are met with respect to the transaction only if
the present value of the reasonably expected pre-tax profit from
the transaction is substantial in relation to the present value of
the expected net tax benefits that would be allowed if the
transaction were respected."

§7701(o)(2)(B) — fees and other expenses considered as expenses:
"Fees and other transaction expenses shall be taken into account
as expenses in determining pre-tax profit."

§7701(o)(3) — state and local tax benefits NOT taken into account.

§7701(o)(4) — financial accounting benefits NOT taken into
account.

§7701(o)(5)(A) — definition of "economic substance doctrine".
§7701(o)(5)(B) — exception for individuals: applies only to
transactions entered into in connection with a trade or business
or activity engaged in for the production of income.
§7701(o)(5)(C) — relevance: doctrine applies "in the same manner
as if this subsection had never been enacted".

## Penalty regime

### §6662(b)(6)
20% accuracy penalty on the portion of an underpayment
attributable to disallowance of claimed tax benefits by reason of
a transaction lacking economic substance (within the meaning of
§7701(o)) or failing to meet the requirements of any similar rule.

### §6662(i) — 40% non-disclosed transactions
40% penalty applied if the transaction lacking economic substance
was NOT adequately disclosed on a Form 8275, 8275-R, or 8886 (for
reportable transactions).

### §6664(c)(2) — reasonable-cause unavailable for §6662(i)
"In the case of any portion of an underpayment which is
attributable to one or more transactions described in section
6662(b)(6), paragraph (1) [the reasonable-cause exception]
shall not apply." Strict liability.

## Post-codification application

### Bank of New York Mellon Corp. v. Commissioner, 801 F.3d 104 (2d Cir. 2015)

STARS-transaction (Structured Trust Advantaged Repackaged
Securities). 2d Cir. affirmed Tax Court disallowance under the
common-law economic-substance doctrine. Demonstrates that pre-
2010 transactions in litigation are still tested under common law,
not §7701(o).

### IES Industries, Inc. v. Commissioner / AIG-related litigation

Multiple cases addressed cross-border STARS, ADR-stripping, and
foreign-tax-credit-generator transactions. Government generally
prevailed; courts focused on the objective prong (no meaningful
pre-tax profit relative to tax benefit).

## Notice 2014-58 — IRS guidance

Issued November 3, 2014. Key clarifications:
- "Transaction" defined: a series of interconnected steps that
  together form an integrated whole are treated as one transaction;
  steps that "could be undertaken separately" may be analyzed
  separately.
- "Similar rule of law": the §6662(b)(6) penalty applies to
  doctrines such as the substance-over-form, step-transaction,
  and sham-transaction doctrines.
- The doctrine is "relevant" when the facts and circumstances are
  similar to pre-codification case-law fact patterns.

## LB&I Directives

- Director's approval required for any field-level §7701(o)
  assertion.
- Practitioners can rely on the procedural protection: if the IRS
  exam team raises §7701(o) without LB&I sign-off, the practitioner
  has a procedural defense (record the timeline).

## Practical practitioner takeaways

- Document the non-tax business purpose contemporaneously.
- Quantify pre-tax profit relative to net tax benefits in
  contemporaneous projections.
- Avoid structures that mirror ACM Partnership, Coltec, or STARS
  fact patterns.
- File Form 8275 / 8275-R / 8886 as appropriate to keep §6662(i)
  at the 20% level rather than 40%.
- Maintain the contemporaneous file even for "comfortable"
  transactions; ESD risk is fact-driven and the §6662(i) strict-
  liability regime is unforgiving.
