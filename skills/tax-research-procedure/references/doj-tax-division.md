# DOJ Tax Division — civil and criminal practice

## Source

DOJ Tax Division: https://www.justice.gov/tax

## Civil Tax Division

The Tax Division represents the United States in civil tax
litigation OUTSIDE the Tax Court. Tax Court is the IRS Office of
Chief Counsel's exclusive litigation forum.

### Refund litigation

§7422 — refund suit in District Court or Court of Federal Claims.
Prerequisites:
- Full payment of assessed tax (Flora v. United States, 362 U.S.
  145 (1960)).
- Properly filed administrative claim (§6511).
- Statutory notice of disallowance OR 6 months of IRS inaction.

§7422(a) — administrative claim must specify grounds.

§7422(g) — partnership-level refund procedures.

### Collection-related civil suits

§7401 — collection suit (Code authorizes the United States to sue
to collect taxes).
§7403 — quiet-title action to subject property to lien.
§7402 — injunction.
§7405 — recovery of erroneous refunds.

### Bankruptcy

11 U.S.C. §§ 505 / 507 / 523 / 1129 — tax priority and dischargeability.
§ 6871 — termination of taxable year for estates in bankruptcy.

DOJ Tax represents the U.S. in bankruptcy court for substantive
tax matters; the U.S. Trustee handles administrative bankruptcy
matters.

## Criminal Tax Division

The Tax Division authorizes most federal tax-fraud prosecutions
and collaborates with U.S. Attorney's Offices.

### Common criminal-tax statutes

| Statute | Offense | Maximum |
|---|---|---|
| §7201 | Tax evasion | 5 years + $100K (fine) |
| §7202 | Willful failure to collect / pay over | 5 years + $10K |
| §7203 | Willful failure to file / pay | 1 year + $25K (misdemeanor) |
| §7204 | Fraudulent W-2 / W-4 | 1 year + $1K |
| §7205 | Withholding-statement fraud | 1 year + $1K |
| §7206(1) | Fraud and false statements (jurat) | 3 years + $100K |
| §7206(2) | Aiding and assisting | 3 years + $100K |
| §7212 | Corruptly endeavoring to obstruct | 3 years + $5K |
| 18 U.S.C. §371 | Conspiracy to defraud the U.S. | 5 years + $250K |
| 18 U.S.C. §1956 / §1957 | Money laundering | up to 20 years |
| 18 U.S.C. §1001 | False statements | 5 years |

### Klein conspiracy

18 U.S.C. §371 (Klein conspiracy: defraud-clause) — agreement to
impede the lawful function of the United States. Frequently
charged alongside §7201 / §7206.

### Spies evasion

§7201 includes affirmative-acts test (Spies v. United States, 317
U.S. 492 (1943)): conviction requires more than mere failure to
file; must include affirmative acts of concealment.

### IRS Criminal Investigation (CI)

CI special agents conduct criminal-tax investigations under §7602
summons authority + §7605 restrictions.

CI referrals go through:
1. Special-agent investigation.
2. Department of Justice (DOJ Tax Division) review.
3. Grand jury (if DOJ pursues).
4. Indictment / plea / trial.

### Voluntary Disclosure Practice

Voluntary Disclosure Practice (announced periodically; current
guidance is from IRM 9.5.11 and CI website):
- Pre-emptive disclosure of unreported income.
- Bars criminal prosecution for the disclosed conduct.
- Civil-tax-and-penalty exposure remains.
- Various offshore programs (OVDP) ended; current procedure is
  the Updated Voluntary Disclosure Practice (UVDP) announced
  Nov. 2018.

Practitioners must engage criminal-tax counsel at the first
indication of a CI investigation. CI special agents typically
make unannounced taxpayer contact; the taxpayer should decline
to make statements until counsel is consulted.

## Tax-shelter litigation

Major tax-shelter-promoter and listed-transaction litigation has
been handled by the Tax Division:
- §6700 promoter penalty.
- §6701 aiding-and-abetting penalty.
- §6707A reportable-transaction penalty.
- §7408 injunction against tax-shelter promoters.

## Practitioner overlay

For practitioners advising clients during a DOJ Tax Division
inquiry:

1. **Assess criminal exposure first**: §7201 / §7206 / §7202
   exposure governs all subsequent advice.
2. **Engage criminal-tax counsel**: tax-controversy practitioners
   are often not criminal practitioners; co-counsel is the norm.
3. **Privilege**: §7525 federally-authorized practitioner
   privilege does NOT apply in criminal cases. Attorney-client
   privilege (and Kovel arrangements for accountant participation)
   is the only effective protection.
4. **Document preservation**: §7212 obstruction concerns mean
   document destruction during investigation is itself a crime.
5. **Cooperation**: cooperation can earn USSG §3E1.1 acceptance-
   of-responsibility and §5K1.1 substantial-assistance
   adjustments.

## Privilege overlay

§7525 — federally-authorized practitioner privilege applies in
non-criminal proceedings between a taxpayer and a tax practitioner.
Limited to TAX ADVICE; does NOT cover tax-return preparation.
Does NOT apply in criminal proceedings.

§7525(b) — exception for written tax-shelter communications.

For criminal cases, attorney-client privilege via Kovel
arrangement is the practical protection: a non-attorney accountant
working under counsel's direction is covered by the attorney's
privilege.

## Practitioner audit-defense file (DOJ-related)

- [ ] Identify civil vs. criminal posture early.
- [ ] §7525 privilege analysis for civil; attorney-client / Kovel
  for criminal.
- [ ] Engage criminal-tax counsel at first sign of CI activity.
- [ ] Document-preservation hold issued.
- [ ] Voluntary disclosure timeliness preserved if relevant.
- [ ] Klein conspiracy / Spies evasion exposure assessed.
- [ ] §6531 SOL (criminal: 3-6 years depending on offense).
- [ ] Cooperation / acceptance-of-responsibility decision documented.
