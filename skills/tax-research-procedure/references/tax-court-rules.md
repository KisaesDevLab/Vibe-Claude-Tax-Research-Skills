# U.S. Tax Court — rules and procedure

## Source

Tax Court Rules of Practice and Procedure:
https://www.ustaxcourt.gov/rules.html

DAWSON e-filing system:
https://dawson.ustaxcourt.gov

## Jurisdiction (§7442)

Tax Court has jurisdiction over deficiencies in:
- Income tax.
- Estate tax.
- Gift tax.
- Generation-skipping transfer tax.
- Specified excise taxes.

Plus collateral jurisdiction over:
- §6015 innocent-spouse determinations.
- §6320 / §6330 CDP determinations.
- §6213(b)(1) jeopardy assessments.
- Whistleblower §7623 awards.
- §7436 worker-classification determinations.

Tax Court does NOT have refund jurisdiction. Refund cases go to
District Court or Court of Federal Claims after full payment of
the assessed tax (Flora full-payment rule).

## Trial procedure

### Petition (Rule 34)

Filed within 90 days of statutory notice of deficiency (150 days
if mailed outside U.S.).

Petition contents:
- Caption with taxpayer name and address.
- Assignment of errors.
- Statement of facts.
- Requested relief.

Filing fee: $60 (Rule 20, verify current amount).

### Answer (Rule 36)

Commissioner files answer within 60 days. May raise affirmative
defenses, particularly the burden-of-proof shift under §7491.

### Discovery (Rule 70 series)

- Branerton conference (informal exchange) before formal discovery.
- Interrogatories (Rule 71).
- Production requests (Rule 72).
- Stipulations (Rule 91) — Tax Court strongly favors stipulations.

### Trial (Rule 130 series)

- Trial calendars in major cities.
- Bench-trial format; no jury.
- Burden of proof generally on taxpayer (§7491(a) shift to IRS
  if taxpayer maintains records and cooperates).
- §7491(b) shift on penalties: IRS bears initial burden of
  production.

### Briefing (Rule 151)

Post-trial briefs typically required; opening + answering +
reply.

### Decision (§7459 / Rule 155)

Decision becomes final 90 days after entry unless timely appealed.

Rule 155 — computation of decision: parties submit competing
computations after the opinion if the opinion is principle-based.

## Small tax case (S-case) procedure (§7463 / Rule 170)

Available for amounts ≤ $50,000 per year. Streamlined procedure:
- Less formal pleading.
- Limited discovery.
- Decision NOT appealable.

Election made on petition or by motion before trial.

## Settlement and §7430 costs

Most cases settle through Appeals or via stipulated decision.
Form 870 variants close cases administratively.

§7430 — recovery of administrative and litigation costs:
- Net-worth ≤ $2M individual; $7M organization.
- IRS position not substantially justified.
- Taxpayer substantially prevailed.
- Application by motion within 30 days of judgment.

## Appeals to Court of Appeals

§7482(a) — Court of Appeals has jurisdiction over Tax Court
decisions. Appeal lies in the circuit where:
- Individual: residence at time of petition.
- Corporation: principal place of business.

§7482(b) — venue election can be made by stipulation.

Tax Court Memorandum opinions — published but not formally
binding on the Tax Court. Persuasive within Tax Court; usable as
persuasive authority outside Tax Court.

Tax Court Regular opinions — fully reviewed by the full court
(Rule 145).

## Golsen rule

Tax Court applies the law of the Court of Appeals to which the
taxpayer's case is appealable. Identify the taxpayer's circuit
to predict Tax Court decision-making on circuit-split issues.

## Common practitioner errors

- Missing 90-day petition deadline.
- Filing in District Court / Court of Federal Claims when refund
  jurisdiction was the appropriate path (or vice versa).
- Failing to elect S-case treatment when eligible and appropriate.
- Failing to identify Golsen-applicable circuit.
- Treating Tax Court Memorandum as binding precedent.
- Missing §7430 fee-recovery 30-day deadline post-judgment.

## Procedural checklist

- [ ] Stat-notice received; 90-day deadline calendared.
- [ ] Petition drafted with assignment of errors.
- [ ] Filing fee paid; receipt retained.
- [ ] Branerton conference scheduled.
- [ ] Stipulations identified.
- [ ] §7491 burden-of-proof analysis documented.
- [ ] Settlement / Appeals path evaluated before trial.
- [ ] Post-trial briefs filed timely.
- [ ] Rule 155 computation submitted if applicable.
- [ ] §7430 cost-recovery motion filed within 30 days.
- [ ] §7482 appeal evaluated within 90 days post-decision.
