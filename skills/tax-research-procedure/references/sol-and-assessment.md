# Statutes of limitations — assessment, collection, refund

## §6501 — Period of limitations on assessment

### General rule (§6501(a))

3 years from the LATER of:
- The date the return was filed; OR
- The due date of the return (without extensions).

§6501(b)(1) — early-filed returns deemed filed on the due date.

§6501(b)(2) — paid-after-due-date returns: SOL runs from filing
date.

### Substantial-omission exception (§6501(e))

6-year SOL if return omits gross income > 25% of stated gross
income. Reaches "substantial-omission" of basis-adjustment items
post-2015 (Surface Transportation Act Pub. L. 114-41 amendment).

Home Concrete & Supply, LLC v. United States, 566 U.S. 478 (2012),
held basis-overstatements were NOT § 6501(e) substantial-omission
events; Congress legislatively reversed for tax years after
7/31/2015.

### No-SOL exceptions (§6501(c))

(1) False or fraudulent return with intent to evade.
(2) Willful attempt to evade.
(3) No return filed.

### Listed-transaction extension (§6501(c)(10))

1-year extension after disclosure for listed-transaction-related
items.

### Foreign-information-return extension (§6501(c)(8))

3-year SOL extends until 3 years after foreign-information return
(Form 5471, 8865, 8938, etc.) is filed. Relevant for understated
income items.

### Gift / GST / Estate (§6501(c)(9))

Gift-tax SOL on adequate disclosure: §6501(c)(9) and §6501(e)(2)
require adequate disclosure on Form 709 to start the SOL.

## §6502 — Collection statute

10 years from assessment date for collection of assessed liability.

§6502 tolls during:
- Bankruptcy + 6 months post-discharge.
- Pending OIC + 30 days.
- Pending installment-agreement request + 30 days.
- CDP hearing + appeals (§6330(e)).
- §6503 various exceptions.
- Taxpayer outside U.S. for 6 consecutive months.

Collection Statute Expiration Date (CSED) tracking is critical for
practitioners; an expired CSED bars enforced collection but
voluntary payment is not refundable.

## §6511 — Refund claim deadline

§6511(a) — claim filed within LATER of:
- 3 years from filing of return; OR
- 2 years from payment of tax.

§6511(b)(2) — look-back period: refund limited to amount paid
within applicable look-back (3-year or 2-year window).

§6511(d)(1) — bad-debt and worthless-securities claims: 7 years.

§6511(g) — joint return SOL: applies to the joint return.

## §6411 — Tentative refund (carryback)

NOL carryback (§172) and other carryback claims may be filed on
Form 1045 (individuals) or Form 1139 (corporations) within 12
months of the loss-year close.

§6411(a) review is non-judgmental — IRS allows or disallows within
90 days; not a final determination. Taxpayer can later file Form
1040X / 1120X for full refund-claim treatment.

## §6213 — Tax Court petition deadline

§6213(a) — 90 days from statutory notice of deficiency (150 days
if notice mailed to address outside the U.S.).

JURISDICTIONAL deadline; missed petition = no Tax Court review.

§6213(b) — math-and-clerical-error deficiencies bypass §6213(a)
deficiency procedures.

## Mitigation provisions (§§1311–1314)

Allow correction of double-inclusion / double-exclusion errors
when normal SOL has expired. Limited applicability; technical
operation. Practitioners often miss these.

## Equitable tolling

Generally NOT available for §6501 / §6511 absent statutory hook.
Boechler, P.C. v. Commissioner, 142 S. Ct. 1493 (2022), allowed
equitable tolling for the §6330(d)(1) Tax Court CDP petition
deadline; analogous provisions evaluated on case-by-case basis.

## Common practitioner errors

- Computing 3-year SOL from due date instead of filing date for
  late-filed returns.
- Failing to track §6501(e) 6-year SOL when basis-overstatement
  items exist.
- Filing 1040X / 1120X amended returns past the §6511 deadline.
- Missing the 90-day petition window (treating it as 3 months
  instead of 90 days).
- Missing CDP 30-day window.
- Failing to file protective refund claim under §6511(a) when
  limitations period is about to expire.
