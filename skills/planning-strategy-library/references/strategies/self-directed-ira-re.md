# Self-Directed IRA Real Estate

**Slug:** `self-directed-ira-re`
**Primary IRC section:** IRC Sec. 408; Sec. 4975

## Summary

Self-directed IRA holds real estate as an asset; rental income / appreciation grows tax-deferred (or tax-free in Roth) within the IRA.

## Eligibility

- Self-directed IRA custodian.
- Real estate held in IRA's name.
- All expenses paid from IRA; all income flows to IRA.
- No prohibited transactions under Sec. 4975 (self-dealing, related-party rentals, personal use).

## Mechanics

1. Open self-directed IRA at custodian.
2. Fund IRA via contributions / rollovers.
3. Direct custodian to purchase real estate.
4. Manage carefully to avoid Sec. 4975 prohibited transactions.
5. UBIT (Sec. 511) consideration if leveraged real estate (Sec. 514 UDFI).

## Citations

- IRC Sec. 408 (IRAs).
- IRC Sec. 4975 (prohibited transactions).
- IRC Sec. 511 (UBIT).
- IRC Sec. 514 (debt-financed property).

## State interplay

State property tax may apply at the IRA-asset level.

## Recent legislation impact

No material recent-legislation impact identified at this draft.

## Warnings

- **WARNING:** High Sec. 4975 prohibited-transaction risk if not strictly managed; consequences include disqualification of entire IRA.
