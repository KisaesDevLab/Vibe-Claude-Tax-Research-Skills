# §41(d) four-part test, exclusions, QRE rules, ASC mechanics

## Four-part test (§41(d)(1))

Each business component must independently satisfy ALL four:

### 1. §174 expenditure

Activity must qualify as research and experimental expenditure
under §174. Treas. Reg. §1.174-2(a) defines R&E broadly: "all
such costs incident to the development or improvement of a
product." Excludes ordinary product testing, quality-control
testing, market research, advertising, etc.

Practical screen: would a competent tax professional categorize
this as §174 R&E if no §41 credit existed?

### 2. Technological in nature (§41(d)(1)(B)(i))

Activity must rely on principles of:
- Physical sciences (physics, chemistry).
- Biological sciences (biology, biotechnology).
- Engineering (mechanical, electrical, civil, chemical, etc.).
- Computer sciences.

Excludes social sciences (psychology, sociology, economics),
arts, and humanities.

For software: §1.41-4(a)(4) confirms software development can
satisfy this prong if the underlying activities involve
computer-science principles (algorithms, data structures, system
architecture). Routine programming or configuration is excluded.

### 3. Process of experimentation (§41(d)(1)(C))

"Substantially all" of the activities must constitute a process
of experimentation. §1.41-4(a)(5) defines a process of
experimentation as the systematic evaluation of one or more
alternatives to:
- Test a hypothesis;
- Evaluate the alternatives; and
- Choose among alternatives.

The "substantially all" standard at §1.41-4(a)(6) means at least
80% of activities relating to the business component constitute
elements of a process of experimentation.

Process-of-experimentation evidence:
- Hypothesis: stated objective and uncertainty.
- Test plan / methodology.
- Recorded test outcomes.
- Iteration / refinement based on outcomes.
- Final selection rationale.

### 4. Permitted purpose (§41(d)(1)(B)(ii))

Activity must relate to a NEW or IMPROVED business component for:
- Function;
- Performance;
- Reliability; or
- Quality.

Excludes purposes related to style, taste, cosmetic, or seasonal
design factors.

The "business component" is defined at §41(d)(2)(B) as any
product, process, computer software, technique, formula, or
invention which is held for sale, lease, or license OR used in
the taxpayer's trade or business. The four-part test is applied
SEPARATELY for each business component (§41(d)(2)(A)) — this is
the "shrinking-back rule".

## §41(d)(4) — Excluded activities

### (A) Research after commercial production

Once a product is in commercial production, further activity is
not qualified UNLESS it relates to a new business component or a
significant improvement.

### (B) Adaptation of existing business component

Customizing an existing product for a particular customer is not
qualified.

### (C) Duplication of existing business component

Reverse-engineering an existing product is not qualified.

### (D) Surveys, studies, market research

§1.41-4(c)(2) — efficiency surveys, management studies, consumer
surveys, market research, advertising, and routine data
collection do not qualify.

### (E) Computer software for internal use

§41(d)(4)(E) and §1.41-4(c)(6): software developed primarily for
internal use is excluded UNLESS three high-threshold-of-innovation
tests are met:
1. Innovativeness — software is intended to result in a reduction
   in cost / improvement in speed substantially exceeding
   incremental improvement.
2. Significant economic risk — taxpayer commits substantial
   resources without substantial certainty of recovery.
3. Commercial-availability — comparable software is not
   commercially available for purchase, lease, or license without
   modifications that would make it unsuitable.

§1.41-4(c)(6)(iv) carves out "dual-function" software.

### (F) Research outside the U.S.

§41(d)(4)(F): research conducted outside the U.S., Puerto Rico,
or any U.S. possession is excluded. STATUTORY exclusion — no
Treas. Reg. exception.

### (G) Social sciences, arts, humanities

Excluded by §41(d)(4)(G).

### (H) Funded research

§41(d)(4)(H) — research funded by another person via grant,
contract, or otherwise is excluded UNLESS the taxpayer:
- Retains substantial rights in the research; AND
- Bears financial risk (i.e., is paid only on success or upon
  completion meeting specifications).

A typical fixed-fee contract under which payment is contingent on
deliverable does NOT make the research "funded"; the contractor
bears risk and may retain rights. A cost-plus contract DOES make
the research funded. Document carefully.

## QRE categories (§41(b))

### In-house research expenses (§41(b)(2))

#### Wages

Wages for qualified services:
- ENGAGING in qualified research.
- Directly SUPERVISING qualified research.
- Directly SUPPORTING qualified research.

§1.41-2(c)(2) "substantially all" rule: if at least 80% of an
employee's services are qualified services, all of the wages are
treated as qualified.

Wages = Box 1 of W-2 (or self-employment net earnings for sole
proprietors). Box 12-D 401(k) deferrals INCLUDED in Box 1 for
this purpose under post-2008 IRS guidance (Suder).

#### Supplies

Tangible property used or consumed in the conduct of qualified
research, EXCLUDING land, depreciable property, and general-
administrative supplies. Prototypes count if they are used or
consumed in the experimentation; finished products do not.

#### Computer leasing

Payments to lease computers used in the conduct of qualified
research. Excludes purchase of computers (those are depreciable).

### Contract research expenses (§41(b)(3))

65% of payments to a non-employee for qualified research, IF the
taxpayer:
- Bears the financial risk; AND
- Retains substantial rights in the research.

75% for payments to a "qualified research consortium" (§41(b)(3)(C)
— a tax-exempt §501(c)(3) consortium of unrelated taxpayers and
universities for the purpose of research).

100% for "qualified energy research consortium" payments
(§41(b)(3)(D)).

Time-and-materials (T&M) and fixed-fee contracts generally
qualify; cost-plus contracts often disqualify because the
contractor bears no risk.

### Basic research payments (§41(b)(1)(B)(ii))

Payments to qualified universities and other qualified
organizations for basic research. 100% qualified at the basic-
research credit rate.

## Computation methods

### Regular method (§41(a)(1))

Credit = 20% × (current-year QREs − base amount)

Base amount = fixed-base percentage × average annual gross
receipts for the 4 prior years. Minimum: 50% of current-year QREs.

Fixed-base percentage:
- Pre-1985-Aug-1988 taxpayers: ratio of QREs to gross receipts
  for 1984-1988, capped at 16%.
- Start-up taxpayers (after 12/31/1983): 3% for first 5 years,
  graduated formula thereafter (§41(c)(3)(B)).

### Alternative Simplified Credit (§41(c)(4))

Credit = 14% × (current-year QREs − 50% × average QREs of prior
3 years)

If no QREs in any of the 3 prior years: ASC = 6% × current-year
QREs.

ASC election made annually on Form 6765. Once made, applies for
that year only; can switch year to year.

### §280C(c) reduced-credit election

Default: §174 deduction reduced by the §41 credit.
§280C(c)(2) election: take reduced credit (× (1 − corporate
rate)) instead. For 2024 with 21% corporate rate, reduced credit
= credit × 0.79.

Election made on Form 6765 with timely-filed (with extensions)
return. Cannot be made on an amended return.

## §174 capitalization (post-TCJA / OBBBA)

Tax years beginning after 12/31/2021 (TCJA §13206):
- Domestic R&E: 5-year amortization, mid-year convention.
- Foreign R&E: 15-year amortization.
- Software development included in §174 (§174(c)(3)).

Rev. Proc. 2023-11 provides automatic method-change procedures
under §446(e) for §174 implementation.

Notice 2024-14 provides additional Q&A on §174 application.

OBBBA (Pub. L. 119-XX, 2025) modified §174; verify per
shared/legislation-tracking.md the current effective amortization
or expensing posture.

## Documentation expectations (post-FAQ 25 / amended claims)

For amended-return refund claims (post-2022 FAQ 25), taxpayer
must include:
1. All business components for which the §41 credit is claimed.
2. All research activities performed by business component.
3. All individuals (with their names or unique identifiers) who
   performed each research activity by business component.
4. All information sought to be discovered by each research
   activity by business component.
5. Total qualified employee wage expenses, qualified supply
   expenses, and qualified contract research expenses for the
   claim year.

For original-return claims, the IRS expects but does not require
the same level of detail; contemporaneous business-component
documentation is the standard.

## Common audit-trigger patterns

- Aggregate-level four-part test instead of business-component-
  by-business-component.
- Software development without high-threshold-of-innovation tests
  for internal-use software.
- Foreign research mistakenly included.
- Cost-plus contract research treated as qualified.
- Wages for support staff with < 80% qualified services treated
  as fully qualified under "substantially all".
- Adaptation / duplication / market research disguised as
  qualified research.
- Reconstructive QRE estimates without contemporaneous time
  records.
