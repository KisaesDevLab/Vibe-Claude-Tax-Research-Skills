# ASC 606 — Variable Consideration and Constraint

ASC 606-10-32-5 through -32-13 govern the inclusion of variable
consideration (VC) in the transaction price.

## What is variable consideration

Examples:
- Discounts, rebates, refunds, credits.
- Volume rebates.
- Performance bonuses or penalties.
- Royalties (sales-based or usage-based).
- Returns rights.
- Concessions.
- Price changes that depend on future events.
- Service-level credits.

If any portion of consideration is variable (or contingent on a
future event), VC analysis applies.

## Estimation method (ASC 606-10-32-8)

Two methods:

### Expected-value method
- Sum of probability-weighted amounts.
- Appropriate when many similar contracts exist (homogeneous
  contracts).
- Example: a rebate program for many customers; variability is
  population-driven.

### Most-likely-amount method
- The single most likely amount in a range of possible outcomes.
- Appropriate when there are only two possible outcomes (e.g.,
  contingent bonus payable on milestone) or a discrete range of
  outcomes.

Use the method that better predicts the amount the entity will
be entitled to. Apply consistently.

## Constraint (ASC 606-10-32-11)

Include estimated VC in transaction price ONLY to the extent it
is **probable** that a significant reversal of cumulative
revenue recognized will NOT occur when the uncertainty is later
resolved.

Indicators that increase the likelihood of a significant reversal:
- Highly susceptible to factors outside the entity's influence
  (volatility in markets, third-party actions).
- Long uncertainty period before resolution.
- Limited experience with similar contracts.
- Broad range of possible amounts.
- Practice of offering price concessions.

Apply the constraint at contract inception AND reassess at each
reporting date.

If VC is constrained:
- Include only the constrained amount in transaction price.
- The portion above the constrained amount is recognized in
  later periods if/when the constraint is released.

## Sales-based or usage-based royalties on licenses (ASC 606-10-55-65)

For licenses of intellectual property (functional or symbolic),
sales-based or usage-based royalties are recognized at the LATER
of:
- The subsequent sale or usage occurs.
- The performance obligation to which the royalty has been
  allocated has been satisfied (in whole or in part).

This is a SPECIFIC EXCEPTION to the general VC framework — the
royalty's VC is NOT estimated upfront; it is recognized as
sales/usage occur. Avoids speculation about future sales/usage.

## Refund liabilities (ASC 606-10-32-10A)

For consideration received that the entity expects to refund:
- Recognize a refund liability for the amount received that the
  entity does not expect to be entitled to.
- Update the refund liability at each reporting date.
- For products with right of return: ASC 606-10-55-22+ (separate
  refund liability + return asset for inventory).

## Reassessment (ASC 606-10-32-14)

At each reporting date, update:
- Estimated transaction price (including VC).
- Allocations to performance obligations.
- Constraint application.

Cumulative catch-up adjustment recognized in the period of change
when the change relates to revenue already recognized.

## Common applications

### Volume rebate
- Customer earns rebate at year-end based on annual purchases.
- Estimate VC using expected-value (probability-weighted average
  rebate across customer's possible purchase volumes).
- Apply constraint: include only the amount probable to not
  reverse.
- Reduce transaction price (and thus revenue) for the
  estimated rebate.

### Performance bonus
- Entity earns bonus if it completes a project before a
  milestone date.
- Estimate VC using most-likely-amount method (binary outcome).
- Apply constraint: include in transaction price ONLY if
  probable that it will not reverse.

### Tiered pricing
- Different rates apply at different volume tiers.
- Estimate using expected-value.
- Apply constraint.

### Returns
- Estimate the portion of revenue likely to be returned.
- Recognize revenue net of expected returns.
- Recognize a refund liability for expected returns.
- Recognize a return asset (inventory) for the cost of expected
  returns.

## Reassessment example

- Q1: VC of $1M with 60% probability of full retention; 40%
  probability of $300K reversal. Constraint = $700K (the amount
  probable to not reverse). Recognize $700K of VC in Q1.
- Q2: New information increases probability of full retention to
  85%. Reassessed constraint = $850K (or possibly $1M if the
  remaining 15% reversal probability does not represent a
  "significant" reversal). Cumulative catch-up: recognize the
  difference.
- Q4: Final settlement at $950K. Cumulative VC = $950K
  (approximately).
