# ASC 842 — Lease Classification (Lessee)

ASC 842 supersedes ASC 840. For LESSEES, ALL leases other than
short-term and certain low-value-asset elections require
recognition of an ROU asset and a lease liability.
Classification (operating vs. finance) drives presentation but
not balance-sheet recognition.

## Lease definition (ASC 842-10-15-3)

A lease exists when:
- A contract conveys the right to control the use of identified
  property, plant, or equipment for a period of time.
- In exchange for consideration.

Right to control = right to direct AND right to obtain
substantially all the economic benefits.

If a contract is not a lease, evaluate under ASC 606 (revenue) or
other applicable ASC topics (service contracts, supply
agreements).

## Lessee classification — five tests (ASC 842-10-25-2)

A lease is a **finance lease** if any one of these is met
(otherwise operating):

### Test 1 — Transfer of ownership
The lease transfers ownership of the underlying asset to the
lessee by the end of the lease term.

### Test 2 — Bargain purchase option
The lease grants the lessee an option to purchase the asset that
the lessee is reasonably certain to exercise. "Reasonably
certain" is a high threshold (similar to "probable").

### Test 3 — Lease term ≥ 75% of remaining economic life
The lease term is for the major part of the remaining economic
life of the underlying asset. The 75% threshold is presumptively
"major" but is not a bright-line test under ASC 842 (in contrast
to ASC 840). Practitioners often retain the 75% threshold as
guidance.

### Test 4 — PV of lease payments ≥ 90% of fair value
The present value of the lease payments and any guaranteed
residual value equals or exceeds substantially all of the fair
value of the underlying asset. The 90% threshold is presumptively
"substantially all"; not a bright-line under ASC 842.

### Test 5 — Specialized asset
The underlying asset is so specialized that it is expected to
have no alternative use to the lessor at the end of the lease
term.

## Discount rate (ASC 842-20-30-3)

Lessee uses (in this order):
1. The rate implicit in the lease, if readily determinable.
2. If not readily determinable: the lessee's incremental borrowing
   rate (IBR) — the rate the lessee would have to pay to borrow
   on a collateralized basis over a similar term in a similar
   economic environment.
3. For private companies and certain not-for-profits: the
   risk-free rate may be elected (PCC alternative — ASC
   842-20-30-3 + ASU 2021-09).

## Lease payments (ASC 842-10-30-5)

Components:
- Fixed payments (less any lease incentives received or
  receivable).
- Variable lease payments that depend on an index or rate
  (initially measured at the rate at lease commencement).
- Exercise price of a purchase option that the lessee is
  reasonably certain to exercise.
- Payments for penalties for terminating the lease (if the
  termination is reasonably certain).
- Amounts probable of being owed under residual value guarantees.

EXCLUDED:
- Variable lease payments NOT based on an index or rate (e.g.,
  variable rent based on sales).
- Payments allocated to non-lease components (executory costs
  like maintenance, taxes, insurance — unless lessee elects to
  combine).

## Non-lease components (ASC 842-10-15-31)

A lessee may elect (by class of underlying asset) to NOT separate
non-lease components from lease components — combining them in
the lease accounting. If the election is NOT made:
- Allocate consideration based on relative standalone prices.
- Account for non-lease components under their applicable
  framework (typically expense as incurred for services).

## Short-term lease exception (ASC 842-20-25-2)

A lessee may elect not to apply ROU recognition for short-term
leases (≤ 12 months at commencement, with NO option to purchase
that the lessee is reasonably certain to exercise).

If elected (by class of underlying asset):
- Recognize lease payments straight-line over the lease term.
- No ROU asset; no lease liability on balance sheet.

## Operating lease vs. finance lease — presentation

### Operating lease (lessee)
- ROU asset and lease liability on balance sheet.
- INCOME STATEMENT: single straight-line lease expense.
- CASH FLOW: payments classified as operating activities.
- ROU asset amortization is the plug to achieve straight-line
  total expense.

### Finance lease (lessee)
- ROU asset and lease liability on balance sheet.
- INCOME STATEMENT: separate amortization of ROU asset
  (typically straight-line) AND interest on lease liability.
  Total expense FRONT-LOADED.
- CASH FLOW: principal portion = financing; interest portion =
  operating (or financing, per accounting policy).

## Disclosure (ASC 842-20-50)

- Maturity analysis of lease liabilities.
- Weighted-average remaining lease term (operating, finance).
- Weighted-average discount rate (operating, finance).
- Total operating lease cost.
- Total finance lease cost (separately interest, amortization).
- Variable lease cost.
- Short-term lease cost (if not elected to recognize ROU).
- Sublease income.
- Cash paid for leases.
- ROU assets obtained in exchange for new lease liabilities.

## Special situations

### Sale-leaseback (ASC 842-40)
- Determine whether the transfer of the asset qualifies as a sale
  under ASC 606 (control transfer to buyer-lessor).
- If sale: derecognize asset, recognize sale, then apply ASC 842
  for the leaseback.
- If not sale (failed sale-leaseback): financing transaction.

### Modifications (ASC 842-10-25-9)
- Increase in scope: if separate contract criteria met (additional
  ROU at standalone price), separate contract; otherwise
  modification.
- Decrease in scope: re-measure lease liability + ROU; recognize
  any gain/loss.
- Change in lease term: re-measure.

### Reassessment events (ASC 842-10-25-1)
- Change in lessee's assessment of options (purchase, renewal).
- Change in expected residual value guarantee.
- Resolution of uncertainty regarding right-to-direct.
