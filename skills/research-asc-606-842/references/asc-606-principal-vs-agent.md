# ASC 606 — Principal vs. Agent (control evaluation)

ASC 606-10-55-36 through -55-40 govern the principal/agent
determination. The conclusion drives whether revenue is reported
**gross** (principal) or **net** (agent).

## The control framework

The entity is a **principal** if it CONTROLS the specified
good/service before transferring it to the customer.

The entity is an **agent** if its performance obligation is to
arrange for another party to provide the good/service to the
customer (no control of the good/service before transfer).

Control of a specified good/service before transfer means:
- Ability to direct the use of, and obtain substantially all the
  remaining benefits from, the asset.
- Ability to prevent others from directing its use and obtaining
  benefits.

## Indicators of control (ASC 606-10-55-39)

The following indicate the entity is a PRINCIPAL:
- Primary responsibility for fulfilling the promise (NOT just
  the customer-facing role; includes responsibility for
  acceptability of the good/service).
- Inventory risk before the good is transferred (or before
  service is provided).
- Discretion in establishing prices (the entity sets the price,
  not the third party).

NOT determinative on its own:
- Form of consideration (gross commission vs. net commission).
- Customer-facing role (entity may be customer-facing as agent).
- Risk of credit loss (ASC 606-10-55-39A).

## Examples

### Reseller (principal)
- Entity buys product from supplier, takes title, holds inventory,
  resells to customers.
- Principal: revenue recognized at full sale price; cost recognized.

### Drop-ship reseller (judgment)
- Entity takes orders, supplier ships directly to customer; entity
  may or may not take title.
- Need to evaluate: does entity bear inventory risk? Does entity
  have discretion in pricing? Who is responsible for acceptability?
- Often a principal, but not always.

### Marketplace platform (typically agent)
- Entity hosts a platform connecting buyers and sellers; sellers
  set prices; entity doesn't take title or hold inventory; entity
  takes a commission.
- Agent: revenue recognized at commission only (NET).

### Concert promoter (judgment)
- Promoter sells tickets, contracts with venue and artist.
- If promoter takes risk on ticket sales (sets price, bears
  inventory risk if tickets unsold) → principal (gross).
- If promoter is just a ticketing intermediary (sets a fee,
  doesn't bear inventory risk) → agent (net).

### Travel booking (typically agent)
- Entity facilitates booking with airline / hotel; entity
  doesn't operate the airplane or hotel.
- Agent: revenue at commission/markup only.
- Some travel agencies act as principals on certain wholesale or
  packaged products.

### SaaS reseller (judgment)
- Reseller sells third-party SaaS product; vendor delivers and
  hosts; reseller takes a margin.
- Need to evaluate control before transfer:
  - Reseller doesn't host (vendor does), so reseller doesn't have
    direct possession before transfer.
  - But if reseller has discretion in pricing AND bears credit
    risk AND has primary obligation to customer → principal.
- Often agent, but case-specific.

## Specified good/service identification

Before applying the control test, identify the specified
good/service. For composite arrangements (e.g., reseller plus
implementation services), evaluate principal/agent FOR EACH
specified good/service separately.

A composite arrangement may have:
- Reseller of third-party product (potentially agent for product).
- Implementation services performed by the entity (likely principal
  for the implementation).

## Specific issues

### Shipping and handling

When customer receives the good and entity is principal,
shipping/handling are typically a fulfillment activity (not a
separate performance obligation). However, ASC 606-10-25-18A
permits an accounting policy election to treat shipping/handling
as a fulfillment activity (rather than a separate performance
obligation) for shipments after delivery to the customer (i.e.,
for incremental shipping).

### Delivery to a freight forwarder

For B2B sales where freight forwarder takes possession before
final customer:
- Determine when control transfers per ASC 606-10-25-30.
- If control transfers to customer at freight forwarder pickup,
  recognize revenue then.
- If control transfers at customer's dock, recognize at customer
  receipt.

### Concession on consideration

If the entity grants a concession (price reduction) post-sale:
- Evaluate whether the concession is variable consideration
  (estimate at sale) or a contract modification.
- Concessions tied to a contractual right are variable
  consideration; concessions not tied to a contractual right may
  be modifications.

## Disclosure

ASC 606-10-50: significant judgments include the principal/agent
determination if the conclusion is non-obvious. Disclose:
- The judgment made.
- The basis for the conclusion.
- The methods used to identify control.
