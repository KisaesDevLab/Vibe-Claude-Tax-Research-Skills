# Review-engagement workpaper variant (AR-C §90)

A SSARS review provides **limited assurance** through inquiry +
analytical procedures (the IAP framework). The review variant
builds on the standard scaffold (PBC list + tickmark legend +
lead sheets + indexing) but the procedure set is materially
narrower than an audit. Documentation must support the limited-
assurance conclusion under AR-C §60.A24–.A29.

## Required planning workpapers

### 110 — Engagement letter
Per AR-C §60.A21 + §90.10. Signed before fieldwork begins.

### 120 — Independence assessment
Per AICPA Code §1.200 — **independence is required** for a SSARS
review (no lack-of-independence path; that path is available only
for AR-C §80 compilation). Document threats considered, safeguards
applied, and conclusion that independence is at an acceptable
level.

### 130 — Engagement-acceptance / continuance memo
Per AR-C §60. Documents the firm's acceptance of the engagement,
the firm's competence to perform the review, the relevant ethical
requirements, and any going-concern indicators identified during
acceptance.

### 140 — Materiality determination
Per AR-C §90.18.A29. Even though a review provides limited
assurance, materiality determination is still required. Typically
applied at the F/S level (and to particular F/S items where
lower materiality applies).

### 150 — Review-procedure plan
Per AR-C §90.18. Documents:
- Inquiries planned (of management, governance, and others within
  the entity who can provide relevant information).
- Analytical procedures planned (with expectation development
  and threshold for follow-up).
- Knowledge of the entity, industry, and accounting principles.

## Required substantive workpapers

For every material F/S caption:

### Inquiries (the "I" in "IAP")
Per AR-C §90.A30–.A55. Documented inquiries of:
- Management and personnel involved in F/S preparation.
- Topics: significant accounting principles applied; significant
  estimates; subsequent events; significant transactions or
  arrangements; fraud awareness; going-concern indicators; legal
  matters; non-compliance with laws and regulations.

Each inquiry: who was asked, what was asked, what the response
was, when, and the practitioner's evaluation of the response.

### Analytical procedures (the "AP" in "IAP")
Per AR-C §90.A56–.A78. For every material F/S item:
- Develop expectation independent of management.
- Compare actual to expectation.
- Investigate significant differences (typically variance > 10%
  AND significant in dollar terms; firm methodology governs the
  precise threshold).
- Document the expectation, comparison, follow-up, and conclusion.

Common review-engagement analytical procedures:
- Trend analysis: current vs. prior period(s); current vs.
  budget; current vs. industry data.
- Ratio analysis: gross margin; operating margin; current ratio;
  AR aging vs. revenue trend; AP aging vs. cost-of-revenue trend;
  inventory turns; debt-service-coverage; working capital trend.
- Reasonableness tests: e.g., interest expense reasonableness
  given debt schedule and average rates; depreciation
  reasonableness given fixed-asset schedule and useful lives;
  payroll reasonableness given headcount and average comp.

### Going-concern review procedures
Per AR-C §90.65. The practitioner is required to perform going-
concern procedures consistent with the financial-reporting
framework (GAAP: ASC 205-40; route to `research-financial-
reporting` for substantive ASC analysis). If substantial doubt
exists and is not adequately disclosed, modify the review report
(emphasis-of-matter or qualified conclusion).

### Subsequent-events review
Per AR-C §90.81. Inquire of management about subsequent events
through the date of the review report.

## Required wrap-up workpapers

### 900 — Subsequent-events review
Documented as above.

### 910 — Management representation letter
Per AR-C §90.A102. Required for review engagements. Dated as of
the date of the review report. Signed by management.

### 930 — Draft F/S
The F/S as prepared by management; the practitioner's role is
limited assurance, not preparation (preparation = AR-C §70).

### 940 — Draft review report
Per AR-C §90.A99–.A116. Identifies:
- Title.
- Addressee.
- Introductory paragraph identifying the entity and the F/S
  reviewed.
- Management's responsibility.
- Practitioner's responsibility (review under SSARS, limited
  assurance).
- Practitioner's conclusion.
- Signature, city/state, and date (no earlier than the date the
  practitioner has obtained sufficient appropriate review
  evidence).

Modifications:
- GAAP departure → modified conclusion ("except for") under AR-C
  §90.62.
- Scope limitation → cannot complete review; withdraw or modify.
- Going-concern uncertainty → emphasis-of-matter or modified
  conclusion as warranted.

### 960 — Open-items / points-forward
Items for next year's engagement.

### 970 — Engagement file assembly
Per AR-C §60.A29: documentation must be sufficient to enable an
experienced practitioner without prior connection to the
engagement to understand the procedures performed, the results,
and the significant findings or issues that arose. Retention per
firm policy and applicable state-board rules (typically aligned
with AU-C §230.16 floor of 5 years from report release date,
though SSARS does not impose the 5-year floor explicitly — firm
policy and state-board rules govern).

## Workpaper-review pattern

Per AR-C §60.31. Three sign-off pattern:
- Preparer (staff or senior).
- Reviewer (manager or in-charge).
- Engagement partner.

SQMS 2 engagement-quality-review applies to firm-specified
engagements; review engagements typically do NOT trigger SQMS 2
unless the firm's QM policies require independent review for
public-interest entities with reviewed F/S.

## What a review file is NOT

Per AR-C §90.06: a review does NOT contemplate:
- Tests of accounting records via inspection, observation, or
  confirmation (those are audit procedures).
- Obtaining an understanding of internal control or assessing
  control risk.
- Assessing fraud risk per AU-C §240.
- Designing further audit procedures responsive to assessed risk
  (the AU-C §330 framework).

If the engagement requires those procedures, it has scope-creeped
into an audit and the engagement type and report wording must be
elevated. Document any scope expansion in the review-procedure
plan and update the engagement letter before continuing.

## Documentation sufficiency reminders

Per AR-C §60.A26 (analogous to AU-C §230.08): an experienced
practitioner without prior connection to the engagement must
understand:
- The nature, timing, and extent of inquiries and analytical
  procedures performed.
- The results of inquiries and analytical procedures.
- Significant findings or issues, the conclusions reached, and
  the significant professional judgments made.

Inquiry workpapers without a "who / what / when / response /
evaluation" structure are insufficient. Analytical procedures
without expectation development, comparison, and follow-up are
insufficient.
