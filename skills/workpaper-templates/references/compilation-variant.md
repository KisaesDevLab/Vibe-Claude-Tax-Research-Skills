# Compilation / Preparation workpaper variant (AR-C §70 / §80)

A SSARS preparation engagement (AR-C §70) provides **no
assurance** and produces no report — only the financial
statements with a "no assurance is provided" legend on each page.
A SSARS compilation engagement (AR-C §80) provides **no
assurance** but produces a compilation report.

Both engagements have a substantially compressed workpaper file
relative to a review or audit. The variant scaffold focuses on:
- Engagement letter (mandatory for both).
- Documentation that the F/S were prepared in accordance with the
  applicable framework.
- Independence determination (compilations may be performed
  without independence — with disclosure; preparations don't
  require an independence determination at all under AR-C §70.04).
- Going-concern awareness (compilation only — AR-C §80.A24).

## Required planning workpapers

### 110 — Engagement letter
Per AR-C §60.A21 + §70.10 (preparation) or §80.10 (compilation).
Signed before fieldwork begins. The engagement letter for a
preparation must clearly state that the engagement is a
preparation under AR-C §70 (no report, no assurance, "no
assurance" legend on each F/S page). The compilation engagement
letter must clearly state that the engagement is a compilation
under AR-C §80 (no assurance, compilation report issued).

### 120 — Independence determination (compilation only)
Per AICPA Code §1.200 + AR-C §80.27. The practitioner must
determine independence; if NOT independent, a lack-of-independence
disclosure is required in the compilation report (AR-C §80.27
illustrative report 4). Document the independence determination
and any threats considered.

### 130 — Engagement-acceptance memo
Per AR-C §60. Documents the firm's acceptance of the engagement,
the firm's competence, and any known conditions that would affect
the engagement.

### 140 — Framework determination
Document the financial-reporting framework: GAAP, tax basis, cash
basis, contractual basis, regulatory basis, or other special-
purpose framework. Note any departures the practitioner is aware
of (compilation only — practitioner is required to address known
departures per AR-C §80.A18).

## Required substantive workpapers

A preparation / compilation file is NOT an audit file. The
practitioner does NOT perform tests of details, observations,
confirmations, or analytical procedures designed to obtain
audit evidence. The substantive workpaper set focuses on:

### Trial-balance reconciliation
- TB tied to the F/S.
- Adjusting journal entries (AJEs) prepared by the practitioner
  on behalf of the client (preparation) or proposed to the client
  (compilation).
- Each AJE supported by a brief workpaper (rationale + amount).

### Account-classification review
- Review of TB account classifications for consistency with the
  applicable framework (e.g., ASC 842 ROU asset and lease
  liability properly classified for a GAAP-framework F/S; cash
  vs. accrual vs. tax basis F/S consistency).

### F/S preparation
- F/S drafted by the practitioner (preparation) or prepared by
  management with practitioner assistance (compilation).
- Footnote disclosures appropriate to the framework. (For
  compilation, the omission of substantially all disclosures is
  permitted under AR-C §80.A19 if the engagement letter and the
  compilation report disclose the omission.)

### Going-concern awareness (compilation only)
Per AR-C §80.A24. If the practitioner becomes aware of conditions
or events that raise substantial doubt about the entity's ability
to continue as a going concern, and management has not addressed
them in the F/S, the practitioner should request management to
consider the matter. If management refuses, the practitioner
should withdraw or modify the compilation report.

## Required wrap-up workpapers

### 900 — Subsequent-events awareness
The practitioner is not required to perform subsequent-events
procedures in a compilation or preparation. However, if the
practitioner becomes aware of an event that should be reflected
in the F/S, the practitioner must address it (AR-C §80.A15).

### 930 — Draft F/S
The F/S as prepared. For preparation, "no assurance is provided"
legend on each page (AR-C §70.14). For compilation, the F/S
accompanied by the compilation report.

### 940 — Compilation report (compilation only)
Per AR-C §80.A8 (independent practitioner) or §80.27 (lack-of-
independence variant). Identifies the practitioner did not audit
or review and that no opinion or assurance is provided.

For preparation, NO report is issued. The "no assurance is
provided" legend per AR-C §70.14 appears on each page of the F/S
or is presented prominently with the F/S.

### 960 — Open-items / points-forward
Items for next year's engagement.

### 970 — Engagement file assembly
Per AR-C §60.A29: documentation must be sufficient to enable an
experienced practitioner without prior connection to the
engagement to understand the procedures performed and the
significant findings or issues. Retention per firm policy and
state-board rules.

## Workpaper-review pattern

Per AR-C §60.31. For preparation and compilation engagements,
firm methodology often uses a two-sign-off pattern (preparer +
reviewer) given the compressed scope, with engagement partner
review limited to the engagement letter, the F/S draft, and the
compilation report (compilation only). The three-sign-off pattern
remains best practice for any engagement with material risk or
unusual features.

## What a compilation / preparation file is NOT

Per AR-C §70.05 / §80.04: these engagements do NOT contemplate:
- Tests of accounting records.
- Obtaining an understanding of internal control.
- Assessing fraud risk.
- Inquiry or analytical procedures designed to obtain assurance
  (those are review procedures — AR-C §90).

If the engagement requires any of those procedures, the engagement
type has scope-creeped to a review or audit and must be elevated.
Document any scope expansion in the engagement memo and update the
engagement letter before continuing.

## Documentation sufficiency reminders

Per AR-C §60.A26: an experienced practitioner without prior
connection to the engagement must understand:
- The nature of the engagement (preparation vs. compilation).
- The nature, timing, and extent of procedures performed (in a
  compilation / preparation, these are limited to F/S
  preparation, AJE proposal/recording, and any departures or
  going-concern awareness items addressed).
- Significant findings or issues, the conclusions reached, and
  the significant professional judgments made.

For compilation, document the lack-of-independence treatment if
applicable. For preparation, document the framework and any
departures (although AR-C §70 does not require addressing known
departures in the same way as compilation).

## Cross-engagement notes

- A preparation engagement that is *coupled with* a return-
  preparation engagement (preparation of F/S used to prepare a tax
  return) is best documented in the tax engagement file unless
  the F/S preparation is a separately scoped service.
- A compilation that is *combined with* bookkeeping services is
  permitted, but the practitioner must distinguish between the
  bookkeeping work product (no SSARS engagement) and the
  compilation work product (under SSARS). The engagement letter
  must clearly delineate.
