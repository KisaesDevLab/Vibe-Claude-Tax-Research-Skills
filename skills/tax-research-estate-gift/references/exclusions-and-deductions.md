# Estate / gift / GST exclusions and deductions

For tax year 2024-2025 — verify year-specific Rev. Proc. before
quoting figures. OBBBA may have modified TCJA-era amounts.

## §2010 Unified Credit / Exclusion

| Year | Basic exclusion |
|---|---|
| 2017 | $5.49M |
| 2018 | $11.18M (TCJA-doubled) |
| 2019 | $11.40M |
| 2020 | $11.58M |
| 2021 | $11.70M |
| 2022 | $12.06M |
| 2023 | $12.92M |
| 2024 | $13.61M |
| 2025 | $13.99M |
| 2026+ | Reverts to ~$7M (inflation-adjusted) absent extension; OBBBA may have modified |

§2010(c)(2)(A) — basic exclusion AS APPLIED to taxable estate
plus adjusted taxable gifts.

§2010(c)(2)(B) — DSUE (Deceased Spouse Unused Exclusion):
unused exclusion of predeceased spouse, available to surviving
spouse if portability elected on predeceased's Form 706.

§2010(c)(4) — portability mechanics. Filed on Form 706 within 9
months (or with §6075 extensions). Late-portability relief:
Rev. Proc. 2022-32 grants automatic 5-year extension for estates
not required to file Form 706 (under-threshold estates).

Treas. Reg. §20.2010-1(c) — anti-clawback rule. Gifts made when
the higher exclusion was in effect remain protected even if the
exclusion later reverts. Specifically: "the credit allowable in
computing the estate tax for an estate of a decedent dying after
December 31, 2025 ... is determined using the basic exclusion
amount in effect on the date of the decedent's death OR the
basic exclusion amount applicable to gifts made by the decedent,
WHICHEVER IS GREATER."

The anti-clawback rule eliminates the "use it or lose it" pressure
on pre-sunset gifting EXCEPT for the spread between gift and
estate exclusion in subsequent years.

## §2503(b) Annual Exclusion

| Year | Amount |
|---|---|
| 2018 | $15,000 |
| 2019 | $15,000 |
| 2020 | $15,000 |
| 2021 | $15,000 |
| 2022 | $16,000 |
| 2023 | $17,000 |
| 2024 | $18,000 |
| 2025 | $19,000 |

§2503(b)(2) present-interest requirement — gift in trust does
NOT qualify unless the donee has a present right to enjoy the
property. Crummey withdrawal power (typically 30 days) used in
ILITs and other trusts.

§2503(c) Minor's Trust — special rules for trusts for the
benefit of a minor:
- Property and income may be expended for the minor before age
  21.
- Any unspent property and income passes to the minor at age 21.
- Treated as present-interest gift for §2503(b) purposes.

§2503(e) — direct payment for tuition or medical care; unlimited
exclusion. Payment must go directly to the educational
institution or medical provider; reimbursement to the donee
counts as gift.

§529(c)(2)(B) — 5-year-front-load for §529 contributions:
contribute up to 5 × annual exclusion in one year ($95,000 single
/ $190,000 MFJ 2025; verify year-specific) and elect 5-year
spread for §2503 purposes.

## §2055 Charitable Deduction (estate)

§2055(a) — unlimited charitable deduction for transfers to:
- §170(c)(1) governmental units.
- §170(c)(2) §501(c)(3) organizations.
- §170(c)(3) veterans' organizations.
- §170(c)(4) fraternal societies.
- §170(c)(5) cemetery companies.

Split-interest trusts:
- §2055(e)(2) generally disallows for split-interest unless
  qualifying CRT / CLT.

## §2522 Charitable Deduction (gift)

§2522(a) parallels §2055(a). Includes lifetime gifts to qualifying
organizations.

§2522(c)(2) split-interest trust requirements parallel §2055(e)
estate requirements.

## §2056 Marital Deduction

### §2056(a) Outright bequest to U.S. citizen spouse

Unlimited marital deduction for property passing outright to a
U.S. citizen surviving spouse.

### §2056(b)(7) QTIP — Qualified Terminable Interest Property

Marital deduction for property in which surviving spouse has
qualifying income interest for life:
- All income payable to spouse at least annually.
- No power to appoint property during spouse's life to anyone
  other than spouse.
- QTIP election made by executor on Form 706.

QTIP property included in surviving spouse's estate at death
under §2044.

Reverse-QTIP election (§2652(a)(3)) — for GST purposes, treat
the predeceased spouse as the transferor of QTIP property.

### §2056(d) QDOT — Qualified Domestic Trust

Required for marital deduction to a non-U.S.-citizen surviving
spouse:
- At least one trustee must be U.S. citizen or domestic
  corporation.
- No distribution of corpus without estate-tax withholding (with
  exceptions for hardship).
- Property remaining in QDOT at death of surviving spouse is
  subject to estate tax as if part of the original decedent's
  estate.

§2056(d) override of the "not by reason of property passing to a
non-citizen spouse" rule.

§2056A — additional QDOT rules.

## §2032 Alternate Valuation

Election available to value gross estate as of:
- 6 months after date of death; OR
- Date of distribution / sale / disposition (if earlier than 6
  months).

Conditions:
- Gross estate value at alternate date < value at date of death;
  AND
- Estate tax due at alternate date < estate tax due at date of
  death.

Election irrevocable, made on Form 706 §2032(d).

§2032 election common during market downturns; can save
substantial tax for asset-heavy estates.

## §6166 Installment Payment

For estates with > 35% closely-held-business interests:
- 14-year deferral period (5 years interest-only + 10 years
  principal-and-interest).
- Special low-interest rate on the first $1.84M of tax (2024 —
  verify year-specific) attributable to the closely-held-business
  interest: 2%.
- Standard underpayment rate on excess.
- Acceleration on disposition of business interest or default in
  payment.

§6166 election made on Form 706.

## §2032A Special-Use Valuation

Qualified family farm / closely-held-business reduction: up to
$1.39M (2024 — verify year-specific) reduction from FMV.

Conditions:
- Decedent / family ownership and material-participation
  requirements.
- Heir continued-use requirement (10-year recapture).

§2032A(c) recapture if property sold or use changed within 10
years.

## §2511 Gifts

§2511(a) — gift by a non-resident alien of intangible property
not in U.S. — generally not subject to gift tax.

§2511(b) — community property gifts.

## §6075 / §6019 Filing Requirements

§6075(a) — Form 706 due 9 months after date of death; §6075(b)(2)
9-month gift-tax-return deadline applies in same year as Form
709 calendar-year due date (April 15).

§6075(c) — 6-month extension via Form 4768.

§6019(a)(1) — Form 709 required if gifts to a single donee
exceed annual exclusion in a year, OR if a present-interest
question requires reporting (e.g., Crummey gifts in a trust may
trigger filing even below annual-exclusion-per-donee thresholds).
