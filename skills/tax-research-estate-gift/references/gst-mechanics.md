# Generation-Skipping Transfer Tax (GST) mechanics

## §2611 — taxable events

GST applies to three types of transfers to a "skip person":

### Direct skip (§2612(c))

Transfer to a skip person — typically a grandchild, great-
grandchild, or unrelated person more than 37.5 years younger than
the transferor — that is also subject to gift or estate tax.

Direct skip examples:
- Outright gift to grandchild.
- Bequest to grandchild in will.
- Gift in trust where all interests are held by skip persons.

### Taxable termination (§2612(a))

Termination of an interest in property held in trust where, after
the termination, only skip persons hold the interests.

Example: Trust pays income to son for life, then to grandchildren
upon son's death — son's death triggers a taxable termination.

### Taxable distribution (§2612(b))

Distribution from a trust to a skip person that is not a direct
skip or taxable termination.

Example: Discretionary trust distribution to grandchild while
parent still alive (not a termination).

## §2613 — skip person

A "skip person" is:
- A natural person who is two or more generations below the
  transferor (typically grandchild or younger);
- An unrelated natural person more than 37.5 years younger than
  the transferor; OR
- A trust if all interests are held by skip persons.

§2651 — generation assignment rules. §2651(e) "predeceased-parent
exception" — skip-person status may not apply if the transferor's
deceased child is the parent of the would-be skip person.

## §2641 — GST tax rate

40% (2018+) — matches the highest §2001 / §2502 rate.

## §2632 — GST exemption

§2010 / §2631 exemption: $13.99M for 2025 (verify year-specific;
identical to estate basic exclusion).

§2632(a)(1) — automatic allocation of GST exemption:
- To direct skip transfers automatically.
- To indirect skips (post-2001 EGTRRA — see §2632(c)).

§2632(b) — election OUT of automatic allocation.
§2632(c) — election IN to automatic allocation for indirect
skips.

Once allocated, GST exemption establishes "inclusion ratio" for
the trust:
- Inclusion ratio of 0 = no GST tax (fully exempt).
- Inclusion ratio of 1 = full GST tax (40%).
- Fractional ratio for partial allocation.

§2642 — applicable rate; "inclusion ratio" computation.

### Late allocation

§2642(b)(1) — late allocation possible by:
- Election on Form 709 for the year of allocation.
- Effective on the date specified in the election (typically the
  date of allocation, NOT the date of the original transfer).
- Late allocation captures appreciation since original transfer
  (disadvantageous compared to timely allocation).

### Reformation (§2632(d))

Limited circumstances allow reformation under Treas. Reg.
§26.2632-1(b)(1).

## QTIP and reverse-QTIP

Reverse-QTIP election (§2652(a)(3)) — for QTIP property,
treat the predeceased spouse as the transferor for GST purposes
(rather than the surviving spouse). Reverse-QTIP captures the
predeceased spouse's GST exemption.

Without reverse-QTIP, the QTIP property is included in the
surviving spouse's estate (§2044) and the surviving spouse is
the transferor for GST purposes. With reverse-QTIP, the
predeceased spouse's GST exemption can shield the QTIP property.

Common practice: bypass trust funded with predeceased spouse's
basic exclusion is exempt; QTIP trust funded with marital-
deduction property has reverse-QTIP election to use predeceased
spouse's GST exemption.

## §2702 — GRAT and QPRT mechanics

§2702 special-valuation rules for transfers to family members
where transferor retains an interest.

Two pathways:
1. §2702(b) "qualified interest" — annuity, unitrust, or
   non-contingent remainder. Allows favorable valuation.
2. Default valuation = zero; entire transfer treated as gift.

GRAT (Grantor Retained Annuity Trust):
- §2702(b) qualified annuity interest.
- Annuity payments to grantor for term; remainder to family.
- "Zeroed-out GRAT" — set annuity equal to PV of contribution
  using §7520 rate; results in near-zero gift.
- Walton v. Commissioner, 115 T.C. 589 (2000) — "zeroed-out"
  approved.
- Risk: grantor must survive term; otherwise full inclusion
  under §2036(a)(1).

QPRT (Qualified Personal Residence Trust):
- §2702(a)(3)(A) carve-out from §2702 valuation rules.
- Transfer of personal residence with retained right of
  occupancy for term.
- Gift = remainder value computed using §7520 rate.
- Risk: grantor must survive term; otherwise full inclusion.

## §2511 — gift completion

Transfer must be a "completed gift" — transferor parts with
dominion and control. §2511(c) and Treas. Reg. §25.2511-2.

Incomplete gifts are not subject to gift tax but are includible
in the gross estate at death.

## §6166 GST treatment

§6166 installment payment available for GST tax under §6166(i)
in limited circumstances.

## Common practitioner errors

- Assuming GST exemption is automatically allocated to all
  trusts (it is for direct skips and post-2001 indirect skips,
  but the automatic-allocation election out under §2632(b)
  may have been made).
- Failing to make timely §2632(c) elections IN for indirect
  skips.
- Missing the reverse-QTIP election for marital-deduction
  property.
- Late allocation that captures appreciation.
- Counting the predeceased-parent exception incorrectly.
- Using the §7520 rate from the wrong month for GRAT / QPRT
  valuation.
