# Trust Fund Recovery Penalty (§6672) and collection

## §7501 — trust-fund concept

§7501 designates withheld income tax and the employee's portion of
FICA as a trust fund held by the employer for the United States.
The employer is the trustee.

Trust-fund items:
- Federal income-tax withheld from wages (§3402);
- Employee's 6.2% OASDI (§3101(a));
- Employee's 1.45% Medicare (§3101(b));
- Employee's 0.9% additional Medicare (§3101(b)(2));
- Backup withholding (§3406).

Employer's share of FICA, FUTA, and self-paid taxes are NOT trust-
fund items. They are the employer's own liability.

## §6672 — Trust Fund Recovery Penalty

§6672(a): "Any person required to collect, truthfully account
for, and pay over any tax imposed by this title who willfully
fails to collect such tax, or truthfully account for and pay
over such tax, or willfully attempts in any manner to evade or
defeat any such tax or the payment thereof, shall, in addition
to other penalties provided by law, be liable to a penalty equal
to the total amount of the tax evaded, or not collected, or not
accounted for and paid over."

100% penalty — equal to the trust-fund liability. Joint and
several among multiple responsible persons.

### Responsible person

Treas. Reg. §301.6672-1: a person who has the authority and
responsibility to collect, account for, and pay over the trust-
fund tax. Factors:
- Hold corporate office (e.g., president, treasurer, controller).
- Sign tax returns.
- Authorize payments / draw checks.
- Hire / fire employees.
- Make management decisions.

Outside professionals (CPA, attorney) are generally NOT
responsible persons absent unusual control. But a CPA who controls
the bank account or signs checks may be.

### Willfulness

Pomponio v. United States, 429 U.S. 10 (1976) (per curiam):
willfulness = voluntary, conscious, intentional decision to use
trust-fund money for other corporate purposes. NOT a fraud
standard.

Slodov v. United States, 436 U.S. 238 (1978): later acquired
unencumbered funds must be applied to delinquent trust-fund
liability; willful failure to do so triggers §6672.

### Cash-flow defense

NOT a defense. The argument "we needed the money to keep
operating" specifically establishes willfulness.

### Reasonable cause

§6672 has NO reasonable-cause defense. Reliance on a bookkeeper
or CFO has been rejected.

## Form 4180 interview

The IRS revenue officer interviews each potentially responsible
person on Form 4180:
- Authority over financial decisions.
- Check-signing authority.
- Knowledge of unpaid trust-fund liability.
- Decisions on creditor priority.

Practitioners should:
1. Engage the responsible-person before the Form 4180 interview.
2. Review prior-year payroll filings, board minutes, bank
   signature authorities.
3. Distinguish authority from actual exercise.
4. Consider §530 / classification challenges if relevant.

## §6672 procedure

1. Letter 1153 — proposed assessment.
2. 60-day window for protest to Appeals.
3. Form 4180 interview (typically before Letter 1153).
4. Assessment.
5. CDP (Collection Due Process) hearing rights under §6320 / §6330.
6. Tax Court review of CDP determination.

§6203 assessment: separate assessment for each responsible
person.

## Joint-and-several with right of contribution

§6672(d) — IRS may abate against a responsible person if another
responsible person has paid. §6672(e) — right of contribution
among responsible persons.

State courts adjudicate the right-of-contribution claim. Federal
court has jurisdiction over the underlying §6672 liability.

## §6020(b) substitute return

If employer fails to file Form 941 / 940, IRS may prepare
substitute return under §6020(b). Substitute returns generally
maximize the assessment by:
- Estimating wages from prior periods or from W-2 / 1099 data.
- Disallowing deductions / credits.
- Triggering FTF / FTP penalties.

Recommend filing original returns to displace §6020(b) substitute
returns where possible.

## In-business trust-fund express agreement (IBTF Express)

Streamlined installment agreement available for taxpayers owing
$25,000 or less in business trust-fund tax (verify current
threshold). Avoids personal financial-disclosure requirements.

## Voluntary classification settlement program (VCSP)

Rev. Proc. 2013-22 — VCSP provides reduced employment-tax
liability (10% of one year's employment tax) for employers who
voluntarily reclassify workers from contractor to employee
prospectively.

VCSP NOT available if:
- Employer is currently under audit or in a worker-classification
  examination.
- Employer is contesting a prior worker-classification
  determination.
- Employer has not filed all required Forms 1099 for the workers
  to be reclassified.

## §3509 reduced rates for misclassification

§3509 — when an employer is held liable for employment tax due
to worker reclassification, the employer's liability is reduced
to:
- 1.5% of wages for FIT (instead of full withholding).
- 20% of the employee's FICA (instead of full).
- Full employer FICA and FUTA still apply.

§3509(c) — reduced rates UNAVAILABLE if the employer's failure
was intentional disregard.

## §530 safe harbor — federal employment tax only

§530 of the Revenue Act of 1978 (uncodified) bars federal
employment-tax reclassification by the IRS if the taxpayer:
1. Reasonably relied on (a) judicial precedent, (b) past audit,
   (c) industry practice, or (d) other reasonable basis;
2. Filed all required Forms 1099 consistent with treatment as a
   contractor; AND
3. Treated all similarly-situated workers as contractors.

Route worker-classification questions to
`predict-worker-classification` for full §530 + ABC-test analysis.

## Common audit-defense items

- [ ] Identification of all potentially responsible persons.
- [ ] Bank-account signatory records.
- [ ] Board minutes evidencing financial-control structure.
- [ ] Prior 941 / 940 / W-2 / 1099 filings.
- [ ] Communications with bookkeeping / payroll service.
- [ ] Cash-flow records to assess Slodov "later-acquired funds"
  exposure.
- [ ] Form 4180 prep with each interviewee.
- [ ] CDP rights preserved if Letter 1153 issued.
